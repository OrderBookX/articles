# Bitunix Futures Grid Long vs Short — How Funding Rates Reveal Market Bias

Funding rates are the most underused data point in grid direction selection. On Bitunix, funding displays in real time — and understanding what it means gives you a directional edge most grid traders ignore.

### What Funding Rates Are

Perpetual futures on Bitunix use funding to keep contract price aligned with spot. Every 8 hours, one side pays the other. Positive funding: longs pay shorts. Negative funding: shorts pay longs. The rate reflects net market positioning.

## Reading Funding for Direction

Persistently positive funding on Bitunix means the market is crowded long. This supports deploying a long grid (you align with consensus) but comes with a holding cost. Alternatively, it supports a short grid (you earn funding payments) but means you are trading against the crowd.

### The Contrarian Signal

When funding is extremely positive (above 0.05%), the market may be overleveraged long. Mean reversion becomes more likely. A short grid during extreme positive funding can be profitable both from grid cycles and from funding income. Bitunix displays this data clearly — use it.

## Funding Cost Impact on Grid Profitability

A long grid at 5x leverage during 0.03% positive funding pays approximately 0.1% daily on total exposure. Over a 14-day grid run, that is 1.4% in cumulative funding cost. If your grid earns 5% total return over that period, funding consumed 28% of your profit. Significant.

### Direction-Funding Alignment Strategy

When Bitunix funding is positive: short grids earn funding. When negative: long grids earn funding. Align direction with favorable funding when chart structure supports it — you add an income stream to grid profit instead of a cost.
