## The Hunt Brothers Crisis: What Happened and Why It Matters

The Hunt Brothers accumulated silver futures contracts representing hundreds of millions of ounces of silver, funded partly by margin loans. When commodity exchanges changed margin requirements specifically to limit the Hunt Brothers' ability to continue accumulating, the cost of maintaining their leveraged position became unsustainable. The forced liquidation created a feedback loop where selling drove prices lower, which required additional margin calls, which required additional selling.
