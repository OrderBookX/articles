## The Annual Performance Report Structure

Section 1   Year in Review (Two Pages):

Total return for the year, measured as: (ending account value - starting account value + withdrawals) / starting account value. Compare to relevant benchmarks: gold spot return, copper spot return, S&P 500 return.

Monthly performance chart: bar chart showing monthly P&L as percentage of account equity. Identifies seasonal patterns and difficult periods.

Maximum drawdown during the year: peak-to-trough account value decline. Was it within the defined 15% annual maximum?

Section 2   Instrument-Level Attribution (One Page):

For each instrument actively traded during the year: number of trades, win rate, average gain on winning trades, average loss on losing trades, Sharpe ratio, contribution to total annual return. The highest-alpha instruments deserve more capital next year; the lowest-alpha deserve less.

Section 3   Analytical Accuracy Assessment (One Page):

For each major thesis held during the year: was the primary driver prediction correct? What was the actual driver move versus the expected move? The primary driver accuracy rate is the most important analytical quality metric.
