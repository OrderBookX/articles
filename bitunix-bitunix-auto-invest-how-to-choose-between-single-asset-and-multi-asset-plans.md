## The Case for Single-Asset Plans

If your thesis is centered on one asset, typically Bitcoin, a single-asset plan offers simplicity and maximum concentration. Every dollar goes toward building your position in one place. There is no allocation math to worry about, no rebalancing decisions to make, and no temptation to chase the latest trending altcoin. Bitcoin-only DCA has historically produced strong results across complete market cycles. For investors who believe Bitcoin will continue its long-term appreciation trajectory, concentrating their recurring investment makes strategic sense. You are betting on one thesis with full conviction.

## The Case for Multi-Asset Plans

If you want broader exposure to the crypto ecosystem, a multi-asset plan spreads your recurring investment across several tokens. This reduces the impact of any single asset underperforming and captures growth from different sectors. A multi-asset approach requires more upfront research. You need to justify each inclusion and assign appropriate percentage allocations. But once configured, the plan runs with the same automation as a single-asset plan. The system handles the distribution automatically.

## A Decision Framework

Ask yourself three questions. First, do you have strong conviction in one specific asset? If yes, a single-asset plan may be appropriate. Second, do you want exposure to smart contracts, DeFi, or other sectors beyond Bitcoin? If yes, consider multi-asset. Third, how much time are you willing to spend on research and quarterly reviews? Multi-asset plans require more ongoing evaluation because each included asset needs periodic reassessment.

## Hybrid Strategy

You can run multiple auto invest plans simultaneously on Bitunix. Some investors maintain a Bitcoin-only plan as their core and a separate multi-asset plan as their satellite allocation. This separates the high-conviction base from the exploratory diversification layer, making each plan easier to evaluate independently.

## Avoiding Analysis Paralysis

The biggest risk in this decision is not making the wrong choice. It is not making any choice at all. A single-asset Bitcoin plan started today will outperform a perfectly diversified plan started six months from now while you were still deciding allocations.
