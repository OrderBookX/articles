## The Baltic Dry Index and COPPERUSDT

The Baltic Dry Index measures the cost of shipping bulk commodities including iron ore, coal, grain, and some copper concentrates. When the BDI rises sharply over 2-3 consecutive weeks, bulk commodity shipments are increasing   charterers are actively booking vessels, implying physical trade is accelerating.

The BDI-COPPERUSDT connection: copper concentrate is shipped from mines to smelters in bulk carriers. When BDI sub-indices for Capesize vessels (the largest bulk carriers, primarily used for iron ore and copper concentrate from South America and Australia to China) are rising specifically, the China-bound raw material shipments are accelerating.
