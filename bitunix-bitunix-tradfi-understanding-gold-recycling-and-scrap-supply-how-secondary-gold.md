## The Price Elasticity of Gold Recycling

Gold recycling is highly price-elastic: when gold prices rise significantly, more holders decide to sell their gold items for recycling. Jewelry owners sell pieces that are no longer worn. Industrial users optimize their scrap recovery processes. Gold buyers (pawn shops, cash-for-gold operations) experience increased volumes from customers motivated by higher prices.

The empirical data: annual gold recycling volume was approximately 1,200 tonnes in 2019 (when gold averaged $1,400/oz) and rose to approximately 1,550 tonnes in 2020 (when gold reached $2,069/oz for the first time)   a 29% volume increase in response to the 48% price increase. This recycling surge provided counter-cyclical supply that partially offset the investment demand driving prices higher.
