## TradFi Liquidity Characteristics

Traditional financial markets like the New York Stock Exchange and COMEX have deep liquidity during operating hours. Market makers provide continuous quotes, and institutional order flow creates thick order books. However, liquidity drops dramatically outside trading hours, during holidays, and in after-hours sessions. Weekend gaps between Friday close and Monday open can create significant price risk for leveraged positions. These structural limitations have been accepted in TradFi for decades.

## DeFi Liquidity Challenges

Decentralized exchanges use automated market maker models that provide liquidity through pooled capital rather than order books. While innovative, this approach creates problems for large orders: significant price impact, impermanent loss for liquidity providers, and vulnerability to MEV extraction. DeFi liquidity is also fragmented across hundreds of protocols and chains, making it difficult to execute large positions efficiently.
