## Why the Trade Was Wrong

The mistake was not reading PMI wrong   PMI had indeed recovered. The mistake was trading a narrative without understanding which part of the Chinese economy had actually recovered. When I went back to analyze why copper had not responded to the PMI recovery, the answer was in the composition: the PMI recovery was almost entirely in the services sector (restaurants, tourism, consumption) reopening after a COVID wave. The manufacturing and construction components of PMI   the copper-consuming sectors   had barely moved.

I had seen "PMI above 50" and assumed it meant copper demand was recovering. I had not broken PMI into its components to determine whether the recovery was in copper-relevant or copper-irrelevant sectors.
