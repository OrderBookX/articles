## Water Scarcity and Chilean Copper Production

Chile's Atacama Desert, which hosts the world's largest copper mines (Escondida, Collahuasi, Los Pelambres), receives minimal rainfall. Mine operations require large volumes of water for ore processing and dust suppression. As climate change reduces water availability in the Atacama, copper producers face increasing water constraints.

Escondida BHP has invested over $3 billion in desalination plants to reduce freshwater dependence. But the transition to desalinated water increases energy costs, which combined with ore grade decline, has contributed significantly to rising AISC.
