## Gold as the Zero-Coupon Perpetual Currency

Fiat currencies pay "interest" through the risk-free rate   holding dollars in a savings account earns the fed funds rate. Gold pays zero   it is a zero-coupon monetary asset. The relative return of gold versus the dollar is therefore entirely determined by the real interest rate: when real rates are negative (the dollar loses purchasing power faster than gold's zero return), gold is the superior monetary asset. When real rates are positive (the dollar earns more in real terms than gold's zero), the dollar is the superior monetary asset.

This framing produces an important prediction: in the very long run, gold's price should appreciate approximately in line with the cumulative inflation minus the cumulative real interest earned on dollar savings. Decades of zero or negative real rates make gold's long-run appreciation versus the dollar structurally expected.
