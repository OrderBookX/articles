## Real Interest Rates as the Second XAUUSDT Factor

Real interest rates, measured by the 10-year TIPS yield, represent the opportunity cost of holding gold versus inflation-protected government bonds. When real rates are negative or declining, gold becomes more attractive relative to bonds, driving investment demand. When real rates are rising toward positive territory, gold's relative attractiveness decreases.

The TIPS yield provides a real-time market-based measure of real rate expectations, updating continuously as the bond market prices in changing inflation and growth expectations. For XAUUSDT positioning, tracking the TIPS yield trend direction alongside DXY provides the two-factor picture that is superior to either factor alone.
