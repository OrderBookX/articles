## The CFO's Production Hedging Decision and Its Near-Term XAUUSDT Impact

A gold mining company CFO faces a fundamental choice when gold prices are high: sell future production forward at current high prices (hedging), or leave production unhedged and bet on gold prices remaining elevated. The hedging decision has a specific immediate effect on XAUUSDT: when mining companies aggressively forward-sell production (creating a "hedge book"), they are adding supply to the futures market that caps near-term price appreciation.

The gold mining industry learned a painful lesson from the late 1990s when aggressive hedging at $350-$400 created a massive short position that had to be covered at the 2005-2011 bull market prices   destroying shareholder value as companies bought back their forward sales at much higher prices. After this experience, the industry shifted toward minimal hedging, meaning that today's gold mining companies typically hedge less than 20% of annual production.

This structural change in industry hedging behavior is a permanent XAUUSDT tailwind: unlike the 1990s when producer hedging could cap price rallies, today's minimal hedge books mean mining company selling pressure during gold bull markets is much lower.
