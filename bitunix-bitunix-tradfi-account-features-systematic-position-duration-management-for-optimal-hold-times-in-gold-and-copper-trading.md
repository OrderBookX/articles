## The Time Stop as a Second Risk Management Layer

Time stops complement price stops in duration management. A position that has been held for twice its expected duration without achieving meaningful progress toward the target is a position where the expected move is not occurring on the anticipated schedule. Whether to hold or exit requires a fresh analytical assessment: has the thesis been invalidated (exit) or is it just taking longer than expected (hold if thesis still valid)?

The time stop forces this fresh assessment rather than allowing default holding "until something changes."
