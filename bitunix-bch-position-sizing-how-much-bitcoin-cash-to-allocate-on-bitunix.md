## Starting With Your Risk Budget

Every portfolio has a risk budget, whether you define it explicitly or not. Your risk budget is the maximum percentage drawdown you can tolerate across your entire portfolio without making emotional decisions that compound the damage.

For most investors, a total portfolio drawdown of 20 to 30 percent is the psychological breaking point where fear overrides strategy. If your crypto allocation can contribute to that drawdown, your BCH position sizing needs to account for worst-case Bitcoin Cash performance within acceptable total portfolio impact.

If you allocate 15 percent of your portfolio to Bitcoin Cash crypto and the token experiences a 70 percent drawdown in a severe correction, your total portfolio impact is approximately 10.5 percent. For most risk profiles, this is painful but survivable and does not trigger capitulation.

## Conviction-Based Sizing Within Your Risk Framework

Within your risk budget, allocation size should scale with conviction. Higher conviction in Bitcoin Cash fundamentals, backed by research rather than hope, justifies larger allocation. Lower conviction warrants smaller positions that still provide exposure without excessive risk.

A practical tiering approach: 5 to 10 percent of crypto allocation for tokens you are researching but not yet fully convinced about. 10 to 20 percent for tokens with strong fundamentals and clear catalysts like BCH. Maximum 25 percent for highest-conviction positions in established large-cap assets.
