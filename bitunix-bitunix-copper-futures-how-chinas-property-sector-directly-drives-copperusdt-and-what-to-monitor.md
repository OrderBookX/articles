## Why Property Construction Uses So Much Copper

A typical residential building in China requires approximately 70-90 kilograms of copper per apartment unit in electrical wiring, plumbing, air conditioning systems, and elevator motors. At peak construction levels, China was building millions of new apartment units annually, making property construction a dominant driver of copper demand. The property sector, combined with related infrastructure development, represented approximately 25-30% of Chinese copper demand at its peak.

The ongoing slowdown in China's property sector, driven by debt restructuring at major developers including Evergrande and Country Garden, has reduced this demand component significantly. Understanding where in the property cycle China is at any given time provides crucial context for COPPERUSDT medium-term direction.

## Leading Indicators for China Property Copper Demand

Real estate sales volume: Monthly new home sales data from China's National Bureau of Statistics (released monthly with a one-month lag) shows whether the property transaction market is recovering or continuing to contract. Rising sales volume eventually translates to increased construction activity and copper demand, typically with a 6-12 month lag.

Housing starts data: The NBS also publishes monthly housing starts data. New construction starts directly translate to near-term copper demand as building infrastructure is installed. Rising starts are a more direct copper demand signal than sales volume.

Infrastructure investment as a substitute: When property demand falls, the Chinese government typically offsets it with increased infrastructure investment (roads, railways, power grids). This infrastructure substitution also uses copper, partially offsetting property sector copper demand weakness. Monitoring central government infrastructure investment announcements alongside property sector data provides the net copper demand picture.
