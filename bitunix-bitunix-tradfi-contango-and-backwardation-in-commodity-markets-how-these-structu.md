## Contango: When the Curve Says the Market Is Comfortable

When a commodity's futures curve is in contango (near-term price lower than far-term price), the market is saying: there is no urgency to buy the physical commodity right now because supply is adequate for current needs. Buyers are comfortable waiting. Storage facilities are not stressed.

For COPPERUSDT and bitunix oil futures, persistent contango signals: adequate physical inventory, no immediate supply stress, and therefore reduced near-term price pressure from physical market tightness. Contango is the "normal" curve shape when markets are well-supplied.

When a commodity's futures curve is in backwardation (near-term price higher than far-term price), the market is saying: physical buyers need this commodity now and are willing to pay a premium for immediate delivery. Near-term demand exceeds immediately available supply.
