How to Use Futures Grid Bot — Complete Setup Walkthrough

Setting up a futures grid bot doesn't require coding skills, but it does require deliberate decisions at each step. Here's the practical walkthrough from start to finish.


## Step-by-Step Setup

Step one: choose your trading pair. High-volume pairs like BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT have the deepest liquidity and tightest spreads, which matters because grid bots execute many trades and small execution inefficiencies compound. Start with majors until you're comfortable with the mechanics.

## Step 2: Select Direction

Select direction. Long if you're moderately bullish or expect range-bound action above support. Short if you're moderately bearish or expect action below resistance. Review the chart before deciding — this isn't a coin flip.

## Step 3: Define Your Price Range

Define your price range. Enter the lowest price and highest price. The bot only trades within these boundaries. Base your range on 7 to 14 days of recent price action. Set boundaries slightly inside the observed extremes to account for wicks and noise.

## Step 4: Set Grid Quantity

Set grid quantity. A number between 20 and 40 works for most ranges. More grids for tighter ranges, fewer for wider ones. The key is that each grid cycle should produce meaningful profit after trading fees.

## Step 5: Configure Leverage and Margin

Configure leverage and margin. For beginners, 3x to 5x leverage is conservative and survivable. Enter the margin you want to allocate — this is the capital that moves from your spot account to the bot's dedicated account.

## Step 6: Enable TP/SL

Enable take-profit and stop-loss. TP closes the strategy when cumulative profit hits your target. SL exits when losses reach your defined limit. Both are optional but strongly recommended for hands-off operation.

Step seven: review and create. Double-check every parameter. Once confirmed, the bot begins placing orders immediately.

After launching, monitor for the first few hours. Check if the bot is actively trading or if it paused because price left your range immediately. If that happens, your range was too narrow or your timing was off. Stop the bot, reassess, and try again. The first grid you run is a learning experience — treat it as tuition rather than an expected profit. Document your parameters and the outcome so you can refine your approach with each subsequent attempt.
