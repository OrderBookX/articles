## Why MicroStrategy Chose Bitcoin as a Treasury Asset

In 2020, MicroStrategy CEO Michael Saylor concluded that holding cash and short-term US Treasuries in corporate treasury was a losing strategy because low interest rates were providing near-zero nominal returns while monetary expansion was creating real purchasing power losses. He identified Bitcoin as an alternative treasury reserve asset with a fixed supply schedule (21 million coin maximum), rapidly growing institutional adoption, and increasing global monetary recognition.

The core thesis: corporate treasuries holding Bitcoin could preserve and grow purchasing power in ways that cash reserves could not, with the trade-off of accepting Bitcoin's higher short-term volatility against the long-term appreciation potential of a monetary asset with fixed supply and growing demand.
