## Why Gold Functions as a Currency Devaluation Hedge

Gold has no national allegiance. No government can print more of it. Its global supply grows at approximately 1.5-2% per year from mining, regardless of monetary policy decisions. When a government expands its money supply faster than its economy grows, the value of that currency falls in real terms relative to gold, which cannot be similarly expanded.

This mechanism is why gold has preserved purchasing power across virtually every currency devaluation episode in recorded history. The Roman debasement of the denarius, the German hyperinflation of the 1920s, the Argentine peso crises, and modern emerging market currency episodes all showed gold maintaining or appreciating in local currency terms while paper money lost value.
