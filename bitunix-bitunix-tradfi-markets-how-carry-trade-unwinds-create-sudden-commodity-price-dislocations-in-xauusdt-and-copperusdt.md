## The Carry Trade Mechanism and Its Commodity Link

A carry trade investor borrows yen at near-zero rates, converts to dollars or Australian dollars, and invests in higher-yielding assets. To execute this trade profitably, the yen must remain stable or weaken. When yen strengthens unexpectedly (typically triggered by Bank of Japan policy surprises or global risk-off events), carry trade investors face simultaneous losses on their yen position and must liquidate the higher-yielding assets to repay yen loans.

The liquidation cascade: carry trade unwinds force simultaneous selling across the assets that carry investors held. In commodity markets, this creates sudden COPPERUSDT and gold price drops that have nothing to do with physical supply or demand, only with leveraged financial positioning being force-liquidated.

The August 2024 yen carry trade unwind is the most recent major example: the BOJ's unexpected rate hike in July 2024 triggered yen appreciation, forcing global carry trade unwinds that temporarily pushed COPPERUSDT and broad risk assets sharply lower before recovering as the liquidation completed.
