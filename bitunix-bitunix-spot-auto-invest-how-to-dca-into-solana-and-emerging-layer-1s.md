## Why Emerging Layer 1s Attract DCA Interest

Solana, Avalanche, and other alternative Layer 1 blockchains compete for smart contract market share with different technical approaches. Solana prioritizes raw throughput and low transaction costs. These technical differences translate into different ecosystem dynamics, different user bases, and potentially different growth trajectories than Ethereum. Investors who believe the smart contract market will support multiple winners want exposure beyond ETH.

## The Risk Profile Difference

DCA into Bitcoin carries the risk of broad crypto market decline. DCA into an emerging Layer 1 carries that same risk plus competitive risk, technology risk, and ecosystem fragility risk. Solana experienced a major network outage period that would have tested any DCA investor's conviction. These additional risk layers mean that Layer 1 altcoin allocations should be significantly smaller than Bitcoin allocations within the same plan.

## Appropriate Allocation Sizing

A conservative multi-asset plan might allocate 5% to 15% of total DCA budget to emerging Layer 1s. An investor putting $100 per week into crypto might allocate $60 to BTC, $25 to ETH, and $15 to SOL. This gives Solana exposure without creating a position large enough to materially damage the portfolio if the asset fails to maintain its competitive position. The smaller the allocation, the less your overall results depend on any single altcoin's performance.

## Quarterly Thesis Review Is Essential

Unlike Bitcoin, where the investment thesis has remained relatively stable for over a decade, Layer 1 theses can shift rapidly. A new competitor, a critical vulnerability, or a regulatory action can fundamentally change the outlook for any emerging blockchain. Review your Layer 1 allocations every three months with fresh analysis. If your original thesis no longer holds, replace the asset or redirect that allocation to your core Bitcoin position without hesitation.
