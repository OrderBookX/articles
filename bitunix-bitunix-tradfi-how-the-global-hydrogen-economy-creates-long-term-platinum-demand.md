## How Platinum Serves the Hydrogen Economy

Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) electrolyzers   the technology used to produce green hydrogen by splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity   require platinum group metals as catalysts on both the anode (iridium, a platinum group metal) and cathode (platinum). Current platinum loading in PEM electrolyzers: approximately 0.3-0.5 grams of platinum per kilowatt of electrolyzer capacity.

PEM fuel cells   which convert hydrogen back to electricity (for hydrogen vehicles, stationary power generation, and industrial applications)   also require platinum catalysts. Current loading: approximately 0.2-0.5 grams per kilowatt.
