## Private Equity Cycles and Commodity Demand

Private equity investment in resource extraction companies (mining, oil and gas, agriculture) affects commodity supply over the medium term. When PE firms are actively investing in commodity production (typical during commodity price boom periods when returns are attractive), new production capacity is being financed that will come online 3-5 years later. This new supply, when it arrives, creates the price ceiling that terminates commodity bull markets.

Conversely, when PE firms are divesting resource extraction investments (typical during commodity price downturns), capital is not flowing to new production, creating the conditions for future supply constraints.
