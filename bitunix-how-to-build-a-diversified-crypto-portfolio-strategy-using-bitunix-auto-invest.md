## The Core Satellite Approach

A proven framework for crypto diversification is the core satellite model. Your core holdings are large-cap, high-liquidity assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum. These typically represent 60% to 80% of your allocation. They provide stability and broad market exposure. Your satellite holdings are smaller positions in altcoins that offer specific exposure. Layer 1 alternatives like Solana or Avalanche, DeFi tokens, or ecosystem plays that you have researched and believe in long-term. These make up the remaining 20% to 40%.

## Rebalancing Through Allocation Adjustments

Over time, different assets will perform differently. Your original 50/25/10/10/5 split might drift to 60/20/8/7/5 as Bitcoin outperforms. Rather than selling winners to rebalance, you can adjust your auto invest percentages to favor underweight positions in future cycles. This approach is called forward rebalancing. Instead of triggering taxable sales, you direct new capital toward underweight assets until the portfolio returns to target allocations.

## How Many Assets Is Too Many

Diversification has diminishing returns. Holding 3 to 7 assets captures most of the risk reduction benefit. Adding 15 or 20 positions introduces complexity without meaningful additional protection, especially in crypto where most altcoins are correlated to Bitcoin anyway. Keep it focused. Choose assets with different use cases and risk profiles rather than collecting tokens for the sake of quantity.

## Size Positions by Conviction

Not every asset deserves equal allocation. Your highest conviction positions should get the largest percentages. Assets you are less certain about should get smaller slices. This is not about prediction. It is about honest assessment of how much you are willing to lose if any single asset goes to zero.
