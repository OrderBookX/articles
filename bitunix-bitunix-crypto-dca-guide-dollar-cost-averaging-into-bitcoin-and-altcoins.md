## Why DCA Works Better in Volatile Markets

Volatility is not your enemy when you DCA. It is your advantage. When prices crash, your fixed investment amount buys more units. When prices spike, you buy fewer. This natural averaging effect produces a lower cost basis than trying to time entries manually.

Consider a scenario where BTC drops 30% over two months then recovers. A lump-sum buyer at the top is still underwater. A DCA buyer accumulated extra units at lower prices and is already profitable when recovery happens. The math favors consistency over prediction.

Bitcoin is the default core holding for most DCA strategies because it has the deepest liquidity and longest track record. That does not make them bad choices. It means they require smaller allocation percentages and more careful selection. A diversified crypto portfolio strategy might look like 50% BTC, 30% ETH, 10% SOL, and 10% spread across two or three smaller positions.

## Setting the Right Frequency

The frequency of your recurring investment depends on your budget and goals. Weekly purchases provide the most data points for price averaging. Bi-weekly works for moderate budgets. Monthly is sufficient for long-term holders who want minimal interaction with the process. There is no universally correct frequency. What matters is that you choose one and stick with it. Changing frequency based on market conditions defeats the purpose entirely.
