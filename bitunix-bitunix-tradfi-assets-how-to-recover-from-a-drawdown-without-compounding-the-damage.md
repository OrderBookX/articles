## Understanding What a Drawdown Actually Tells You

A drawdown is a reduction in account equity from its peak to its current level. A 20% drawdown means your account is worth 20% less than it was at its highest point. Before addressing recovery, accurately diagnose what caused the drawdown:

Category A: Market conditions were unfavorable for your strategy (the regime changed against your instruments). This is the most common cause and requires waiting for conditions to improve, not abandoning the strategy.

Category B: You made analytical errors (traded against strong macro trends, ignored stop-losses, over-leveraged). This requires process correction before resuming normal trading.

Category C: Bad luck (multiple correct theses produced losses due to unexpected events). This is rare in isolation; sustained drawdowns are rarely pure bad luck.

The diagnosis determines the correct recovery approach. Category A requires patience. Category B requires process change. Category C requires continued disciplined execution.
