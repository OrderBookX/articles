# Futures Grid Bot Risk Management — The Six-Layer Defense

Risk management in grid trading isn't a single setting — it's a layered system. Each layer catches what the previous one misses. Traders who rely on only one or two layers eventually get caught by the risk that their defenses didn't cover. Here's the complete six-layer framework.

## Layer 1: Range Quality

Layer one: range quality. Your range should be based on observable market structure, not hope or prediction. A range derived from 14 days of price action, set slightly inside the observed extremes, gives the bot the best chance of staying active. Poor range selection is the root cause of most grid failures.

## Layer 2: Leverage Discipline

Layer two: leverage discipline. Calculate worst-case exposure (all grids filled on one side) and verify your margin survives it. For most traders, 3x to 5x leverage achieves this. Don't let the availability of 200x leverage tempt you into configurations that can't survive normal volatility.

## Layer 3: TP/SL Automation

Layer three: take-profit and stop-loss automation. TP captures accumulated gains at a defined target — preventing the common mistake of watching profits build and then evaporate. SL limits damage by closing everything when losses exceed your threshold. Without these, you're relying entirely on manual monitoring, which fails the moment you sleep, travel, or get distracted.

## Layer 4: Capital Allocation

Layer four: capital allocation limits. Never commit more than 5% to 10% of your total trading capital to a single grid bot. This ensures that even a worst-case grid failure doesn't devastate your overall portfolio. If you're running five bots at 5% each, your maximum total grid exposure is 25% — survivable even if all five underperform simultaneously.

## Layer 5: Portfolio Diversification

Layer five: portfolio diversification. Run multiple bots across different pairs and directions. A long BTC grid and a short ETH grid create partial directional hedging. Diversification across uncorrelated setups reduces the probability that all bots fail at the same time.

## Layer 6: Active Monitoring

Layer six: regular monitoring and adaptive management. Check bots daily. If market structure has shifted — new trends, broken ranges, approaching catalysts — stop and reconfigure. Don't let a bot run on autopilot through conditions that no longer support its original setup.

Together, these six layers create a defense system where each one reduces the risk that slips through the others.
