---------------------------------

Crypto liquidations occur when a leveraged trading position is forcibly closed due to the trader's margin falling below the maintenance requirement. In the context of the **mega crypto crash**, these liquidations were rampant, as the sudden drop in prices triggered margin calls and forced closings of positions. Understanding the dynamics of *liquidation* is crucial for any trader, especially in volatile markets.

<figure class="inline-image">![Diagram showing how crypto liquidations occur during market volatility.](https://cirrus-media.bintangjtobing.workers.dev/inline-images/2026/02/10/7ad8c4cb-12de-4a20-aacc-db00701f5717.jpg)</figure>### What Triggers Crypto Liquidations?

- **Leverage:** The use of borrowed funds to increase trade size. While leverage can amplify gains, it also increases the risk of liquidation.
- **Volatility:** The rapid price fluctuations in cryptocurrencies can quickly change the value of a position, prompting liquidations.
- **Margin Requirements:** If a trader's equity falls below the maintenance margin, a liquidation may occur.
