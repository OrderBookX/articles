## The EU Critical Raw Materials Act

The European Union passed the Critical Raw Materials Act in 2024, establishing supply security targets: by 2030, 10% of EU critical mineral consumption from EU extraction, 40% from EU processing, 15% recycling, no more than 65% from any single third country for any critical mineral.

For XPTUSDT: South Africa currently provides approximately 70% of EU platinum supply   above the 65% single-country concentration limit. This creates regulatory pressure to develop alternative platinum supply chains (more Australian and North American processing, more recycling), which may permanently increase platinum processing costs as supply chains diversify.

## The China Response: Export Restrictions

China has responded to Western critical minerals policies by imposing export restrictions on gallium, germanium, graphite, and other critical minerals in which China has dominant processing positions. The pattern suggests that China could impose restrictions on other critical minerals processing where it dominates   including rare earth processing (already restricted) and potentially graphite and lithium. This creates supply diversification urgency that sustains investment in non-Chinese processing capacity.
