## Counting Cycles

Zoom into the 4-hour chart on Bitunix for that same 14-day period. Count the approximate number of times price crossed each grid level. ## What This Tells You

If price only crossed 5 levels in 14 days, a 25-grid setup would have been mostly idle — your grid count was too high for the volatility. If price crossed 15+ levels regularly, the grid would have been highly active and likely profitable. This data-driven insight is more valuable than any theoretical explanation.

### Applying the Backtest to Live Configuration

The range that performed best historically on your Bitunix chart becomes your starting point. Adjust for current conditions: if volatility has increased since the backtest period (check 14-day ATR), widen range slightly. If decreased, tighten it.
