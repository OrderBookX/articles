## What Meeting Minutes Reveal That Statements Do Not

The post-meeting statement is a negotiated consensus document   every word reflects committee agreement. The minutes reveal the range of views that were expressed before the consensus was crafted:

Dissents and near-dissents: the minutes indicate when committee members were close to dissenting from the decision. When "several members" expressed preference for a different policy action than the consensus adopted, the next meeting's decision is more vulnerable to surprise   policy momentum is fragile.

Economic assessment diversity: the minutes reveal how much disagreement exists about the economic outlook. When "a minority of members" expressed significantly more pessimistic or optimistic views about inflation or growth than the consensus, the economic assessment is less stable than the statement language suggests.

Forward guidance language debates: the minutes describe when committee members debated changing specific words in the forward guidance. Debates about adding or removing "patient," "gradual," or "meeting-by-meeting" from the statement reveal which policy transition the committee is preparing.
