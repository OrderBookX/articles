## The Historical Rate-Commodity Super-Cycle Relationship

1945-1980   Rising rates cycle: US 10-year Treasury yields rose from approximately 2.5% to 15.7% over 35 years. During this period, commodity prices were mixed   gold was suppressed by the gold standard through 1971, then exploded after Nixon closed the gold window. Industrial commodities experienced the 1973-1980 commodity boom driven by OPEC shocks.

1980-2020   Falling rates cycle: US 10-year Treasury yields fell from 15.7% to essentially 0% over 40 years. During this period, two commodity super-cycles emerged: the 2000-2011 commodity super-cycle (driven by Chinese industrialization and real rate declines), and the 2018-2023 gold bull market (driven by negative real rates during the COVID-response era).

The correlation: when long-term nominal rates are falling AND real rates are declining (the 1980-2020 falling rate cycle), commodity prices tend to find structural support from the reduced opportunity cost of holding real assets.
