## The Report's Structure and What to Read First

The GDT report is approximately 30-40 pages. A practitioner's time-efficient reading prioritizes five sections:

Section 1   The Summary Tables (pages 1-3, read first, 3 minutes): two tables provide quarterly demand and supply data by category: jewelry demand by country, investment demand (ETFs, bars/coins), technology demand, and central bank demand. Compare each category to the prior quarter and to the same quarter last year. Identify the year-over-year growth or decline.

Section 2   Jewelry Demand Country Detail (pages 6-10, read second, 4 minutes): focus on India, China, and the Middle East   the three largest jewelry markets collectively. For India: compare to the seasonal expectation (Q2/Q3 typically strong due to wedding and festival seasons). For China: compare to macro context (is the reading consistent with China PMI data for the same period?).

Section 3   Central Bank Demand (pages 14-17, read third, 3 minutes): Which countries bought? Which sold? Is the aggregate pace above or below the prior-year average? Look specifically for new buyers (countries not previously in the regular buyer list   a new buyer signals spreading diversification motivation) and any sustained sellers (likely Western central banks monetizing gold for budget reasons).

Section 4   ETF Flows Commentary (pages 18-20, read fourth, 2 minutes): the GDT provides regional ETF flow data not available in the weekly GLD/IAU tracking. Western ETF flows, Asian ETF flows, and OTC investment are broken out separately. When Asian ETF flows are positive while Western ETF flows are negative, the demand is geographically shifting   a structural change signal.

Section 5   Mine Supply and Recycling (pages 24-28, read fifth, 3 minutes): compare mine supply to analyst forecasts. Any significant deviation (above 2% from consensus estimate) from expected mine production indicates disruptions or outperformance in production. Compare recycling volume to prior quarters   rising recycling indicates elevated gold price is stimulating scrap supply.

## The One Key Number to Extract

From every GDT report, extract one composite number: total gold demand versus total gold supply. When demand exceeds supply: the market is drawing down above-ground inventory   fundamentally bullish. When supply exceeds demand: inventory is accumulating   fundamentally bearish. This supply-demand balance confirms or contradicts what the TIPS yield direction implies.
