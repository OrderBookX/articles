## What the Annual Report Contains That Monthly Data Cannot Provide

The Johnson Matthey Annual Report (available free at matthey.com) provides several data points that cannot be assembled from monthly sources:

Automotive loading data: average grams of platinum and palladium per vehicle, broken down by region and vehicle type. When this figure shows loading declining year-over-year, thriftification and substitution are occurring. When it shows rising loadings (stricter emission standards), demand is growing structurally.

Recycling by category: how much platinum and palladium was recovered from automotive catalysts, jewelry, industrial scrap, and electrical equipment. The recycling composition reveals whether secondary supply is growing due to high prices (behavioral response) or due to the aging vehicle fleet reaching scrap age (structural growth).

Mine supply by country: South Africa production, Russia production, other producers. Year-over-year changes reveal whether supply constraints or expansions are structural or weather/labor event-driven.

Demand by application beyond automotive: fuel cells, chemicals, glass, electrical. When non-automotive platinum demand is growing, the EV risk to automotive demand is being partially offset by new demand applications.
