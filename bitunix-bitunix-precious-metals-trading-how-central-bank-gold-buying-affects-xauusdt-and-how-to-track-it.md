## Why Central Banks Are Buying Record Amounts of Gold

Central bank gold buying accelerated after 2022 when the US and its allies froze Russian central bank assets denominated in dollars and euros following the invasion of Ukraine. This event demonstrated to every central bank globally that dollar and euro reserve assets could be frozen as a geopolitical tool. Countries that wanted to maintain reserve assets outside this risk began diversifying into gold, which cannot be frozen by any external authority.

This geopolitical reserve diversification is a structural, not cyclical, driver of gold demand. It does not reverse during periods of economic growth or rising interest rates the way investment demand does. Central banks buying gold for reserve diversification purposes continue buying regardless of short-term gold price levels because the strategic objective (reducing dependence on dollar and euro reserves) is not price-sensitive.

## Where to Track Central Bank Gold Buying Data

The World Gold Council publishes quarterly central bank gold demand data in its Gold Demand Trends report (available free at gold.org). This report shows total central bank net purchases, the countries buying and selling, and the quarterly trend relative to historical averages.

The International Monetary Fund publishes monthly reserve asset data from member countries through its International Reserves and Foreign Currency Liquidity template. This data updates monthly and provides more current information than the quarterly World Gold Council report.
