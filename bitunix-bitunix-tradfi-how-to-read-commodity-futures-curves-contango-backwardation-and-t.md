## The Term Structure of COPPERUSDT and What It Means

LME copper trades in a three-month forward structure with specific prompt dates. The spread between the LME cash price (immediate delivery) and the 3-month forward price is the most widely quoted signal of physical market tightness.

Backwardation (cash > 3-month): immediate copper is more valuable than copper delivered in 3 months. This occurs when physical buyers need copper now   they are paying a premium for immediate availability. Strong backwardation (above $50/tonne cash premium) signals acute physical tightness. This is Stage 3 of the LME inventory cycle.

Contango (3-month > cash): copper for future delivery is priced above spot. This is the normal carry structure   the price differential pays for 3 months of storage, financing, and insurance costs. Normal contango of $20-40/tonne is neutral. Deep contango (above $80/tonne) signals that the market expects physical oversupply to persist   no one is willing to pay a premium for immediate delivery.

## The Gold Futures Curve and Its Unique Carry Structure

Gold's futures curve is almost always in contango   gold is in permanent contango because the cost of carry (storage, insurance, financing) must be paid by those holding physical gold forward. The normal gold contango closely equals the T-bill rate × the gold price (the financing cost of holding gold inventory).

When gold contango is wider than the T-bill-implied carry cost, it signals that physical gold holders are not willing to lend their gold into the futures market at current rates   they expect gold prices to rise faster than the carry implies. ## The Oil Futures Curve Intelligence

WTI crude oil futures curves provide multi-year demand and supply expectations. When the first 12 months of the WTI curve are in backwardation (near months above far months), the physical market is tight and inventory is drawing. When the full curve is in contango, the market expects inventory builds and supply adequacy.
