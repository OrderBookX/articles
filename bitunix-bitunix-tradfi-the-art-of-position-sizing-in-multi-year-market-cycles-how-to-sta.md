## The Pyramid Strategy for Multi-Year Cycles

Enter at low conviction (25-30% of intended multi-year position size) when the thesis first becomes visible   before the cycle is widely recognized. As the cycle develops and is confirmed by additional evidence (multiple PMI beats, LME inventory entering Stage 3, copper-gold ratio confirming the industrial expansion), add additional tranches (25-30% each) at progressively higher price levels.

This "pyramid" approach builds the maximum position as conviction increases, rather than starting at maximum size when the thesis is weakest and the price is cheapest. The practical benefit: if the thesis turns out to be wrong early (before conviction is confirmed), the initial small position creates modest losses. If correct, the subsequent additions build the full position as the cycle matures.
