## The Current CBDC Landscape

As of 2024, three major economies have launched retail CBDCs:

China: Digital Yuan (e-CNY). Deployed through major state banks and select private platforms. Used primarily for government salary payments and subsidized retail transactions. Penetration remains limited outside government-directed use cases.

Nigeria: eNaira. The first African retail CBDC. Adoption has been limited by infrastructure and trust concerns.

India: Digital Rupee. Piloting since late 2022 with select banks. Expanding gradually.

Major economies still in development or pilot phase: European Digital Euro (ECB), Digital Dollar (Federal Reserve officially not pursuing, though monitoring), UK Digital Pound. Most major developed market CBDCs are still 3-7 years from broad retail deployment.

## Why CBDCs Do Not Immediately Threaten USDC

The competitive analysis between CBDCs and USDC reveals they serve somewhat different markets:

CBDCs are government money: subject to government surveillance, programmable restrictions (the government could program a CBDC to be spent only on specific categories), and subject to the full monetary policy decisions of the issuing government. These characteristics make CBDCs less attractive for privacy-conscious users and for cross-border transactions where a foreign government's digital currency is not useful.

USDC is private sector dollar-pegged money: usable globally on DeFi protocols, programmable for private contracts, and not subject to government transaction surveillance at the individual level (beyond KYC at the exchange level).
