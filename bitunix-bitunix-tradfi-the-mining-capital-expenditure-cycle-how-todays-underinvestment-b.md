## How the Mining CapEx Cycle Creates Price Cycles

The copper mining CapEx cycle follows a predictable pattern driven by commodity price incentives: when copper prices are high, mining companies commit to new project development. When prices fall, development investment is cut. Because new mines take 7-10 years from investment commitment to production start, the CapEx cuts made during price downturns create supply constraints 7-10 years later that drive the next price cycle.

The most actionable historical example: the copper price collapse of 2012-2016 (from $4.50/lb to $2.00/lb) triggered massive CapEx cuts across the industry. Major greenfield projects were deferred, cancelled, or scaled back. The supply growth that would have come from those projects never materialized   contributing to the supply deficit that emerged in 2021-2022 when demand recovered faster than the reduced supply pipeline could accommodate.
