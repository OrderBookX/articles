## The Gold VIX: GVZ as an XAUUSDT Timing Tool

The CBOE Gold ETF Volatility Index (GVZ), sometimes called the "Gold VIX," measures the 30-day implied volatility of GLD options. It is freely available on the CBOE website (cboe.com) and on macrotrends.net.

When GVZ is at historical lows (below 12-13): options market participants expect minimal gold price movement in the next 30 days. Low IV is the best entry timing condition   when the options market is pricing in minimal volatility, entering a directional position costs less (lower spread risk, lower funding rate) and the potential for a volatility expansion move (which would benefit the position) is higher than when IV is already elevated.

When GVZ is at historical highs (above 25-30): the options market is pricing in extreme volatility. Entering large positions during high-IV periods means the position is entering when expected moves are already priced in   the most expensive time to be long. High IV is often associated with major scheduled events (FOMC meetings, key economic data) or recent acute price moves. Reduce position size during high-IV periods.
