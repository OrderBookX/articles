## The Open Interest-Price Relationship Framework

When price rises and open interest rises simultaneously, new money is entering the market on the long side, confirming the uptrend. The combination of rising price and rising OI is the strongest trend confirmation signal available in futures markets.

When price rises but open interest falls, existing short positions are being covered rather than new longs being established. Short-covering rallies are technically weaker because the demand is finite (limited by the number of existing shorts). Once short covering is complete, the upward price pressure disappears and the rally often reverses quickly.

When price falls and open interest rises, new money is entering on the short side, confirming the downtrend. When price falls and open interest falls simultaneously, long positions are being liquidated. Like short-covering rallies, liquidation-driven declines have a finite floor once existing longs have sold.
