# Bitunix Futures Grid Parameters Explained — Why ATR Should Guide Your Range

Most grid traders set ranges by eyeballing charts. Better grid traders on Bitunix use Average True Range — a volatility indicator that quantifies how much price actually moves — to set ranges that match the asset's real behavior.

### What ATR Tells You on Bitunix Charts

Open your pair on Bitunix TradingView Pro and add the ATR indicator (14-period). For BTC/USDT, you might see a daily ATR of $3,000. This means BTC typically moves $3,000 in a single day. For ETH, maybe $150. For gold on the TradFi section, perhaps $40.

## Translating ATR to Grid Range

A practical rule: set your grid range at 2.5 to 3.5 times the 14-day ATR. For BTC with $3,000 ATR, that suggests a $7,500 to $10,500 range. This width comfortably contains most daily swings while keeping the bot active.

### If Range Equals ATR

If your range roughly equals one ATR, price will frequently exit the range in a single day. The bot pauses constantly. Too tight.

### If Range Exceeds 5x ATR

If your range is five times ATR, most grid levels will never trigger during normal conditions. The bot is deployed but inactive. Too wide. Capital sits idle when it could earn yield in Bitunix Earn.

## ATR Changes Over Time

Volatility is not static. After major moves, ATR expands. During long consolidation, it contracts. Check ATR weekly on your Bitunix chart and be prepared to stop and reconfigure your grid if ATR has shifted meaningfully. A grid configured for $3,000 ATR underperforms when ATR drops to $1,500 or spikes to $5,000.

## Applying ATR to TradFi Metals

Gold (XAUUSDT) on Bitunix typically has much lower ATR relative to price than BTC. This means tighter absolute ranges work, potentially with more grid levels for frequency. The same ATR methodology applies — just calibrated to the asset's specific volatility profile.
