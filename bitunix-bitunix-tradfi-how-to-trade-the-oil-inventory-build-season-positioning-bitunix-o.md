## The Annual Refinery Maintenance Calendar

Spring turnaround season (March-May): US and European refineries schedule their primary annual maintenance during this period   chosen because it falls between the winter heating season (when refineries run at maximum to produce distillates) and the summer driving season (when they run at maximum to produce gasoline). During spring turnaround, crude throughput falls, crude inventory builds (refineries not processing crude), and product inventory falls (drawdown from winter production ends).

Fall turnaround season (October-November): a secondary maintenance period before the winter heating season.

The predictable crude inventory build during March-May creates a foreseeable bitunix oil futures headwind   inventory is building not because of demand weakness but because of scheduled supply chain maintenance. This seasonal inventory build depresses crude prices temporarily and creates a mean-reversion entry opportunity when the turnaround season ends (refinery restarts drive rapid crude inventory draws in May-June).
