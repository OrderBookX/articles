## The Five Metrics That Matter on Bitunix

Grid profit shows realized gains from completed cycles — this only increases while the bot actively trades. Unrealized PnL shows current open position value — it fluctuates in real time. Margin usage shows how much of your allocation is currently deployed — if approaching 100%, liquidation risk rises.

## Daily Routine: Three Numbers, Five Minutes

Every morning, open the Bitunix app or web dashboard. For each running bot: check total PnL (are you net positive or negative?), check bot status (active or paused?), and note margin usage trend (climbing or stable?). If all three are healthy, close the app and continue your day.

## Warning Signals and What They Mean

A paused bot means price left your range. If paused for over 48 hours, the market structure has likely shifted — stop and reassess on the Bitunix TradingView chart. Declining total PnL with rising grid profit means the trend is working against you — the bot completes cycles but accumulates directional loss faster. Margin usage climbing toward 100% means the bot is filling one-sided — potential liquidation territory.
