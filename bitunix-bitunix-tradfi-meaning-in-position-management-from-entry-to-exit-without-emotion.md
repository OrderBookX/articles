## Scaling Out vs All-Out Exits

The worst exit execution approach is waiting for a single perfect exit price and then closing the entire position at once. Markets rarely give you the perfect exit; waiting for it means frequently exiting at worse prices than a systematic partial-exit approach.

Scaling out: take 30% at first target, 30% at second target, hold 40% until trailing stop is triggered. This approach guarantees partial profits at multiple levels, eliminates the "I should have held longer" regret from early full exits, and eliminates the "I should have exited earlier" regret from holding full size too long.

## Adding to Winning Positions: The Pyramid Strategy

Adding to positions that are already profitable (pyramiding) is a disciplined way to increase exposure to confirmed winning trades without taking additional risk beyond the initial stop-loss. Each addition uses the previously realized gains as additional margin, meaning the total risk of the pyramided position is never more than the initial stop-loss risk.

Add-on levels should be pre-defined in the journal before the trade, not improvised mid-trade.
