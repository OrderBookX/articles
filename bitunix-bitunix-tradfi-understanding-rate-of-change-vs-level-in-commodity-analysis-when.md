## The Rate-of-Change Principle for XAUUSDT

XAUUSDT does not respond primarily to the level of TIPS yields   it responds to the direction (rate of change) of TIPS yields. This distinction is critical:

Incorrect reasoning: "TIPS yields are at 1.5%   historically, this level has been associated with gold at $2,100. Gold is at $2,000. Therefore gold is cheap at current yields and should be bought."

Correct reasoning: "TIPS yields are at 1.5% but have been rising for three consecutive weeks. The rate of change is positive (rising). Therefore the monetary environment for gold is deteriorating regardless of the absolute yield level   and the entry timing is incorrect regardless of the apparent level-based cheapness."

Gold's best periods have occurred when TIPS yields were falling, even from relatively high levels (2019-2020 as yields fell from 1.0% to -1.0%).
