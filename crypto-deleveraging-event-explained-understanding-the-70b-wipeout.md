What is a Crypto Deleveraging Event?
------------------------------------

A **crypto deleveraging event** refers to a rapid reduction of leveraged positions in the market, often triggered by significant price movements or external factors. This process can lead to a *forced liquidation cascade*, where the liquidation of a few positions triggers further liquidations, amplifying market volatility.

<figure class="inline-image">![Illustrate a crypto deleveraging event with a flowchart showing the cascade effect.](https://cirrus-media.bintangjtobing.workers.dev/inline-images/2025/12/27/a7cc0590-214b-4da1-98c6-faa0580d99d7.jpg)</figure>### The Mechanisms Behind Deleveraging

Deleveraging typically begins with a **margin breach**, where traders' positions fall below the required margin levels. This can result in a **margin call**, prompting traders to either add more funds or face liquidation. If many positions are liquidated simultaneously, it leads to an **auto-deleveraging (ADL)** effect, causing a cascade as the market attempts to rebalance itself.
