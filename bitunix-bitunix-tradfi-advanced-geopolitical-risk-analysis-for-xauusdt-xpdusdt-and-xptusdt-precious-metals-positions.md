## Gold's Geopolitical Risk Profile: The Safe-Haven Premium vs Supply Risk

Gold has a dual geopolitical risk profile: it simultaneously benefits from safe-haven demand (geopolitical fear drives gold buying) and faces supply risk (gold production is concentrated in politically stable but not immune countries). The net effect depends on whether a specific geopolitical event affects gold supply or primarily creates safe-haven demand.

Top gold producing countries by geopolitical risk classification: China (19% of production, moderate political risk but state-controlled), Australia (10%, low risk), Russia (9%, elevated risk since 2022), Canada (6%, low risk), United States (6%, very low risk), Ghana (6%, moderate risk), South Africa (4%, moderate risk). A geopolitical event affecting Russia, Ghana, or South Africa simultaneously threatens supply and creates safe-haven demand, creating amplified price moves relative to events that only create safe-haven demand without supply impact.

For XAUUSDT positioning: when geopolitical events occur in major gold-producing countries, the supply disruption premium adds to the standard safe-haven premium, creating larger and more sustained price appreciation than purely demand-side events.
