## What Is TradFi's Normal Basis Behavior?

What is TradFi's theoretical basis value? In a market with no unusual supply or demand pressures, the futures price should exceed the spot price by the cost of carry: the interest cost of financing the physical commodity, plus storage costs, minus any convenience yield. For gold, which has minimal storage costs and acts as a near-cash instrument, the futures basis approximately equals the interest rate differential between the futures maturity and today.

When the basis is significantly wider than normal carry cost (futures price much higher than theory predicts), it signals that sellers of the futures contract (who must hold the physical) are doing so at a discount, implying they either expect future prices to be lower or have specific near-term supply reasons to discount.
