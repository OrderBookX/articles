## The Weekly Framework

A weekly grid routine looks like this. Start each week by analyzing the market. Which pairs are consolidating? Where are the clear support and resistance levels? What does volatility look like? Use Bitunix's TradingView-integrated charts with K-Line Ultra indicators to identify candidates.

On Monday or Tuesday, deploy grids with conservative parameters on the pairs that show the best consolidation patterns. Use 3x to 5x leverage. Set ranges based on actual recent price data. Enable TP and SL on every bot.

## Mid-Week Check-ins

During the week, check performance daily — five minutes is enough. Look at grid profit, unrealized PnL, total PnL. Note any bots that have paused due to range exits. Check if the broader market structure has shifted.

## Friday Review

By Friday, evaluate results. Stop underperforming grids. For successful ones, decide whether to let them continue into the following week or take profits and reset. If market conditions have changed, adjust ranges or directions for the next week's deployments.

## The Trading Log

Keep a simple log: date, pair, direction, range, grid count, leverage, margin, TP/SL levels, and final result. After a month, you'll have enough data to identify patterns — which pairs work best for your style, which leverage levels produce the best risk-adjusted returns, and which conditions correlate with grid success or failure.
