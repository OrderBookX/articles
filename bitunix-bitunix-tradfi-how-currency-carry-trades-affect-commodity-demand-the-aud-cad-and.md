## Why Commodity Currencies Matter for COPPERUSDT and XAUUSDT

Australia is a major gold and iron ore exporter. Canada is a major oil and mining metals exporter. Brazil is a major copper, iron ore, and agricultural commodity exporter. When carry trades are active   borrowing in JPY or CHF to invest in AUD, CAD, or BRL   these currencies strengthen, which:

Makes AUD-priced gold exports more expensive for USD buyers (potentially reducing Australian gold export competitiveness). More importantly, AUD strength reflects positive global risk appetite, which typically coincides with strong commodity demand periods.

When carry trades unwind   typically during risk-off episodes when global investors reduce risk exposure simultaneously   AUD, CAD, and BRL fall sharply while JPY rises sharply (yen safe-haven buying). This synchronized currency move compresses commodity prices because: (1) global risk aversion reduces demand expectations, (2) commodity exporters' currencies fall (initially appearing to make their exports cheaper), and (3) the unwinding of financial flows reduces commodity investment demand.
