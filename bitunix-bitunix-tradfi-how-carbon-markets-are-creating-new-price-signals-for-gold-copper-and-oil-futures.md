## Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) and Metal Trade

The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, being phased in during 2023-2026, charges imports of steel, aluminum, cement, and other carbon-intensive goods for their embedded carbon emissions. While copper and precious metals are not yet included in CBAM, the mechanism establishes the policy framework that could expand to metals.

If CBAM expands to copper, it would advantage low-carbon copper producers (who can sell into Europe without CBAM charges) and disadvantage high-carbon producers, potentially reshaping global copper trade flows and creating new supply-cost dynamics for COPPERUSDT.
