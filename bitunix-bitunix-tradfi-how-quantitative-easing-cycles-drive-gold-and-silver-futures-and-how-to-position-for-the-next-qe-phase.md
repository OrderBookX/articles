## How QE Affects Gold Through Four Transmission Mechanisms

Mechanism 1: Real interest rate suppression. QE purchases of government bonds push bond prices up and yields down. When bond yields fall below the inflation rate, real rates become negative, making gold the most attractive store of value because cash and bonds are now providing negative real returns. This is the most powerful XAUUSDT driver activated by QE.

Mechanism 2: Dollar weakening. QE expands the US dollar money supply faster than economic growth, which decreases each dollar's purchasing power relative to real assets including gold. A larger money supply chasing the same supply of gold means higher gold prices in dollar terms.

Mechanism 3: Inflation expectations elevation. QE signals that monetary authorities are tolerating or encouraging inflation, which raises inflation expectations. Gold is the most historically reliable inflation hedge, so rising inflation expectations increase gold demand.

Mechanism 4: Risk-on spillover. QE reduces bond yields, pushing investors into riskier assets including equities, emerging markets, and precious metals. The risk-on environment broadly benefits XAUUSDT and XAGUSDT through capital flow into real assets.
