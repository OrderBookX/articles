## What Financial Conditions Index Measures

The Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index (NFCI, published weekly at chicagofed.org) and the Goldman Sachs Financial Conditions Index (GS FCI) are the most widely referenced versions. Both measure whether financial conditions are tighter or looser than historical average.

A negative NFCI reading means conditions are looser than average (easy credit, tight spreads, rising asset prices): accommodative for risk assets including COPPERUSDT and equity derivatives. A positive NFCI reading means conditions are tighter than average (expensive credit, wide spreads, lower asset prices): defensive for risk assets and potentially supportive for XAUUSDT through the safe-haven channel.
