## The Four Historical Commodity Supercycles

Academic economists Jacks, O'Rourke, and Williamson identified four major commodity supercycles in their comprehensive 2011 study spanning the 19th and 20th centuries.

Supercycle one: 1870s-1890s. Driven by the first wave of American and European industrialization. Steel, coal, and copper led the cycle.

Supercycle two: 1900s-1930s. Driven by the second industrialization wave, railroad construction, and the automobile age emergence. Oil became a commodity in this cycle.

Supercycle three: 1945-1975. Post-World War II reconstruction of Europe and Japan, followed by the first wave of Asian industrialization. All commodities participated.

Supercycle four: 1999-2016. Chinese industrialization and urbanization. Copper, iron ore, coal, and oil all reached multi-decade or all-time highs during this cycle.
