## The Recycling Supply Mechanism

When gold prices rise significantly, holders of existing gold objects (jewelry, dental gold, electronics containing gold) are incentivized to sell their holdings for cash. Jewelry stores collect unwanted or broken pieces, electronics recyclers increase their gold recovery operations, and dentists find patients requesting gold crown replacements. This recycling creates new above-ground gold supply that supplements mine production.

The recycling supply response is price-elastic: a 20% gold price increase may increase recycling volumes by 10-15% over the following year as recycling pipelines respond to the price signal. This counter-cyclical supply is one reason gold price appreciation tends to slow and eventually reverse during prolonged bull markets, even without macro driver changes: rising prices encourage supply release from existing holders.
