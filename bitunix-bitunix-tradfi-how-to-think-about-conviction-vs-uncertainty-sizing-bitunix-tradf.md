## The Conviction Drift Problem and Its Solution

A common error: entering at conviction 7 and then mentally increasing conviction to 9 as the position moves in favor. This "conviction drift" leads to adding to positions at worse risk-reward ratios than the original entry. The rule: conviction level is fixed at entry. Re-evaluation occurs only at the next weekly review with fresh data   not based on price movement alone.
