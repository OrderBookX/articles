# Bitunix Futures Grid Trading Bot — Grid Profit vs Total PnL Explained

The most dangerous number on your Bitunix grid dashboard is grid profit — not because it is wrong, but because it tells an incomplete story. Understanding what it includes and what it hides is critical.

## Grid Profit: What It Shows

Grid profit is the cumulative realized gain from completed buy-sell cycles. Every completed cycle adds to it. It only goes up while the bot actively trades. When Bitunix displays $150 in grid profit, that means $150 was captured from successful oscillations.

## Unrealized PnL: What Grid Profit Hides

Your bot also holds an open position that fluctuates with the market. If price moved against your direction since the last fill, unrealized PnL is negative. Bitunix displays this separately — and it is the number many traders ignore.

## Total PnL: The Truth

Total PnL combines both: grid profit plus unrealized PnL. A bot showing $150 grid profit with negative $200 unrealized PnL has a total PnL of negative $50. Closing the bot now means a net loss despite the encouraging grid profit headline.

### Why This Disconnect Happens

In trending markets, the bot completes cycles (grid profit rises) while accumulating directional exposure against the trend (unrealized loss grows).
