## The NBS PMI Report Structure

The National Bureau of Statistics Manufacturing PMI releases five primary sub-indexes alongside the headline composite PMI. Each sub-index carries distinct information for COPPERUSDT analysis:

Production sub-index: measures current factory output. Relevant for copper only if it reflects actual manufacturing activity rather than destocking. Compare to New Orders to check validity.

New Orders sub-index (most important for COPPERUSDT): measures new purchase orders received by manufacturers. Since copper must be ordered before production commences, this sub-index leads copper demand by 4-6 weeks. Any reading above 51.0 with the prior month also above 51.0 represents sustained new order recovery.

New Export Orders sub-index: measures new orders specifically from foreign buyers. When New Export Orders diverges from New Orders (domestic)   for example, export orders weak while domestic orders strong   the copper demand is driven by domestic Chinese infrastructure and real estate rather than export manufacturing. Domestic infrastructure uses more copper per unit of output than export electronics assembly.

Input Prices sub-index: measures prices paid for raw materials including copper. When Input Prices is rising above 55.0 while New Orders is above 51.0, manufacturers are accepting higher copper prices to meet strong demand   a confirmation of genuine physical tightness.

## The Caixin PMI Report Structure

The Caixin PMI is a survey of smaller private-sector manufacturers and is published with more granular commentary than the NBS release. The Caixin press release (available in English on tradingeconomics.com or the Caixin website) provides an industry-specific breakdown that the NBS release does not.

The Caixin comment section often specifically mentions copper-intensive sectors: "output growth in electrical equipment," "new orders rising in construction materials," or "input costs rising in metals-intensive sectors." These qualitative comments translate directly into copper demand signals that the quantitative sub-indexes alone cannot provide.
