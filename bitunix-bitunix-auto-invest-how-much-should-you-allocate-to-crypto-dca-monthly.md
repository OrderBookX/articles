## The Sustainability Test

Take your monthly income after all fixed expenses, including rent, utilities, food, insurance, and debt payments. From what remains, identify the amount you could lose entirely without affecting your quality of life. Now take 50% of that number. This is your starting DCA budget. The 50% buffer exists because markets can decline for extended periods, and you need to be financially comfortable enough to watch your investment drop without needing to liquidate.

## Common Allocation Frameworks

Some financial planners suggest the 50/30/20 rule. Fifty percent of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and investments. Your crypto DCA allocation should come from the 20% savings portion, not from the wants category. If your total investment allocation is $400 per month, a reasonable crypto DCA amount might be $50 to $100 per month depending on your overall portfolio composition and risk tolerance.

## The Minimum Viable Amount

There is no minimum that is too small. Even $10 per week deployed consistently over years builds a meaningful position. The psychological benefit of starting small is that you build the habit without the stress of significant capital at risk. Once the habit is established and your confidence in the strategy grows, increasing the amount is a simple plan adjustment.

## Scaling With Income

As your income grows, consider increasing your DCA allocation proportionally. A raise of $500 per month could translate into an additional $50 to $100 in your auto invest plan. Incremental increases compound over time without ever creating the financial pressure that leads to plan abandonment.

## What Not to Do

Never fund your DCA plan with borrowed money, emergency savings, or capital earmarked for near-term expenses. Never increase your allocation during a bull market euphoria because it feels like free money. Never allocate so aggressively that a 50% market decline would cause genuine financial hardship. The goal is to invest an amount that is meaningful enough to matter in 5 years but small enough that losing it entirely would not change your life today.
