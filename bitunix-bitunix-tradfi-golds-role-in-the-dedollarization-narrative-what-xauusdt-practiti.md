## The Measurable Evidence: What the Data Actually Shows

Three specific data series measure dedollarization progress:

Global reserve composition (IMF COFER data): published quarterly with a 6-month lag, tracks the percentage of global foreign exchange reserves held in each currency. The dollar's share has declined from approximately 72% in 2000 to approximately 58% in 2024   a genuine 14 percentage point decline over 24 years. But the decline has been gradual and much of the reduction has gone to euro, not to gold or alternative currencies.

Central bank gold as a percentage of total reserves: the World Gold Council's Central Bank Gold Statistics track this. When emerging market central banks (Poland, Hungary, Turkey, India, China) are increasing gold as a percentage of total reserves, they are simultaneously reducing other reserve currencies   this is the cleanest measurable evidence of gold-for-dollar substitution.

SWIFT payment share: the percentage of global payments conducted in USD through SWIFT. This has also declined modestly (from approximately 43% in 2019 to approximately 38% in 2024) but remains dominant. Alternative payment systems (CIPS in China) are growing but from a very small base.
