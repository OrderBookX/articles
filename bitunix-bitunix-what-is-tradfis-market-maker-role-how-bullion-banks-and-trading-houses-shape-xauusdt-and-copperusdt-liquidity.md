## What Is TradFi's Bullion Bank Market Making?

What is TradFi's bullion bank function in gold markets? Major bullion banks (HSBC, JPMorgan, UBS, Standard Chartered) maintain large, continuously updated two-sided markets in gold. They buy from producers who need to sell forward production and sell to consumers (jewelers, manufacturers) who need to lock in input costs. Between these physical market activities, they also trade with speculative investors, ETF providers, and retail demand aggregators.

Bullion banks manage their resulting net exposure through futures, options, and swaps markets, creating a continuous chain from physical mine output through financial markets to end consumers. Their market-making function means they are rarely purely directionally positioned, instead managing net book exposure within defined risk limits.
