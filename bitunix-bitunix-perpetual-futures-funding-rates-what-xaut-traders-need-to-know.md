## How Funding Rates Work

Funding rates are periodic payments exchanged between long and short position holders. When the funding rate is positive, longs pay shorts. When negative, shorts pay longs. This mechanism keeps the XAUT perpetual futures price aligned with the spot reference price. On Bitunix perpetual futures, funding settlements occur at regular intervals throughout the day.

## What Drives XAUT Funding Rates

When more traders are long XAUTUSDT than short, the funding rate tends to be positive, making it costly to hold long positions. When more traders are short, the rate turns negative, making shorts expensive. During gold bull markets when sentiment is overwhelmingly bullish, positive funding rates can significantly erode long position profits on XAUT perpetual futures Bitunix contracts over time.
