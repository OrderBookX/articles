## The Quarterly Silver Demand Decomposition

Silver demand can be decomposed into five quarterly-trackable components:

Q1 (January-March): Seasonally weak. Post-Chinese New Year destocking. Electronics manufacturers begin restocking for the year but actual production is still in startup phase. Solar installations slow in northern hemisphere winter. Historically the weakest quarter for silver demand.

Q2 (April-June): Building. Electronics manufacturing ramps for the year. Solar installations begin in Europe and North America. India pre-Akshaya Tritiya buying builds (late Q1 or early Q2 depending on calendar). Historically moderate-to-positive silver demand growth.

Q3 (July-September): Seasonally strong. Peak solar installation season globally (maximum daylight hours in northern hemisphere driving maximum solar economics). Electronics manufacturing at full capacity for back-to-school and holiday pre-production. Historically the strongest industrial demand quarter.

Q4 (October-December): Mixed. Solar installations slow as daylight decreases. Electronics demand for holiday season completed earlier (most Christmas electronics were manufactured in Q2-Q3). However, Indian festive jewelry demand (Diwali) creates physical silver buying.
