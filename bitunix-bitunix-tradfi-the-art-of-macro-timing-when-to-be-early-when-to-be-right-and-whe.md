## The Three Timing Modes

Mode 1   Anticipatory Entry (Be Early):

When macro regime transitions are clearly underway and the timing uncertainty is about weeks not months, anticipatory entry captures more of the move at the cost of potentially being 2-4 weeks early. The specific conditions that justify anticipatory entry: the Fed has signaled a definitive policy pivot (not just one dovish speech but a policy statement language change), physical market tightness in copper has been building for 8+ consecutive weeks, or the OPEC compliance data has shown >80% adherence for three consecutive months.

In these cases, waiting for additional confirmation misses too much of the move. Enter with position size at 50% of intended maximum and add on confirmation.

Mode 2   Confirmation Entry (Wait to Be Right):

When the directional signal is present but not yet confirmed by physical market data, waiting for the second or third confirming data point reduces entry risk at the cost of slightly higher entry prices. This is the standard approach for most COPPERUSDT PMI-based entries and XAUUSDT TIPS yield-based entries.

Mode 3   Patient Holding (Be Patient Through the Development):

When a long-duration thesis (energy transition copper demand, Fed multi-year rate cutting cycle) is playing out over months or years, the appropriate response to temporary adverse moves is patience rather than action   as long as the long-duration fundamental has not changed.
