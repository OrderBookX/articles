## The Problem With Limited Market Hours

Traditional commodity markets like COMEX operate roughly 23 hours per day on weekdays but close on weekends. Stock exchanges operate even fewer hours. These closures create gap risk: price movements that occur while markets are closed result in opening prices that can differ significantly from closing prices. For leveraged positions, gap risk can trigger unexpected liquidations or margin calls before traders can react. Weekend geopolitical events are particularly dangerous for TradFi positions that cannot be adjusted until Monday.
