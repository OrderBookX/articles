## The Core Cross-Margin vs Isolated Margin Distinction

Cross-margin: the entire account balance serves as the margin pool for the position. If the position moves adversely, it draws on unrealized gains from other positions before liquidation triggers. This provides maximum flexibility and resistance to temporary adverse moves.

Isolated margin: a fixed amount of margin is allocated to the position. If the position moves adversely beyond that allocated margin, it is liquidated regardless of the account's overall equity. This prevents losses from spreading but creates more frequent liquidations from normal volatility.
