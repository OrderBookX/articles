## Mistake: DCA Into Dying Projects

The most expensive DCA mistake is continuing to buy an asset that has fundamentally failed. A token that has lost 90% of its value and has no active development, no community, and no clear use case is not a DCA opportunity. It is a dead project. Automation makes this mistake worse because you keep buying automatically while ignoring the evidence of failure. Review every altcoin in your plan quarterly with honest eyes. Ask whether you would add this asset to your plan today if it were not already there. If the answer is no, the sunk cost fallacy is the only thing keeping it in your portfolio, and that is not a valid investment reason.

## Mistake: No Stop Loss on Altcoin Allocations

Bitcoin has demonstrated resilience across every bear market. Most altcoins have not. DCA into Bitcoin through a 70% drawdown has historically rewarded patience. DCA into the wrong altcoin through a 70% drawdown often means accumulating a position that never recovers. Set a personal rule for altcoin DCA. If an altcoin drops more than 80% from its peak and your thesis has changed, remove it from the plan regardless of sunk cost feelings.

## Mistake: Funding DCA With Money You Need

This is the mistake that creates real financial hardship. If your DCA plan forces you to choose between investment and expenses during a downturn, you will sell at the worst possible time to cover costs. Only invest truly disposable income. If you lose your job tomorrow, your DCA plan should be something you can pause without affecting your ability to pay rent and buy food for six months.

## Mistake: Sharing Your Plan Publicly

Telling social media about your DCA plan invites unsolicited opinions during every market move. When BTC drops 20%, people will tell you to stop buying. When it pumps 30%, people will tell you to sell. These external voices add noise to a strategy that requires silence and consistency. Keep your plan private. The discipline required for successful DCA is fragile enough without external voices adding doubt during drawdowns and pressure during rallies. Protect your execution environment by limiting who can influence your decisions.

## Mistake: Ignoring Tax Implications

Every automated purchase creates a taxable event in most jurisdictions. If you run a daily DCA plan for three years, you have over 1,000 individual purchase records to manage. Start tracking from day one or face a nightmare during tax season. Export your transaction history regularly and store it securely.
