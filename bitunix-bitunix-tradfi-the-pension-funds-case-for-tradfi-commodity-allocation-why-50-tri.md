## The Liability Matching Problem That Commodities Solve

Pension funds have obligations denominated in nominal currency (future pension payments) but their beneficiaries' real purchasing power requirements are determined by inflation. If inflation runs at 5% for 20 years while the pension fund's assets return only 4% annually, the fund's real purchasing power erodes by 20% over the period   leaving it unable to meet its real obligations even if it meets its nominal ones.

Commodities, and gold specifically, provide inflation correlation that other asset classes (particularly nominal bonds) cannot. When inflation rises from 2% to 5%, a 10% gold allocation provides a portfolio return enhancement that partially offsets the inflation erosion on nominal fixed income holdings.
