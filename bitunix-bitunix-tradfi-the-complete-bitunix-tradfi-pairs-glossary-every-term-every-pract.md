## Monetary and Rate Terms

Real interest rate: the nominal interest rate minus inflation expectations. Measured by TIPS yield (FRED series DFII10). Gold's primary driver. When negative, provides structural support for all real assets.

TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities): US government bonds whose principal adjusts with CPI. The yield on TIPS measures the inflation-adjusted rate of return   i.e., the real interest rate.

TIPS breakeven: the difference between nominal Treasury yield and TIPS yield of the same maturity. Measures market inflation expectations. Used alongside TIPS yield to decompose the real rate into its nominal rate and inflation expectation components.

Z-score: a measure of how many standard deviations a current observation is from its historical mean. Used for XAUUSDT to measure how far gold has deviated from its TIPS-yield-implied fair value.

## Physical Market Terms

Backwardation: market condition where the spot (nearby) price is higher than the forward (deferred) price. In copper markets, indicates physical tightness   buyers are paying a premium for immediate delivery. Stage 3 of the LME inventory cycle.

Contango: the opposite of backwardation. Spot price below forward price. Normal carry structure in markets with storage costs. Stage 1 of the LME inventory cycle.

Cancelled warrants: LME warehouse warrants that have been cancelled, indicating an intent to withdraw metal from warehouse. Rising cancelled warrants are an early sign of physical tightness before actual inventory drawdown is visible.
