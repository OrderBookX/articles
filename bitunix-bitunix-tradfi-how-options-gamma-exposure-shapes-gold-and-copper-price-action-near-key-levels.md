## What Gamma Exposure Means for Price Action

Options market makers who sell calls or puts must delta-hedge their books to remain directionally neutral. As the underlying price approaches the strike price of large open interest positions, the delta of those options changes rapidly (this rapid delta change is gamma). Market makers must buy more underlying as price rises toward a large call strike (their delta hedge requires it), creating upward price pressure near that level. This buying support near large call strikes is why round-number price levels often act as temporary magnets.

Once price breaks through a large gamma level (above a call-heavy strike or below a put-heavy strike), the market maker hedging flips direction: they must now sell what they were buying (or buy what they were selling), creating acceleration in the breakout direction. This is why commodity futures breakouts through round numbers that align with large options open interest often accelerate sharply rather than grinding through slowly.
