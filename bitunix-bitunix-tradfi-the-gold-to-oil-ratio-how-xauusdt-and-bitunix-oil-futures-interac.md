## The Historical Range and What Extremes Signal

The gold-oil ratio has historically ranged from approximately 6:1 (one ounce of gold buys only 6 barrels of oil, when oil is extremely expensive) to approximately 40:1 (one ounce of gold buys 40 barrels, when oil is extremely cheap or gold is extremely expensive).

The 6:1 extreme (oil very expensive relative to gold): historically during acute oil supply shock events   the 1980 peak after the Iranian revolution, the 2008 spike. At this extreme, oil's geopolitical risk premium has been maximally inflated and the ratio typically mean-reverts as either oil falls or gold rises.

The 40:1 extreme (gold very expensive relative to oil, or oil very cheap): historically during deflationary demand collapse   COVID 2020 (oil crashed when demand collapsed), early 1999 (oil at $10, gold at $250), the 2015-2016 oil supply glut. At this extreme, oil is at a historically undervalued level relative to gold and the ratio typically reverts as either oil recovers or gold falls.
