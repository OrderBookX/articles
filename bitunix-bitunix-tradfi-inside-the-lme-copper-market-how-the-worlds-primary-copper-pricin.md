## The LME's Unique Prompt Date System

The LME does not use the quarterly contract structure common in CME futures. Instead, the LME trades copper for delivery on any business day within the next three months (daily prompt dates), and then specific weekly and monthly dates out to 63 months forward. This daily prompt date structure creates a specific advantage for industrial users who need copper on exact dates for manufacturing schedules.

The most liquid and most referenced contract is the 3-month forward (LME Cash + 3 months). When traders and media quote "copper at $9,000 per tonne," they are typically quoting the LME 3-month forward price. This is the primary COPPERUSDT reference.

## The Open Outcry Ring: The Last of Its Kind

The LME maintains a physical open outcry trading ring   the last functioning open outcry commodity ring in the world   where official prices for each metal are set by a verbal auction process during specific 5-minute trading sessions each morning. These Ring sessions occur from approximately 11:40 AM to 1:00 PM London time, with each metal getting multiple 5-minute sessions.

The official LME Ring prices set during these sessions are the legally binding benchmark used in physical contracts globally. They differ from the electronic LME Select platform prices that trade continuously. Discrepancies between Ring official prices and LME Select electronic prices indicate supply-demand imbalances in the physical delivery market versus the electronic market.
