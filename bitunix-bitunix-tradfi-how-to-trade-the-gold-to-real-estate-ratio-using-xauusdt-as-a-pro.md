## The Historical Gold-Real Estate Relationship

In the United States, the Case-Shiller Home Price Index relative to gold (dividing the home price index by the gold price per ounce) shows how many ounces of gold are required to purchase the average US home. Historical analysis: in the 1980 gold peak, an average US home could be purchased for approximately 100 ounces of gold (gold was very expensive relative to houses). By 2001, an average US home required approximately 500-600 ounces (gold was very cheap relative to houses). By 2011, the ratio had returned to approximately 150-200 ounces. By 2024, with gold at record highs but home prices also elevated, the ratio was approximately 250-300 ounces.

The ratio's analytical value: when the gold-to-home ratio is below 150 ounces (gold cheap relative to real estate), historical episodes have been followed by gold outperformance. When above 400-500 ounces (gold expensive relative to real estate), gold has tended to underperform real assets.
