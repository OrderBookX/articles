## The Fed's 2020 Framework Shift and Its XAUUSDT Implications

In August 2020, the Federal Reserve adopted Average Inflation Targeting (AIT): instead of targeting 2% inflation at all times, the Fed committed to allowing inflation to run modestly above 2% for a period after inflation had been below 2% for some time. The explicit goal: achieve a 2% average over time rather than a 2% target at each moment.

The XAUUSDT implication of AIT: under AIT, the Fed would be slower to raise rates in response to above-2% inflation than under the previous symmetric targeting framework. Slower rate response = more prolonged negative real rates during any inflation surge = more sustained XAUUSDT bull environment.

This framework shift directly contributed to the Fed's 2021 decision to characterize inflation as "transitory" and delay rate hikes   which sustained the 2020-2021 XAUUSDT bull market approximately 12-18 months longer than the prior framework would have permitted.
