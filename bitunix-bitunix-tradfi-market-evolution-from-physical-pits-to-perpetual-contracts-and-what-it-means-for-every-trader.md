## From Trading Pits to Electronic Access

The COMEX trading floor in New York, where gold futures were once traded by physical presence in the pit, required traders to be physically present in New York, registered with the exchange, and backed by clearing member capital. The transition to electronic trading in the 1990s-2000s removed the geographic and physical presence barrier but maintained the institutional requirements: regulated brokerage account, minimum deposits, US banking relationships.

These requirements effectively excluded approximately 95% of potential global participants from direct commodity futures market access. A retail trader in Jakarta, Lagos, or São Paulo who wanted gold futures exposure had to navigate correspondent banking, regulated brokerage accounts, and minimum deposit requirements that put direct market access out of practical reach.
