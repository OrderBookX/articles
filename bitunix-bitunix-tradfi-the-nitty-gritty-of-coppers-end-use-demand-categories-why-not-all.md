## The Five End-Use Sectors and Their Specific Drivers

Sector 1   Power Infrastructure (approximately 45% of Chinese copper demand): transmission lines, transformers, substations, EV charging infrastructure. This is the largest single copper-consuming sector and is driven by government capital expenditure on power grid modernization and renewable energy connection. The State Grid Corporation of China (SGCC) annual investment plan, published each January, is the primary forward-looking signal for this sector. When SGCC announces above-budget investment (frequently occurs when the government is using infrastructure investment as economic stimulus), power infrastructure copper demand receives an immediate forward catalyst.

Sector 2   Real Estate and Construction (approximately 25% of Chinese copper demand): electrical wiring in new residential and commercial buildings, HVAC systems, plumbing. This sector has been the most significant variable in Chinese copper demand over 2021-2024 due to the Evergrande crisis and Chinese property market contraction. Property starts, completions, and floor area sold are the monitoring inputs.

Sector 3   Appliances and Consumer Electronics (approximately 15%): air conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, smartphones. Driven by Chinese domestic consumption and export orders. Tracked through CAAM (China Association of Automobile Manufacturers) for air conditioner compressor data and CIPA for consumer electronics.

Sector 4   Transportation including EVs (approximately 10%): automotive wiring harnesses, EV batteries and motors. Growing rapidly. Tracked through CAAM monthly vehicle production.

## The Sector Composition Signal

When PMI is rising but the composition shows the recovery is in consumer electronics and appliances (lighter copper content) rather than power infrastructure and construction (heavy copper content per unit of economic activity), the copper demand implied by the PMI headline is weaker than the headline suggests.

Conversely, when SGCC has just announced above-budget investment AND property completions are running above starts (existing projects being completed while new starts lag), the copper demand from project completions can exceed what property sector pessimism implies.
