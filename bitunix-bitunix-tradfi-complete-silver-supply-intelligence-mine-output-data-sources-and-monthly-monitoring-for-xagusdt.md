## Silver's Byproduct Supply Structure

Approximately 70-75% of silver mine supply comes as a byproduct of mining other primary metals: 35-40% from lead-zinc mines, 20-25% from copper mines, 10-15% from gold mines. Only 25-30% of silver comes from primary silver mines where silver is the primary product.

This byproduct structure means that silver supply is partially driven by lead-zinc demand, copper demand, and gold demand rather than by silver price alone. When copper mining expands to meet electrification demand, silver supply increases as a byproduct even if silver prices are flat.
