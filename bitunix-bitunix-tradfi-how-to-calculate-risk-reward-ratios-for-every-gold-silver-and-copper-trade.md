## What Is Risk-Reward Ratio and Why It Determines Long-Term Profitability

Risk-reward ratio compares the potential loss on a trade (from entry to stop-loss) against the potential gain (from entry to target). A 1:3 risk-reward ratio means risking $1 to make $3. At this ratio, you only need to be right 25% of the time to break even. A 1:1 ratio requires 50% accuracy to break even. Understanding this math reveals why most retail traders lose despite being directionally correct more than half the time: they take trades with poor risk-reward ratios that require 60-70% win rates to generate profit.
