## The Math Behind Frequency

Weekly DCA produces 52 purchase points per year. Monthly DCA produces 12. More data points mean tighter averaging because your purchases capture more price variation throughout the month. In a volatile asset like Bitcoin that can swing 15% in a week, those additional purchase points smooth your average cost more effectively. However, the mathematical difference between weekly and monthly DCA over a full year is typically small. Studies across multiple Bitcoin cycles show the difference in final average cost between weekly and monthly DCA is usually within 2% to 5%.

## When Weekly Makes Sense

Choose weekly if you have consistent weekly cash flow, if you prefer the psychological comfort of frequent small purchases, or if you are accumulating during a period of high volatility where price swings are significant within each month. Weekly also works better for smaller budgets. Instead of one $200 monthly purchase, four $50 weekly purchases feel more manageable and produce a slightly better average cost.

## When Monthly Makes Sense

Choose monthly if you prefer simplicity, if your income arrives monthly, or if you are accumulating large-cap assets in relatively stable conditions. Monthly is also easier to track and review. Twelve line items per year versus 52 makes performance evaluation simpler. For Bitcoin specifically, monthly DCA has produced strong historical results across every complete market cycle. The additional precision of weekly buying adds only marginal benefit.

## The Frequency That Actually Matters

The honest truth is that the difference between weekly and monthly matters far less than the difference between doing DCA and not doing DCA. An investor who commits to monthly purchases for three years will massively outperform someone who debates weekly versus monthly for six months and never starts. Analysis paralysis is the real enemy of returns.
