## What to Record for Every BAT Trade

Each journal entry should capture: date and time of entry and exit, direction (long or short for futures, buy or sell for spot), entry price and exit price, position size and leverage used, the reason for entering the trade based on your analysis, stop-loss and take-profit levels, the actual outcome in both absolute and percentage terms, and your emotional state during the trade.

The emotional state component is often the most revealing. Trades entered during fear, greed, boredom, or revenge consistently underperform trades entered during calm, analytical states. Tracking this correlation gives you concrete evidence that emotional discipline directly impacts your BAT coin returns.

## Analyzing Your BAT crypto Performance Data

After accumulating 20 to 30 journal entries, patterns emerge. Calculate your win rate, average winner size, average loser size, and the ratio between them. A profitable system can have a win rate below 50 percent if average winners are significantly larger than average losers. Conversely, a high win rate means nothing if occasional large losses wipe out accumulated gains.

Segment your BAT results by strategy type, time of day, market condition, and order type. These segments reveal where your edge lives and where your weaknesses concentrate. Double down on what works. Eliminate or reduce what does not.
