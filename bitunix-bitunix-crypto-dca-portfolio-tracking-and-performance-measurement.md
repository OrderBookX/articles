## Key Metrics to Track

Focus on four core metrics. Total units accumulated per asset. This is the most important number because it represents your actual holdings independent of current market price. Average cost per unit. Calculate this by dividing total invested by total units held. It tells you the breakeven price for each asset. Current portfolio value. The market value of all accumulated holdings at today's prices. Total return percentage. The difference between current value and total invested, expressed as a percentage.

## Benchmark Comparisons

Compare your DCA returns against two benchmarks. First, the asset's price performance over the same period. If Bitcoin went from $50,000 to $80,000 during your DCA period, how does your average cost compare to a lump-sum buy at $50,000? Second, compare against what you would have realistically done without automation. Would you have invested at all during the scary months? Probably not, and that comparison usually favors your automated plan significantly.

## Avoiding Common Measurement Errors

Do not evaluate performance during the first six months. The sample size is too small for meaningful conclusions. Do not compare your returns to cherry-picked entry points. Anyone can identify a perfect entry in hindsight. Do not compare your Bitcoin DCA returns to a meme coin that did 50x. Compare like strategies to like strategies.

## Using Data to Inform Adjustments

Performance data should drive quarterly allocation reviews. If one asset consistently underperforms your thesis expectations, investigate whether the thesis has changed. If your average cost is tracking well below current price, your plan is working as designed. If an altcoin in your plan has declined 80% with deteriorating fundamentals, the data supports removing it. Let numbers, not emotions, guide your adjustments.
