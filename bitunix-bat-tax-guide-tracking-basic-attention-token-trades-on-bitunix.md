## Why Crypto Tax Tracking Matters for BAT coin

In most countries, cryptocurrency is treated as property for tax purposes. This means buying, selling, trading, swapping, and earning BAT through staking all create potential taxable events. The specific rules vary by jurisdiction, but the common thread is that accurate records of every transaction are essential.

Capital gains tax applies when you sell BAT crypto for more than you paid. If you bought Basic Attention Token at 1.00 USDT and sold at 1.50 USDT, the 0.50 USDT gain is typically taxable. The rate depends on how long you held the asset and your local tax bracket.

Staking rewards through Bitunix Earn may be treated as income in many jurisdictions, taxable at the fair market value when received. Swaps through Convert create taxable events because they are treated as selling one asset and buying another, even though no fiat currency was involved.

## Cost Basis Methods for BAT crypto

Your cost basis is what you paid for each BAT token, and it determines your taxable gain or loss when you sell. Several methods exist for calculating cost basis: First In First Out (FIFO), Last In First Out (LIFO), and Specific Identification. Different jurisdictions allow different methods, and the choice can significantly affect your tax liability.

If you use Bitunix HODL for dollar-cost averaging into Basic Attention Token, each recurring purchase creates a separate tax lot with its own cost basis. Tracking each lot individually provides the most accurate tax calculations and the flexibility to choose the most tax-efficient lots when selling.
