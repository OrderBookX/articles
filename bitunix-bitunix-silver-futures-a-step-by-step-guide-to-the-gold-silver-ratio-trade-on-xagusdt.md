## Understanding the Ratio: What Number Matters?

The gold-silver ratio is simply the gold price divided by the silver price. If gold is at $2,000 per ounce and silver is at $25 per ounce, the ratio is 80:1. The historical average is approximately 60-70:1. When the ratio rises above 90:1, silver is historically cheap relative to gold. When it falls below 50:1, silver is historically expensive relative to gold.

Current ratio levels can be checked instantly by dividing the XAUUSDT price by the XAGUSDT price on the bitunix exchange at any moment.

## Step 1: Identify the Ratio Entry Signal

Check the current gold-silver ratio. If the ratio is above 90:1, you have an entry signal for the long XAGUSDT / short XAUUSDT spread. If it is below 50:1, you have an entry signal for the short XAGUSDT / long XAUUSDT spread.

The further the ratio is from its historical average, the stronger the mean reversion case. A ratio of 100:1 provides a stronger entry signal than a ratio of 90:1.

## Step 3: Set the Exit Levels

Define two exit conditions: the target exit when the ratio reverts toward historical average, and the stop-loss exit if the ratio continues moving against the position.

Target exit: When the ratio reaches 65-70:1 (from an entry at 90:1+), close both legs and take profits.

Stop-loss exit: If the ratio moves beyond 110:1 from a 90:1 entry, something structurally different is happening and the mean reversion assumption may be wrong. Close both legs and reassess.

## Step 4: Monitor Funding Rates on Both Legs

If shorts are receiving on the XAUUSDT leg (positive funding rate), this partially offsets the cost. Net funding across both legs affects the daily carrying cost of the spread position.
