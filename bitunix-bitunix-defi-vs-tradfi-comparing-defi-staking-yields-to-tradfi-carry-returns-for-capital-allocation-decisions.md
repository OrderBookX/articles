## DeFi Staking Yield Characteristics

DeFi staking yield comes from securing proof-of-stake blockchains or from liquidity provision in DeFi protocols. Ethereum staking yields approximately 3-5% annually in ETH terms. Liquid staking derivatives (Lido stETH, Rocket Pool rETH) provide similar yields with added liquidity. Protocol liquidity provision yields are higher but carry impermanent loss risk and smart contract risk.

DeFi staking yield is denominated in the staked asset (ETH, not USDT). If ETH price falls 20% while you earn 4% ETH staking yield, the USDT-denominated return is -16% (before considering compounding). The staking yield is positive in ETH terms but does not protect against ETH price risk.

## The Yield Comparison Framework

Comparing DeFi staking yield to TradFi carry requires adjusting for risk:

Risk-adjusted DeFi yield: ETH staking yield (3-5% annual) minus expected ETH price loss during bear markets (can be -50% or more). In bull markets, staking yield plus price appreciation creates very high total returns; in bear markets, staking yield is insufficient to offset price declines.

Risk-adjusted TradFi carry: Funding rate collection yield minus expected stop-loss loss frequency. A 1.5% monthly funding yield collected for three months before a stop-loss loss of 1% of account creates a positive risk-adjusted return if the strategy is sized correctly.
