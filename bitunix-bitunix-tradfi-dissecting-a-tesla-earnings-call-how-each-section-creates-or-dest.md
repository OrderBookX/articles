## Section 1: The Delivery and Production Numbers (Almost Always Pre-Priced)

Tesla publishes its vehicle delivery and production numbers 2-3 weeks before the full earnings call, in a standalone delivery report. By the time the earnings call discusses deliveries, the market has had 2-3 weeks to process the delivery data. Barring significant accounting adjustments or ASP (average selling price) surprises, the delivery discussion adds limited incremental price information.

The delivery report itself, when published, creates the initial TSLAUSDT reaction. If deliveries beat consensus by 5%+, TSLAUSDT typically rises 5-10% on the delivery report day. If it misses by 5%+, TSLAUSDT falls 5-10%. By earnings call day, this initial reaction has largely been fully processed.

## Section 2: The Gross Margin Discussion (The Most Market-Moving Earnings Call Section)

The most consistently market-moving part of any Tesla earnings call is the gross margin discussion, specifically the automotive gross margin excluding regulatory credits. This metric tells practitioners how much Tesla earns per car before accounting for one-time items.

A gross margin above 20% signals Tesla's pricing power and cost efficiency are healthy   supporting the base business valuation that underlies even the most optimistic autonomous premium scenario. A gross margin below 17% signals either pricing pressure (Tesla cut prices to maintain volume at the expense of profitability) or cost creep that is eroding the business fundamentals.
