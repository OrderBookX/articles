## The Two-Speed Drawdown Response System

The two-speed system distinguishes between ordinary adverse moves (thesis still intact, stop not triggered) and extraordinary adverse moves (that warrant position reduction even before the formal stop-loss is hit).

Speed 1   Ordinary adverse move (less than 50% of stop distance): No action required. The position is within normal expected variance. Continue holding with original thesis and stop parameters. Weekly review confirms thesis driver validity.

Speed 2   Extraordinary adverse move (more than 50% of stop distance without thesis invalidation): Optional tactical reduction of position size by 30-40% to reduce risk. The thesis has not been invalidated but the position is close enough to the stop that the risk-reward of continuing at full size has deteriorated. Reducing size preserves capital if the stop is hit while maintaining exposure if the position recovers.

This two-speed response eliminates the common psychological trap of holding full size on a position that is approaching its stop while mentally hoping for a recovery   a hope that often turns into a large loss when the stop is eventually hit.
