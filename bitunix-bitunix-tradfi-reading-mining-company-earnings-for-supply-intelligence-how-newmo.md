## What to Look For in a Gold Mining Earnings Report

The four highest-signal items in any major gold miner's quarterly earnings call:

All-in Sustaining Cost (AISC) guidance: This is the total cost per ounce to mine, including sustaining capital, royalties, and corporate overhead. When AISC is rising year-over-year (driven by energy costs, labor, or ore grade decline), profit margins are compressing and the company may reduce future development spending   a multi-year supply constraint signal.

Mine life and reserve update: If a miner's proven and probable reserve estimate falls year-over-year without significant new exploration additions, the mine's life is shortening. Multiple major miners reporting declining reserves simultaneously signals a structural supply constraint.

Capital expenditure guidance: Current-year sustaining CapEx maintains existing production; growth CapEx builds new production 5-10 years in the future. When aggregate growth CapEx across the top 10 gold miners is declining, the 2030+ supply pipeline is thinning.

Production guidance changes: Mid-year downward production guidance revisions   particularly when attributed to operational issues like equipment failures, ore grade shortfalls, or permitting delays   create immediate supply surprise signals for XAUUSDT.

## What to Look For in a Copper Mining Earnings Report

BHP, Rio Tinto, Freeport-McMoRan, and Antofagasta are the primary free information sources for copper supply intelligence. Key metrics:

Copper cathode equivalent production vs. guidance: When actual quarterly production falls more than 3% below guidance, operational challenges are developing. Multiple consecutive quarters of production misses signal systematic mine degradation.

C1 cash costs (direct cash costs per pound): When C1 costs are rising above $2.50/lb for the industry aggregate, economic incentive for new mine development at current prices is diminishing.

Expansion project milestones: Specific large expansion projects (Escondida expansion, Kamoa-Kakula Phase 3, QB2 ramp-up) have specific production start dates that represent step-changes in copper supply. Delays to these milestone dates push out supply growth timelines and are bullish for near-term COPPERUSDT.
