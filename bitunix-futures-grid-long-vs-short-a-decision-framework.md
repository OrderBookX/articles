# Futures Grid Long vs Short — A Decision Framework

Most grid trading guides tell you "go long if bullish, go short if bearish." That's not wrong, but it's not useful either. Here's a structured decision framework that gives you a concrete process for choosing grid direction.

## The Five-Step Framework

Step one: identify the prevailing trend on the daily chart. Is price making higher highs and higher lows (uptrend), lower highs and lower lows (downtrend), or neither (range)? For an uptrend, long grids have a structural advantage. For a downtrend, short grids do. For a pure range, either direction works — but your range boundaries become more important than direction.

## Step 2: Moving Average Position

Step two: check where price sits relative to key moving averages. If BTC is above the 50-day and 200-day moving averages, the market structure supports long positioning. If below both, short positioning is more aligned. If between them, conditions are mixed and you should be more cautious with leverage and range width.

## Step 3: Funding Rate Signal

Step three: examine the funding rate. Persistently positive funding means the market is net long — there's bullish consensus. This supports long grids but also means you'll pay funding costs. Persistently negative funding suggests bearish sentiment, supporting short grids with the bonus of earning funding payments. Neutral or fluctuating funding provides no directional signal.

### Step 4: Volume Distribution

Step four: assess volume distribution. If buy volume is concentrated at support and sell volume at resistance, the range is healthy and either direction works. If sell volume is increasing at every bounce, the range may break downward — favoring short. If buy volume is increasing at every dip, it may break upward — favoring long.

### Step 5: Macro Context

Step five: consider the macro context. Is there a Fed meeting this week? An ETF decision pending? A major protocol upgrade? These catalysts can override any technical setup. If a high-impact event is imminent, either delay deployment or use very conservative parameters.

A final note on this framework: it improves with practice. The first time you run through these five steps, it might take an hour. After ten iterations, it takes ten minutes. And the quality of your decisions improves with each cycle because you're building pattern recognition from real outcomes. Keep a brief log of your directional decisions and their results — over time, this becomes your most valuable trading asset.
