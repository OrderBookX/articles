# Futures Grid Bot — A Risk Manager's Honest Assessment

Grid bots are often marketed as "passive income machines." That framing is dangerous. Here's an honest assessment of what a futures grid bot actually is, what risks it carries, and how to think about it from a risk management perspective.

## What It Actually Is

A futures grid bot is a rules-based execution tool. It follows your configuration exactly — no more, no less. It buys at predefined levels, sells at predefined levels, and pauses when price exits the range. It doesn't adapt to changing conditions. It doesn't "learn." It doesn't have an opinion on the market. It's a sophisticated limit order system, not artificial intelligence.

## The Real Risks

### Leverage Risk
Every grid level adds to your total position. At 10x leverage with 25 grids, full one-sided accumulation creates massive exposure. Liquidation can happen faster than most traders expect.

### Range Risk
If price exits your range permanently — a new trend establishes — the bot holds a losing position indefinitely until you intervene or SL triggers.

### Complacency Risk
The biggest hidden risk. Automation creates a false sense of safety. "The bot is handling it" becomes an excuse not to monitor. Markets change; bots don't adapt without human input.
