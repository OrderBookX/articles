## Breakeven Inflation Rate as Supplementary Signal

Alongside the TIPS yield, the breakeven inflation rate, derived by subtracting the TIPS yield from the nominal Treasury yield of the same maturity, provides a market-based measure of inflation expectations. Rising breakeven rates indicate that the market is expecting higher future inflation, which adds a monetary demand component to precious metals in addition to the real rate effect. When both real rates are falling and breakeven inflation is rising simultaneously, the combined precious metals signal is particularly strong for bitunix gold futures and bitunix silver futures positioning.
