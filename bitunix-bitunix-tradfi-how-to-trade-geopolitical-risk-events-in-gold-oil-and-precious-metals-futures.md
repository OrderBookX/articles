## The Geopolitical Risk Hierarchy: Which Events Move Which Markets

Not all geopolitical events move commodity markets equally. The hierarchy for market impact:

Tier 1 (immediate 5-15% price moves): Major armed conflict escalation involving oil-producing regions (Middle East), direct military action affecting Russian commodity exports, nuclear risk scenarios, global financial system threat events.

Tier 2 (immediate 2-5% price moves): New economic sanctions on major commodity producers, large-scale cyberattacks on critical infrastructure, election results in major commodity-producing countries with policy implications.

Tier 3 (gradual 1-3% price moves over days): Trade policy changes between major economies, monetary policy shifts in large emerging market economies, political instability in secondary commodity producers.

The analytical preparation required before each tier differs: Tier 1 events require immediate reaction capability; Tier 2 events allow hours of preparation; Tier 3 events allow days of positioning.
