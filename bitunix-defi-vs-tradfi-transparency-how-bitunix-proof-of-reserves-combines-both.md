## TradFi Transparency Model

Traditional financial transparency relies on periodic reporting. Banks publish quarterly financials. Public companies file annual reports. Regulators conduct examinations. Auditing firms verify balance sheets. This model provides institutional-grade accountability but operates on delayed timelines. A bank's quarterly report tells you the state of affairs three months ago, not today. Between reporting periods, users must trust that conditions have not deteriorated.

## DeFi Transparency Model

DeFi transparency is real-time and permissionless. Every transaction on a public blockchain is visible to anyone. Smart contract code is auditable. Total value locked is verifiable at any moment. However, this raw data can be misleading without context. High TVL does not guarantee solvency. On-chain transactions do not reveal off-chain obligations. And smart contract transparency does not prevent economic exploits or governance manipulation.
