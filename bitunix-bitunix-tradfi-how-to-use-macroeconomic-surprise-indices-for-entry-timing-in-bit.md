## The Citi Economic Surprise Index (CESI) and Commodity Timing

The Citi Economic Surprise Index (CESI) is freely available through Bloomberg's free tier and summarized weekly through financial news. It measures the sum of positive minus negative economic data surprises weighted by the importance of each data release, with recent surprises weighted more heavily than older ones.

CESI above zero: recent economic data is systematically exceeding analyst forecasts. The economy is performing better than the consensus expected. This is typically commodity-demand-positive   improving conditions that analysts have not yet fully priced into commodity price targets.

CESI below zero: recent data is systematically disappointing. Commodity demand conditions are deteriorating faster than analyst forecasts assumed.
