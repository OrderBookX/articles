## The Problem with Manual Trading

Manual futures trading requires constant presence. You watch for setups, enter positions, manage them tick by tick, and exit. Most of the actual profit comes from range-bound conditions — buying support, selling resistance — over and over. The directional trades get the glory, but the range trades pay the bills.

The irony is that range trading is the most automatable part. The entries are predefined (support/resistance levels). The exits are predefined (the opposite level). The only decision is whether the range is valid. A bot can execute the repetitive part better than any human because it doesn't hesitate, doesn't get distracted, and doesn't fat-finger orders.
