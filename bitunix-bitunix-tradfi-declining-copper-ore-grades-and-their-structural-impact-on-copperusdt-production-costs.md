## The Ore Grade Decline Mechanics

In 1900, average copper ore grades were approximately 4-5% copper by weight (meaning 4-5 kg of copper per 100 kg of ore). Today, the global average copper ore grade has fallen to approximately 0.6-0.7% copper. This represents a dramatic reduction in the amount of copper extracted per tonne of ore processed.

The practical implication: to produce the same quantity of refined copper, modern mines must mine, crush, and process six to eight times more ore than mines operating a century ago. Each tonne of additional ore processed requires diesel fuel, water, labor, electricity, and capital equipment, all of which have their own cost trajectories. As grades continue declining, the energy and water intensity of copper production rises, creating structural cost inflation that is independent of short-term commodity price cycles.
