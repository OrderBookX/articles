## The Asymmetric Damage of Early Stops

When you stop a DCA plan during a downturn, you lock in a high average cost. All your purchases were made at higher prices, and you missed the opportunity to bring your average down by continuing through the cheaper prices ahead. If and when the market recovers, you do not benefit from the lower-cost units you would have accumulated. The plan that was designed to capitalize on volatility instead captured only the expensive side of it.

## Historical Cost of Stopping

Consider an investor who started weekly BTC DCA in November 2021 near the cycle peak and stopped in June 2022 during the crash. If they had continued through December 2022, their average cost would have dropped significantly as they bought BTC below $20,000. By the time BTC recovered above $60,000 in 2024, the investor who continued had a dramatically better return than the one who stopped during the fear.
