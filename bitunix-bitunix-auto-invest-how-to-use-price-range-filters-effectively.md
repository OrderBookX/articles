## What the Price Range Filter Does

When you set a price range, you define a minimum and maximum price for execution. If the asset price is within your range at the scheduled time, the purchase executes normally. If the price is above your maximum or below your minimum, the plan skips that cycle. The capital stays in your account, and the plan resumes checking at the next scheduled interval.

## When to Use a Maximum Price Filter

A maximum price filter prevents purchases during extreme spikes. If Bitcoin typically trades between $80,000 and $110,000 and suddenly spikes to $130,000 during a short squeeze, your filter can skip that overheated purchase. You avoid buying at a statistical outlier price that is likely to mean-revert. The trade-off is that if the spike represents a genuine breakout to a new trading range, you miss accumulation at what turns out to be a reasonable price in hindsight. This is why the maximum should be set generously above the current range rather than tightly.

## When to Use a Minimum Price Filter

A minimum price filter is less common but can serve as a basic risk management tool. If you are DCA into an altcoin and it drops below a level that suggests fundamental problems, a minimum price filter can pause purchases automatically. This gives you time to reassess whether the decline is a buying opportunity or a warning signal.

## Setting Appropriate Ranges

The key is to set ranges that filter out genuine outliers without interfering with normal market fluctuation. A practical approach is to set your maximum at 30% to 50% above the current range high and your minimum at 50% to 70% below the current range low. This catches extreme events while allowing normal volatility to execute uninterrupted.

## When Not to Use Filters

If your investment horizon is 5 years or longer and you are DCA into Bitcoin, price filters add complexity without meaningful benefit. The difference between buying at $95,000 and skipping a buy at $105,000 is negligible over a multi-year accumulation period. Filters work best for shorter time horizons or altcoins with more volatile and less predictable price behavior. For long-horizon Bitcoin accumulation, the purest approach is unfiltered DCA that buys at every interval regardless of price conditions.
