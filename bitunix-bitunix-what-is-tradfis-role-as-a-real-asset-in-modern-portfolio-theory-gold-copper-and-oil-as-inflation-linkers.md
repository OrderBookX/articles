## What Is TradFi's Real Asset Definition?

What is TradFi's real asset classification criteria? Real assets have three defining characteristics: physical substance (gold bars, copper metal, oil barrels exist independently of financial systems), intrinsic productive or monetary value (copper is consumed in manufacturing, gold is a monetary store), and positive correlation to inflation over time (as prices rise, the dollar value of real assets tends to rise proportionally or more).

The positive inflation correlation of real assets is the most important characteristic for portfolio construction purposes. When inflation erodes the purchasing power of cash and nominal bonds, real assets appreciate in nominal terms, preserving and often increasing real purchasing power. This makes real assets the natural complement to financial assets (stocks, bonds) in a complete portfolio.
