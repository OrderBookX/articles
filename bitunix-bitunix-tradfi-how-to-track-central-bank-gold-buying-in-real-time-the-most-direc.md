## The Data Sources for Central Bank Gold Buying

World Gold Council monthly ETF and Official Sector Statistics (free at gold.org/gold-data): publishes monthly estimates of central bank net purchases and sales globally. Released approximately 4-6 weeks after the reporting month. This is the primary free source for official sector gold demand.

IMF International Financial Statistics (IFS): all IMF member countries are required to report their official gold reserves monthly (free at imf.org/en/Data). The monthly IFS update provides the most comprehensive coverage, including emerging market central banks that are the primary buyers in recent years.

Individual central bank press releases: the People's Bank of China (PBoC), Reserve Bank of India (RBI), Central Bank of Turkey, and National Bank of Poland each publish monthly reserve data on their official websites. These are the largest and most active buyers   monitoring their individual monthly reports creates leading intelligence before the WGC aggregates the data.

Third week: WGC monthly ETF and official sector update typically arrives. Review the aggregate net purchase/sale figure. Compare to the prior three-month average   is the buying pace accelerating, decelerating, or steady?
