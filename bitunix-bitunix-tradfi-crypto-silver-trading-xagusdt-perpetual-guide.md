## Understanding Silver Market Dynamics

Silver price movements are influenced by two overlapping sets of factors. On the monetary side, silver follows gold during macro-driven risk-off events, rising when inflation expectations increase and when the US dollar weakens. On the industrial side, silver demand increases during manufacturing expansion, particularly when solar panel production ramps up or electronics demand surges. This dual demand structure makes silver more volatile than gold, with sharper rallies during favorable conditions and deeper corrections during downturns.

## Silver vs Gold: Choosing Your TradFi Exposure

Gold provides stability and consistent safe-haven response during macro stress. Silver offers the same directional tendency but with higher beta, meaning larger moves in both directions. During a gold rally of 5 percent, silver might move 8 to 12 percent in the same direction. This amplification makes silver attractive for traders who want more aggressive TradFi exposure but also requires tighter risk management. Position sizing for silver should typically be smaller than gold to account for the higher volatility.
