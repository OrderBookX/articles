**Mentor:** The "leading indicator" property is real but context-dependent. **Student:** So when does the leading indicator property work best?

**Mentor:** When China is the dominant driver, the signal is clearest. China consumes 55% of global copper. ## Understanding the Supply Side

**Student:** I understand demand monitoring. But how do I think about supply?

**Mentor:** Start with mine production concentration. Chile provides 25-28% of global copper mine supply. Peru provides 10-12%. Together, two countries supply 35-40% of the world's copper. Any supply disruption in Chile or Peru is immediately significant at a global level.

**Student:** What kind of disruptions?

**Mentor:** Three categories. Labor: copper mining is heavily unionized in both countries. Contract negotiations occur every 2-4 years. Strike threats and actual strikes at major mines like Escondida in Chile can remove 50,000-100,000 tonnes of copper from the market within weeks. Second, weather: La Nina events reduce water availability in Chile's Atacama mining region, forcing production cuts. Third, political: both Chile and Peru have had periods of resource nationalism pressure on mining companies.

## The Inventory Signal Conversation

**Student:** I've been told to watch LME copper inventories. What am I actually looking for?

**Mentor:** The direction and rate of change, not the absolute level. LME copper inventory falling for 4+ consecutive weeks while Chinese physical premiums are positive means the physical market is drawing down the buffer   a bullish setup. LME inventory rising for 4+ consecutive weeks while Shanghai premiums are negative means the market is rebuilding the buffer from oversupply   a bearish setup.

**Student:** What's the most important thing I'm missing right now?

**Mentor:** Probably the distinction between LME warranted stocks and cancelled warrants. When copper inventory is "cancelled"   earmarked for withdrawal from the warehouse   it appears in official statistics but has effectively already been sold. A high percentage of cancelled warrants in LME inventory creates the appearance of adequate supply while the actual available supply is much tighter.
