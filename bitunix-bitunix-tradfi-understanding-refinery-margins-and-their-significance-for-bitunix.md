## The Three-Crack Spread Methodology

The most commonly used refinery margin proxy is the 3-2-1 crack spread: the gain from converting 3 barrels of crude oil into 2 barrels of gasoline and 1 barrel of distillate (diesel/heating oil). The formula: (2 × RBOB gasoline price + 1 × ULSD price - 3 × WTI price) / 3.

When the 3-2-1 crack is above $20/barrel, US refinery margins are healthy   refineries are incentivized to maximize crude intake and processing. Below $10/barrel, margins are compressed and refineries reduce throughput, reducing crude demand.
