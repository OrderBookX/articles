## The Dollar Cycle Historical Framework

The US dollar has moved in long cycles historically, averaging 5-7 years per major directional phase. Major dollar bull markets: 1980-1985 (Reagan-era fiscal expansion and Fed tightening), 1995-2002 (dotcom boom capital inflows), 2011-2020 (post-GFC Fed strength and growth outperformance). Major dollar bear markets: 1985-1995, 2002-2008, and briefly 2020-2021.

The cycle's driving factors are monetary policy divergence (US rates higher than peers attracts capital inflows), growth outperformance (US GDP growth faster than major economies attracts equity capital inflows), and safe-haven flows (during global stress, dollar attracts risk-off demand).
