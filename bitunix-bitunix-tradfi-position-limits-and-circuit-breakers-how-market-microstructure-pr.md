## COMEX Position Limits for Gold and Silver

The CFTC imposes "spot month" and "all months combined" position limits on precious metals futures. For COMEX gold: the spot month (immediately deliverable) position limit is 3,000 contracts (300,000 ounces = approximately $600 million at $2,000/oz). The all-months limit is 6,000 contracts.

These limits are designed to prevent any single participant from cornering the physical delivery market   precisely the scenario the Hunt Brothers created in silver in 1980. When a large participant approaches position limits in the spot month, they must either deliver/take delivery or roll to the next month, preventing indefinite accumulation of near-term contracts.

The market signal from position limit approaches: when COMEX data shows the large trader category approaching concentration limits in the spot month (visible in the COT disaggregated report), a forced rolling operation by that participant creates predictable buying in the deferred month and selling in the spot month   a temporary supply of spot-month selling that can suppress spot prices briefly before the underlying trend resumes.
