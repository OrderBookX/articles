## The GVZ as a Fear Gauge for XAUUSDT

The CBOE GVZ measures the implied volatility of gold options   the market's consensus expectation of how much XAUUSDT will move over the next 30 days, expressed as an annualized percentage. When GVZ is low (below 12%), the options market expects calm, directional gold movement. When GVZ is high (above 20%), the options market expects large moves in either direction.

## Options Skew and What It Reveals About Directional Sentiment

Gold options skew measures the difference in implied volatility between out-of-the-money (OTM) put options and OTM call options at the same distance from current price. Positive skew (puts more expensive than calls) indicates the market is paying up to protect against sharp XAUUSDT declines   the options market is net bearish or fearful. Negative skew (calls more expensive than puts) indicates the market is pricing in upside breakout potential   institutions are paying for upside participation.
