Understanding Futures Trading in Cryptocurrency
-----------------------------------------------

Futures trading involves contracts that obligate the buyer to purchase, or the seller to sell, an asset at a predetermined future date and price. In the context of cryptocurrency, these contracts allow traders to speculate on the **price movements of digital assets** like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Key characteristics include:

- **Hedging:** Futures can be used to protect against adverse price movements, thus managing *risk* effectively.
- **Speculation:** Traders can profit from market volatility by predicting price trends.

### Types of Futures Contracts

In the crypto world, futures contracts can be classified into two main types:

- **Standard Futures:** Contracts with a fixed expiration date.
- **Perpetual Futures:** Also known as **perpetual contracts**, these do not have an expiration date and rely on *funding rates* to align prices with the spot market.
