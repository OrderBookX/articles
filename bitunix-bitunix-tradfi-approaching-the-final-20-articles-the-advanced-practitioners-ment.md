## The Three Categories of Practitioner Questions

Category 1   Pre-Trade Questions (Asked Before Every Entry):

"What specifically is the primary driver for this instrument, and which direction is it currently moving?" If the answer requires more than two sentences, the thesis is not yet clear enough.

"What is the specific, measurable condition that would indicate this thesis is wrong?" If this answer is "if the price falls significantly," the invalidation condition is not specific enough   it must reference the driver, not the price.

"At this entry price, stop-loss level, and target, what is the probability-weighted expected value?" If the expected value is below 1.5:1, the entry should be skipped.

"Am I entering this position because of a genuine analytical signal, or because I feel compelled to be active in the market?" Honest self-assessment before every entry.

Category 2   During-Position Questions (Asked at Every Weekly Review):

"Are the primary driver conditions that justified this entry still intact?" Yes = hold. No = exit within this session.

"Has the evidence count for this position's thesis increased, decreased, or stayed the same since entry?" Decreased evidence = reduce size.

"If I did not currently hold this position, would I enter it now at current prices?" If no, consider exiting.
