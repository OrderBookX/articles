## What Proof of Reserves Actually Proves

Proof of reserves is a cryptographic attestation that an exchange holds at least as much of each asset in its wallets as it claims to hold on behalf of customers. The technical process: the exchange publishes Merkle tree proofs showing the aggregate customer balance and their wallet holdings, then an independent auditor (typically a Big 4 accounting firm) verifies that the on-chain wallet balances equal or exceed the customer liabilities.

What proof of reserves proves: the exchange's on-chain wallet balances are genuine and at least cover customer liabilities at the time of the attestation.

What proof of reserves does not prove: that the exchange hasn't borrowed against those assets to fund other operations between attestation dates, or that liabilities have not changed since the last attestation. The attestation frequency matters   monthly attestations provide stronger assurance than annual ones.

## The MPC Custody Advantage: Why Fireblocks Matters

Multi-Party Computation (MPC) custody fundamentally changes the security architecture compared to traditional hot/cold wallet structures. In traditional custody, the private key to customer funds exists as a complete key that could be stolen, lost, or misused if obtained.

In Fireblocks MPC custody, no complete private key ever exists as a single object. The key is split into multiple shares held by different participants   typically the exchange, Fireblocks itself, and potentially a third party. Any transaction requires cooperation from at least two of the three parties, preventing unilateral misuse of customer funds.
