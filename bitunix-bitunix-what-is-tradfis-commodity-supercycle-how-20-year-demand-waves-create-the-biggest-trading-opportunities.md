## What Is TradFi's Commodity Supercycle History?

What is TradFi's documented commodity supercycle record? Economic historians have identified four modern commodity supercycles. The 1890s-1930s supercycle was driven by railroad expansion and early industrialization. The 1930s-1960s cycle reflected World War II infrastructure destruction and subsequent rebuilding. The 1960s-1990s supercycle tracked Japanese and European industrialization. The 1990s-2010s supercycle was the China-driven cycle: China's rapid industrialization consumed vast quantities of copper, steel, coal, and oil as hundreds of millions of people moved from agricultural to industrial employment.

Each supercycle created prolonged periods of elevated commodity prices that rewarded long-duration holders of commodity exposure handsomely.
