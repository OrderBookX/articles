## The CapEx Cycle and Mine Supply Timeline

A copper mining project follows a predictable timeline from exploration to production: exploration and resource definition (3-5 years) → feasibility study and permitting (3-7 years) → construction (3-5 years) → ramp-up to full production (1-2 years). Total: 10-20 years from initial exploration to full production at a new greenfield copper mine.

When copper prices are high, mining companies commit capital to new projects. When prices fall, projects are deferred. The CapEx cycle creates a self-correcting supply mechanism   but the 10-20 year development timeline means the supply response to high prices arrives long after the price signal appeared.

The current situation (2024): after years of capital underinvestment during the 2013-2020 period (when copper prices were insufficient to incentivize new project development), the pipeline of new projects available for development in 2030-2035 is thin. Even the projects that received final investment decisions in 2020-2022 will not reach full production until approximately 2028-2032.
