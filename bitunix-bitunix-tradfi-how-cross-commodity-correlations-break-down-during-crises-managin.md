## Why Crisis Correlations Spike: The Forced Liquidation Mechanism

In normal markets, a hedge fund holds XAUUSDT long and COPPERUSDT long because their fundamental drivers are different (TIPS yields vs PMI) and the portfolio benefits from diversification. But if the fund experiences margin calls on an unrelated position (equities, FX), it must raise cash immediately. The fastest way to raise cash is selling liquid positions   which includes both XAUUSDT and COPPERUSDT regardless of their individual fundamental merits.

This forced selling from the fund's unrelated margin call creates simultaneous selling pressure in both XAUUSDT and COPPERUSDT   instruments that normally have low correlation now both fall together because the selling force is non-fundamental (cash need) rather than instrument-specific.

The result: during the first two weeks of any financial crisis, virtually all commodity instruments fall together as forced liquidation dominates. The diversification that normally exists temporarily disappears.
