## PPI vs CPI: The Lead-Lag Relationship

Consumer Price Index (CPI) measures prices that consumers pay at the final point of sale. Producer Price Index measures prices at the production stage: what manufacturers pay for raw materials and what wholesalers charge distributors. Because price increases at the production level take time to pass through to consumers, PPI changes typically lead CPI changes by 4-8 weeks.

For XAUUSDT, this lead-lag means: when PPI rises strongly (above consensus), CPI will likely rise similarly in 4-8 weeks. Rising future CPI means rising future inflation expectations, which means falling future real interest rates, which means rising gold demand. ## PPI for Industrial Metals: The Input Cost Signal

Metals and mining PPI tracks producer prices for raw materials and intermediate goods including metals. When metals PPI is rising, it means production costs for downstream manufacturers are increasing, which may indicate either commodity supply tightness (fundamentally bullish for COPPERUSDT and other industrial metals) or demand surge from manufacturing recovery.

When metals PPI is falling, input cost deflation suggests either commodity oversupply or demand weakness. Distinguishing between these causes requires combining PPI data with inventory levels and PMI data: falling metals PPI plus rising PMI = supply relief while demand grows (can be copper-bullish); falling metals PPI plus falling PMI = demand weakness (copper-bearish).
