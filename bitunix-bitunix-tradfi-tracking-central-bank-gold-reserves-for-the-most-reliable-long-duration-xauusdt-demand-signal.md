## The Central Bank Gold Buying Trend: Why It Started and Why It Persists

Central banks began accumulating gold in earnest after 2010, reversing decades of net selling. Two structural reasons drive this trend: First, the 2022 freezing of Russia's central bank dollar reserves demonstrated that dollar-denominated reserves carry geopolitical seizure risk for any country that might come into conflict with the United States or its allies. Gold holds no such counterparty risk. Second, decades of zero and negative interest rates reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold for central banks that compared gold to near-zero-yielding bonds.

Both structural drivers remain intact regardless of short-term gold price movements, creating a persistent demand floor that does not disappear during normal price corrections.
