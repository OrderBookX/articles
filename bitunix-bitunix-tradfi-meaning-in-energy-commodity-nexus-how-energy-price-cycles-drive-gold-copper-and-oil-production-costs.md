Mines powered primarily by solar energy (several Chilean copper mines are implementing this) face energy costs determined by capex-intensive but low-variable-cost solar panels rather than fluctuating diesel prices.

This transition reduces the positive correlation between oil prices and mining production costs over the long term, eventually weakening the energy-commodity nexus for those mines that complete the transition.
