## What Contango and Backwardation Mean

Contango describes a market where futures prices for later delivery dates are higher than current spot prices. The curve slopes upward from spot to futures. In normal markets, most commodities trade in contango because carrying costs (storage, insurance, financing) make future delivery worth more than immediate delivery.

Backwardation describes the opposite: futures prices for later delivery are lower than current spot prices. The curve slopes downward. Backwardation occurs when immediate demand is so strong that buyers pay a premium for immediate physical delivery over future delivery.

The market's carry structure (whether it is in contango or backwardation) affects the return earned or paid by holding futures positions over time, which translates directly into the funding rate dynamics of XAUUSDT and COPPERUSDT perpetuals.
