Understanding Futures Trading
-----------------------------

Futures trading involves entering into a **futures contract**, which is an agreement to buy or sell a particular asset at a predetermined price at a specified time in the future. Unlike [spot trading](https://www.bitunix.com?inviteCode=ab9nr3&utm_source=news&utm_medium=blog-article&utm_campaign=labsnews), which involves immediate settlement of transactions, futures trading allows for speculation on the price movements without owning the underlying asset. This creates opportunities for hedging and speculation, making it a favored strategy among seasoned traders.

<figure class="inline-image">![Illustrate the concept of futures trading with a diagram showing the difference between spot trading and futures trading.](https://cirrus-media.bintangjtobing.workers.dev/inline-images/2026/01/05/df296609-0db1-4951-ab90-d6dd34d2f1b0.jpg)</figure>### Key Features of Futures Trading

- **Leverage:** Futures trading allows traders to control a large position with a relatively small amount of capital. This is done through the use of *leverage*, which amplifies both potential gains and risks.
- **Hedging:** Traders can use futures contracts to hedge against price fluctuations, protecting their investments from unforeseen market movements.
- **Speculation:** By speculating on future price movements, traders can profit from both upward and downward trends in the market.
