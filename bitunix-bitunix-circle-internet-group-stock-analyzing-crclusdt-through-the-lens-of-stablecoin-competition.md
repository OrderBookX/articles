## The DAI and Algorithmic Stablecoin Competitive Threat

DAI is a decentralized overcollateralized stablecoin issued by MakerDAO. Unlike USDC, DAI is created by DeFi protocol users depositing crypto collateral. The competitive dynamics between USDC and DAI are largely cooperative rather than zero-sum: USDC is a major collateral type for creating DAI, meaning USDC growth can stimulate DAI growth rather than compete with it.

Algorithmic stablecoins that maintain dollar pegs without full backing have repeatedly failed (most dramatically with UST/LUNA in 2022), which has consistently increased market preference for fully backed stablecoins like USDC. Each algorithmic stablecoin failure is a comparative strengthener for Circle's fully-backed value proposition.

However, the most likely CBDC deployment scenarios position central bank digital currencies as wholesale settlement tools between financial institutions, not direct retail consumer products that would directly compete with USDC.
