## The Core Managed Futures Principle: Trend Following

Managed futures strategies are fundamentally trend-following: they buy instruments that are rising and sell instruments that are falling, based on systematic price-based rules rather than fundamental analysis. The simplest trend-following rule: if the 50-day moving average is above the 200-day moving average, hold long; if below, hold short (or flat).

The theoretical basis: commodity trends persist because fundamental changes in supply-demand conditions develop gradually and take months to years to fully reflect in prices. A new copper mine takes three years to bring online; the supply tightness that eventually prompts the mine development is visible in prices for years before the supply response arrives. Trend following captures this multi-month-to-multi-year directional movement.
