## The Pillars of Traditional Finance

Traditional finance rests on several core pillars. Central banks manage monetary supply and set interest rates to control inflation and stimulate growth. Commercial banks serve as intermediaries, accepting deposits and issuing loans. Investment banks facilitate capital markets, underwriting securities and advising on mergers. Insurance companies manage risk through pooled premiums and actuarial modeling. Stock exchanges provide transparent, regulated venues for buying and selling equities. Each of these pillars operates under strict regulatory oversight designed to maintain market integrity and protect consumers.
