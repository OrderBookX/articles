## The Cause: Saudi-Russia Price War Meets COVID Demand Collapse

The negative oil price event was the product of two simultaneous shocks that would have been extreme individually and were catastrophic together.

In early March 2020, Saudi Arabia and Russia failed to agree on OPEC+ production cuts. Saudi Arabia retaliated by announcing record production increases, flooding the market with supply at the exact moment COVID was destroying demand. Saudi oil exports reached a record high while global air travel had already fallen 60% and road fuel demand was collapsing.

By April 2020, US crude oil storage was filling at an unprecedented rate. The specific Cushing, Oklahoma storage hub that serves as the delivery point for WTI futures was approaching physical capacity. Traders who held May futures contracts and were required to take physical delivery had nowhere to put the oil   and paid up to $37.63 per barrel to get someone else to take delivery.
