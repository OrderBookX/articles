Understanding Futures Trading
-----------------------------

Futures trading involves contracts to buy or sell a specific quantity of an asset at a predetermined price on a future date. These contracts enable traders to speculate on the price movements of assets like *Bitcoin* and *Ethereum* without owning the underlying asset. This form of trading is popular for its ability to leverage positions, manage risk through hedging, and capitalize on short-term market movements.

Futures contracts are standardized agreements facilitated by exchanges, specifying the quantity, price, and delivery date. Traders can engage in **short selling** to profit from declining markets or use contracts to hedge against potential price declines. Unlike [spot trading](https://www.bitunix.com?inviteCode=ab9nr3&utm_source=news&utm_medium=blog-article&utm_campaign=labsnews), futures allow for significant leverage, enabling traders to control larger positions with a smaller capital outlay.

Risks and Considerations in Futures Trading
-------------------------------------------

While futures trading offers substantial potential returns, it also entails significant risks, especially with high leverage. Traders must be aware of the potential for **liquidation** if market movements go against their positions. Understanding risk management strategies, such as setting *stop loss* orders and maintaining adequate **maintenance margin**, is crucial for mitigating these risks.
