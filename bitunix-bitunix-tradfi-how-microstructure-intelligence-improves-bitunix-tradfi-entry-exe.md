## The Bid-Ask Spread and Its Execution Cost Implication

The bid-ask spread   the difference between the highest current buy offer (bid) and the lowest current sell offer (ask)   represents the immediate transaction cost of any market order. For XAUUSDT at a bid of $2,099.50 and ask of $2,100.50, the spread is $1.00 or approximately 0.05% of price. This spread cost is incurred on entry AND on exit   making the round-trip transaction cost approximately 0.10%.

At 6x leverage, a 0.10% round-trip spread cost represents 0.60% of margin capital   meaningful but manageable for a position expected to move 8-12%.

When spreads widen significantly (during low-liquidity periods or around major events), the transaction cost increases. A spread of $5.00 at 6x leverage represents 3% of margin capital   now a significant portion of the expected return from a correctly-called trade.

The practical rule: only execute entries and exits when spreads are within 2x of their typical level. During events (FOMC, major economic data), spreads widen significantly in the first 30 seconds   wait 30 seconds after any major scheduled data release before executing.

## The Order Book Depth Signal

Deep order books (many limit orders within 0.5% of current price) provide better execution quality   large positions can be filled near the current price without significant slippage. Thin order books (few limit orders near the current price) create slippage   large market orders push through multiple price levels to fill, achieving a worse average price than the quoted bid or ask.
