## Understanding the Cycle Structure

A typical crypto cycle has four phases. The bear market bottom where prices are low and sentiment is negative. The early recovery where prices begin climbing but most investors remain skeptical. The acceleration phase where mainstream attention returns and prices rise rapidly. And the euphoric peak where everything seems like it will go up forever. DCA through all four phases produces an average cost that captures the cheap accumulation of the bear and early recovery while diluting the expensive purchases during euphoria.

## Planning Your Auto Invest Around Cycles

If you are starting during a bear market, you have an excellent entry environment. Your early purchases will be at depressed prices, and as the cycle progresses, those units will appreciate significantly. If you are starting during a bull market, you are paying higher prices now but your plan will continue into the next bear market where your cost basis will drop. The longer your time horizon, the less your starting point matters.

## Position Sizing Across a Full Cycle

Commit an amount you can sustain for at least two years, ideally four. The cycle length means that short-duration DCA plans may only capture one phase, which skews results. A plan that runs through an entire cycle captures the full averaging benefit across all market conditions and sentiment extremes.

## Exit Strategy Considerations

DCA is an accumulation strategy, not a forever hold strategy. At some point, you may want to take profits. Some investors reduce or pause their auto invest plan during obvious euphoric peaks and resume during the subsequent correction. Others maintain constant DCA but periodically sell a percentage of accumulated holdings when their target returns are met. Define your exit criteria before you start. Without predetermined exit rules, you will either sell too early from fear or hold too long from greed.
