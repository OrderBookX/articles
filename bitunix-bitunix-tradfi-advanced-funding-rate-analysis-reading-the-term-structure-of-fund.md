## The Funding Rate Term Structure

A funding rate term structure is built by plotting the 8-hour funding rate over time for a specific instrument. Three distinctive patterns emerge:

Pattern 1   Persistently positive and rising funding (bullish momentum with increasing crowding): managed money is consistently paying to be long. The rate has risen from 0.01% to 0.04% per 8-hour period over two weeks. This pattern indicates: the instrument has been in a sustained bull move, longs are building, the cost of being long is increasing. At 0.04% per 8 hours (annualized: 44%), the funding burden is becoming significant. History shows this level is associated with near-term consolidation or correction   the high funding cost eventually forces leveraged longs to reduce positions.

Pattern 2   Persistently negative funding (short sellers paying to stay short): negative funding for two consecutive weeks indicates unusual short seller conviction against the current price. This is typically associated with either a sharp recent price rise that has created contrarian shorts, or fundamental bearish conviction overriding the recent price. When negative funding persists despite a stable or rising price, short sellers are losing money to maintain their positions   a potential short squeeze setup.

Pattern 3   Oscillating funding (neutral sentiment): funding alternates between mildly positive and mildly negative without trend. This indicates genuinely balanced sentiment   neither longs nor shorts are confident enough to build positions that create directional funding pressure. This is typically a low-volatility consolidation period before the next trend.
