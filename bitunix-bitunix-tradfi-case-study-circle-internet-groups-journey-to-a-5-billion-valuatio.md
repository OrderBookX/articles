## The Pivot That Defined Circle

Circle launched in 2013 as a consumer Bitcoin payment app and exchange, competing with early exchanges in a Wild West regulatory environment. The company raised over $100 million from institutional investors including Goldman Sachs and IDG Capital. But by 2017, the regulatory risks of running a crypto exchange had convinced Circle to pivot toward a different model: building regulated financial infrastructure for digital dollars.

The acquisition of Poloniex exchange in 2018 (and subsequent sale in 2019) was the turning point that crystallized Circle's direction. The lesson from Poloniex: exchange businesses require constant regulatory navigation and face existential risk from single regulatory decisions. Infrastructure businesses (stablecoin issuance, payment settlement) have regulatory complexity but not existential regulatory risk.

## USDC Launch and the Revenue Model That Built the $5B Valuation

USDC launched in September 2018 as a joint venture between Circle and Coinbase through the Centre Consortium. The timing was not ideal   the 2018-2019 crypto bear market meant USDC adoption was slow initially. But Circle's insight was that USDC's utility would grow with DeFi adoption, and DeFi would grow with Ethereum's maturation.

By 2021, DeFi summer had made USDC the essential settlement currency for every major DeFi protocol. USDC supply grew from $1 billion in 2020 to $55 billion by January 2022.
