## The Rating Downgrade Channel for XAUUSDT

When a major sovereign credit rating is downgraded, institutional holders of that country's government bonds face several constraints: some investment mandates require a minimum credit rating for holdings, forcing automatic selling. Insurance regulators in several jurisdictions require additional capital buffers for below-investment-grade sovereign bond holdings. Foreign central banks that hold the downgraded country's bonds may be less willing to add to positions.

The forced reallocation from downgraded sovereign bonds often flows partially into gold, which has no credit rating to be downgraded. This is particularly true for central bank reserve managers who replace downgraded sovereign bond reserves with gold.
