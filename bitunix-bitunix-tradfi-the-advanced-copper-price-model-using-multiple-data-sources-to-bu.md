Variable 1   China PMI New Orders Trend (leads copper by 6-8 weeks): Current NBS New Orders sub-index and its direction over the past two months. A New Orders reading of 52.5 and rising for two months implies copper demand growth beginning within 6-8 weeks.

Variable 2   LME Inventory Trajectory (current physical balance): Current LME stocks and the 4-week rate of change. Quantify in terms of weeks of global consumption: LME stocks divided by weekly global copper consumption (approximately 500,000 tonnes/week globally). ## Translating Model Inputs Into a Price Range

The 90-day forecast is expressed as a range rather than a point estimate:

Bull case price: assume the most favorable outcomes from each variable (PMI recovery accelerating, inventory draws continuing, backwardation deepening, no new supply disruptions, credit conditions easing). What price does this combination imply based on historical sensitivity?

Bear case price: assume the least favorable outcomes from each variable. What price does this combination imply?

The 90-day price range: the spread between bull and bear cases is the 90-day price uncertainty range. Position size should be calibrated so that the maximum loss from the bear case is within the defined risk budget.
