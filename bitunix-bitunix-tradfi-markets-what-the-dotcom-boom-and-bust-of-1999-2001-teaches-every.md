## The Dotcom Setup: When the Narrative Was Right But the Valuation Was Wrong

The dotcom narrative was not wrong in its fundamental prediction. The Internet did transform commerce, media, communication, and virtually every industry. Amazon, Google, and Apple went on to create more than $5 trillion in combined market value. The narrative was correct.

What was wrong was the timeline and the valuation. Investors in 1999 were pricing in a transformation that would take 15-20 years to materialize as if it would be complete within 3-5 years. Companies with no revenue and no plausible path to profitability were valued at hundreds of millions of dollars because their business plan included the word "internet."

The Nasdaq peaked at 5,048 in March 2000 and fell 78% to 1,114 by October 2002. Companies that were genuinely going to be important (Amazon, which fell from $100 to $5 during the crash) were not immune to the repricing.
