## The Hydrogen Economy Platinum Demand Potential

Two applications create platinum demand in the hydrogen economy:

PEM Electrolyzers (hydrogen production): Proton Exchange Membrane electrolyzers use platinum (and iridium) catalysts to split water into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity. Current platinum loading: approximately 0.1-0.3 grams per kilowatt of electrolyzer capacity. At the IEA's Net Zero Scenario projection of 2,000 gigawatts of electrolysis capacity by 2050, platinum demand from electrolyzers could reach 200-600 tonnes annually   compared to current total annual platinum mine supply of approximately 150 tonnes.

PEM Fuel Cells (hydrogen consumption): hydrogen fuel cell systems use platinum catalysts to convert hydrogen back to electricity. Current platinum loading in automotive fuel cells: approximately 30-40 grams per vehicle (Toyota Mirai, Hyundai Nexo). At significant scale, even 1 million hydrogen fuel cell vehicles annually would require 30-40 tonnes of platinum   meaningful but not transformative alone.
