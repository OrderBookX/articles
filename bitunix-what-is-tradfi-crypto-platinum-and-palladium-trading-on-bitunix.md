## Understanding Platinum Market Dynamics

Platinum is rarer than gold, with annual mining production significantly smaller. Its primary demand comes from the automotive industry, where it is used in catalytic converters for diesel vehicles. Jewelry and industrial applications account for the remainder. Supply is geographically concentrated, with South Africa producing approximately 70 percent of global output. This concentration creates supply risk that can drive sharp price movements when mining disruptions occur due to labor disputes, power outages, or regulatory changes.

## Understanding Palladium Market Dynamics

Palladium is primarily used in catalytic converters for gasoline vehicles. Russia and South Africa dominate global production, creating geopolitical supply risk. The shift toward electric vehicles represents a long-term demand threat, but the transition timeline remains uncertain, and current vehicle production still requires substantial palladium input. Price spikes driven by supply constraints have historically created some of the most volatile moves among precious metals.
