## What Funding Rates Are and Why They Exist

Perpetual futures contracts on Bitunix have no expiration date, which means there is no natural settlement mechanism to keep the futures price aligned with the buy Cosmos Atom spot price. Funding rates fill this role by creating periodic payments between long and short traders that incentivize the futures price to stay close to spot.

When the futures price trades above spot (positive funding), long position holders pay short position holders. This payment discourages excessive long positioning and pushes the futures price back toward spot. When futures trades below spot (negative funding), shorts pay longs, discouraging excessive short positioning.

Funding payments on Bitunix occur every eight hours. The rate varies based on the premium or discount between ATOM futures and spot prices. Rates can be positive, negative, or very close to zero depending on market sentiment and positioning.

## How Funding Rates Affect Your ATOM crypto Positions

If you hold a long ATOM futures position during a period of consistently positive funding, you pay the funding rate every eight hours. These costs accumulate and reduce your effective returns. Conversely, if you hold a short position during positive funding, you receive payments every eight hours. Experienced traders sometimes take positions specifically to capture funding income when rates are elevated, treating it as a yield strategy rather than a directional bet.
