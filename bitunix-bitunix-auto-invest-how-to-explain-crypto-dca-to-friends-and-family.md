## The Simple Explanation

Dollar cost averaging into crypto works exactly like a retirement savings plan. You invest a fixed amount every week or month into assets you believe will grow over time. You do not try to pick the perfect day to buy. You just buy consistently and let time smooth out the volatility. Most people understand this concept immediately because it is how 401k and pension plans have worked for decades.

## Addressing the Volatility Concern

When people point to crypto volatility as a reason not to invest, explain that volatility is exactly why DCA works well. Large price swings mean your fixed investment buys significantly more units during dips and fewer during spikes. Over time, this produces an average cost that is lower than what most manual investors achieve. Volatility is not a bug in this strategy. It is a feature that the strategy is designed to exploit.

## Addressing the Risk Concern

Be honest about risk. DCA does not eliminate the possibility of loss. If the crypto market declines permanently, you lose money regardless of how disciplined your purchases were. But explain that you limit your exposure to an amount you can afford to lose entirely, you focus on assets with the strongest fundamentals and longest track records, and you review your plan regularly to ensure your investment thesis still holds.
