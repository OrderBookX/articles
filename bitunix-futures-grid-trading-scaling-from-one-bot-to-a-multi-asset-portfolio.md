## Core and Satellite Structure

Core bots run on the most liquid pairs with conservative parameters. BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT grids at 3x to 5x leverage, moderate ranges, reliable oscillation. These are your consistent performers and should receive 60 to 70% of your grid capital.

### Satellite Positions

Satellite bots use more volatile pairs or the new Bitunix TradFi assets like gold (XAUUSDT) or silver (XAGUSDT). Higher potential returns but also higher risk per bot. Keep satellite allocation at 20 to 30% of total grid capital.

## Direction Diversification

If all your bots are long, a broad selloff hurts everything simultaneously. Include short grids on pairs with bearish setups to create a natural hedge. On Bitunix, each bot runs in its own isolated account — long and short positions across different bots do not interfere.

## Portfolio-Level Tracking

Individual bot tracking on Bitunix tells you which configurations work. Portfolio-level tracking tells you whether your overall approach generates adequate risk-adjusted returns. You may find winners offset by losers — that rebalancing insight is valuable.

### Weekly Rebalancing

Every Friday, review all running bots. Stop underperformers — bots that have been paused for days or showing persistent negative total PnL. Redeploy capital into pairs currently showing fresh consolidation. This active management is what separates a grid portfolio from a collection of abandoned bots.

## The Reserve Buffer

Keep 20 to 30% of total grid capital uninvested. This reserve handles redeployment after SL triggers and captures new opportunities mid-week without cannibalizing existing bots.
