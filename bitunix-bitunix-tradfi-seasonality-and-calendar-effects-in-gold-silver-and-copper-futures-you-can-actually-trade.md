## January Effect in Gold

Gold's January has historically been positive in more years than any other month, driven by three simultaneous demand factors: institutional rebalancing into precious metals at the start of a new financial year, physical gold demand from Chinese New Year preparation (particularly in Asia), and investment demand from individuals and funds making new year investment commitments.

The January XAUUSDT seasonal trade: establish a long XAUUSDT position in late December when the seasonal demand is building but before the broad market has positioned for it. Exit target in late January or when New Year demand has peaked. Historical analysis shows this seasonal trade has worked in approximately 60-65% of years, well above random chance.

Caveat: macro conditions override seasonal effects. A January where the Federal Reserve is mid-rate-hike cycle, real rates are rising sharply, and the dollar is strengthening will see the seasonal gold demand overwhelmed by macro headwinds. Use seasonality as a timing enhancement for macro-supported trades, not as a standalone signal.

## Post-Chinese New Year Copper Recovery

Copper typically experiences a seasonal weakness in January and early February as Chinese factories shut for the Lunar New Year holiday period. The post-holiday restart in late February and March creates a copper demand surge as factories resume operations, inventory levels are replenished, and delayed orders are executed.

The post-holiday COPPERUSDT seasonal trade: look for COPPERUSDT weakness in January as Chinese demand seasonally pauses. Establish long positions in late January to early February, positioning for the post-holiday manufacturing restart demand surge. Exit in March when the restart demand has been absorbed and inventory levels have normalized.

## Year-End Window Dressing and TSLAUSDT

Institutional fund managers practice year-end window dressing: buying the year's best-performing stocks before year-end portfolio disclosures to show they held the winners. In years when TSLAUSDT has been a strong performer, window dressing buying in November-December can extend the year-end appreciation. Conversely, funds dump underperformers before year-end, potentially exacerbating TSLAUSDT weakness in losing years.
