## The Five Regional Demand Components

Total global copper demand = China + North America + Europe + Japan/Korea + Rest of World.

Component 1   China (approximately 55% of global demand):
Use ICSG (International Copper Study Group) quarterly data for apparent copper demand (production + imports - exports). When not yet published, estimate from CAAM auto production + grid investment + property completion data weighted by copper intensity per sector (detailed in the sector article).

Component 2   North America (approximately 12%):
US copper apparent demand from US Census Bureau monthly survey of copper fabricators. When fabricator apparent demand is above its 5-year seasonal average, North American consumption is above trend.

Component 3   Europe (approximately 14%):
European Copper Institute quarterly data. Alternatively, estimate from German industrial production (Germany is the largest European copper consumer through automotive and machinery sectors).

Component 4   Japan and South Korea (approximately 7%):
Japan Copper and Brass Association monthly statistics, Korean non-ferrous metals industry association data.

Component 5   Rest of World (approximately 12%):
Growing at highest rate due to electrification in India, Southeast Asia, and Africa. IMF economic growth forecasts for emerging markets provide the demand growth multiplier.

## Building the Supply Side

Total global copper supply = mine production + recycling.

Mine production: ICSG provides monthly estimates with 2-month lag. BHP, Rio Tinto, Freeport quarterly earnings provide real-time guidance for the 3 largest producer contributions to total supply.

Recycling: World Bureau of Metal Statistics (WBMS) annual survey, supplemented by ICSG quarterly estimates.

## The Deficit/Surplus Calculation and Its Price Implication

Demand minus supply equals the market's structural position.
