## The Current State of Dollar Dominance

Despite recurring dedollarization narratives, the dollar's dominance remains substantial and change is slow:

SWIFT payment flows: dollar-denominated international payments represent approximately 47% of SWIFT traffic (SWIFT annual report, free at swift.com). While this is down from 55%+ in 2015, the change over 8 years is modest.

FX reserves: IMF COFER data shows the dollar's share of global FX reserves at approximately 59% (down from 65% in 2016). Again, a real but slow decline.

Commodity pricing: oil, copper, gold, and most agricultural commodities are still globally priced in dollars. No major commodity pricing shift away from the dollar has occurred despite multiple political attempts.

The practical implication: dedollarization is real but slow. It is a 10-30 year structural shift, not a cyclical 1-3 year catalyst.
