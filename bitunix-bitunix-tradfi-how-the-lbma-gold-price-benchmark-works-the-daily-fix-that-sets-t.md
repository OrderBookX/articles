## The Benchmark Process: Not an Auction, But an Algorithm

The LBMA Gold Price is not set by a traditional open-outcry auction or by a single market maker. Since 2015, ICE Benchmark Administration runs an electronic auction process twice daily   the AM benchmark at 10:30 AM London time and the PM benchmark at 3:00 PM London time. The process:

A starting price is proposed. Participating banks (currently including HSBC, ICBC Standard Bank, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Standard Chartered, and others) enter their aggregated client buy and sell orders at that price. If the imbalance between buy and sell orders exceeds 10,000 ounces, the price is adjusted until the buy-sell imbalance falls below 10,000 ounces. The price at which the imbalance falls below the threshold becomes the benchmark.

The AM benchmark is the most widely referenced: central bank reserve valuations, gold ETF NAV calculations, mine offtake agreements, and jewelry wholesale contracts worldwide are priced off the LBMA AM fix. When traders say "gold is at $2,000," they typically mean the most recent LBMA AM benchmark.

## The Historical Manipulation Legacy and Its Modern Relevance

Deutsche Bank settled charges related to gold benchmark manipulation in 2016. The legacy: the modern electronic IBA process was designed explicitly to prevent the coordination that enabled the historical manipulation allegations.
