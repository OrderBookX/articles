## Open Interest: The Conviction Signal

Open interest is the total number of outstanding contracts in a futures market that have not been settled. Rising open interest during a price trend confirms that new money is entering the market in the direction of the trend, suggesting the move has genuine conviction behind it. Falling open interest during a price trend suggests the move is driven by short covering or long liquidation rather than new directional positioning, which is typically a weaker and more reversible trend.

When gold prices are rising alongside rising open interest in COMEX futures, the move is more likely to continue. ## Futures Basis: The Spot vs Futures Premium Signal

The basis in commodity futures is the difference between the futures price and the current spot price. When futures trade above spot (contango), the market is pricing in carrying costs and expects supply to be available. When futures trade below spot (backwardation), physical demand is so strong that buyers are willing to pay a premium for immediate delivery over future delivery.

Backwardation in copper or gold markets is a strong bullish signal for COPPERUSDT or XAUUSDT respectively, because it indicates tightness in the physical market that is driving immediate demand at premium prices. Sustained backwardation often precedes significant futures price appreciation as the physical tightness is absorbed.
