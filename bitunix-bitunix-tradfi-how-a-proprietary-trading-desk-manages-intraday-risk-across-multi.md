## The Intraday Stop Protocol

Prop desks have a specific intraday stop protocol that is separate from position-level stop-losses: a maximum intraday portfolio loss limit. If the portfolio loses more than 1.5% of total equity in any single trading session, the protocol requires stopping all new position entries for the remainder of that session. Active positions are maintained but no new risk is added.

This portfolio-level intraday stop prevents the "averaging down in a crisis" behavior that turns manageable drawdowns into catastrophic losses. It also prevents the psychological trap of adding to losing positions during the worst intraday sessions.

The retail practitioner adaptation: calculate 1.5% of account equity as the daily portfolio loss limit. When intraday losses across all open positions exceed this threshold, stop all new entries for the day and review the next morning.
