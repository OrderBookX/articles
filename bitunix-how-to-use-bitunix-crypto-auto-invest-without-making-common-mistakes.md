## Mistake 1: Changing the Plan After Every Market Move

The number one killer of DCA performance is constant tinkering. Price drops 10% and you pause the plan to wait for a better entry. Price pumps 20% and you increase your allocation to catch more upside. By the end of the month, you have made six changes to a plan that was supposed to run on autopilot. Every change you make introduces a timing decision. The whole point of auto invest is to remove timing decisions. Set it and commit for at least three months before evaluating.

## Mistake 2: Allocating More Than You Can Sustain

Enthusiasm is highest at the beginning. Many investors set aggressive cycle amounts that feel comfortable during their first paycheck but become burdensome after a few months, especially if the market declines. If your auto invest amount forces you to choose between investment and living expenses, the plan is too aggressive. Start conservative. You can always increase later. You cannot easily recover from stopping a plan during a bear market because you overstretched your budget.

## Mistake 3: Ignoring Asset Selection

Automating bad decisions does not make them good. If you add a token to your DCA plan based on social media hype rather than fundamental analysis, automation just ensures you consistently lose money on a bad bet. Research every asset before including it in a recurring plan. Look at trading volume, development activity, use case clarity, and whether the project has survived at least one full market cycle. If it has not, size the position smaller accordingly.

## Mistake 4: Never Reviewing the Plan

Passive investing does not mean permanent neglect. Markets evolve. Projects fail. New opportunities emerge. A monthly review of your plan takes 15 minutes and ensures your allocations still make sense in the current landscape.

## Mistake 5: Expecting Guaranteed Returns

DCA is a risk management strategy, not a profit guarantee. It reduces your average cost and smooths volatility impact. It does not protect against sustained market declines or poor asset selection. Keep expectations realistic and focus on the discipline rather than short-term results. Think of auto invest as a savings account with market exposure. Consistent deposits over time, periodic review, and patience.
