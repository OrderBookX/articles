Understanding Perpetual Futures
-------------------------------

Perpetual futures, often referred to as *perps*, are derivative contracts that, unlike traditional futures, do not have an expiration date. This feature allows traders to hold their positions indefinitely, which is particularly useful in a 24/7 trading environment like cryptocurrency markets.

The **funding rate mechanism** in perpetual futures ensures that the contract price aligns closely with the spot price. Traders can benefit from **capital efficiency via leverage**, meaning they can control a larger position with a smaller amount of capital, amplifying potential returns. However, this also comes with increased **risk of liquidation** if the market moves against the position.

----------------------

Spot trading involves buying or selling cryptocurrencies for immediate delivery at the current market price. It is a straightforward and popular method for many investors and traders, providing direct ownership of the digital asset without the complexity of derivative contracts.

While spot trading is simpler, it lacks the ability to utilize **leverage**, which means traders must fully fund their positions. This can limit the potential for higher returns but also reduces exposure to risky events like forced liquidations.
