## The Credit Cycle Transmission to COPPERUSDT

Credit tightening affects copper through three channels:

Channel 1   Chinese property developer financing: Chinese property developers (the primary buyers of copper-intensive building materials) depend heavily on pre-sale receipts and bank financing for project construction. When Chinese bank credit to the property sector tightens (as occurred in 2021 with the "three red lines" policy), property starts decline   directly reducing copper demand from the construction sector.

The monitoring signal: China's aggregate financing to the real economy (AFRE), published monthly by the PBOC, measures total new credit creation including bank loans, shadow banking, and bonds. When AFRE growth falls below 9% year-over-year for two consecutive months, credit tightening is significant enough to affect investment-driven copper demand.

Channel 2   US investment grade credit spreads: when US IG credit spreads widen beyond 150 basis points (the ICE BofA Investment Grade Index OAS, free at FRED series BAMLC0A0CM), companies are paying materially more to borrow   creating capital expenditure restraint across all industries, including copper-intensive infrastructure and manufacturing.

Channel 3   Global interbank lending rates: LIBOR/SOFR spread above the risk-free rate (the TED spread historically) indicates interbank stress   the financial system's plumbing is under pressure, reducing credit availability globally.
