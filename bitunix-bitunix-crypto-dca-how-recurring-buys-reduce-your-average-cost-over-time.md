## The Math in Practice

Imagine investing $100 per week into an asset priced at $50, $40, $30, $40, and $50 across five weeks. Your purchases buy 2, 2.5, 3.33, 2.5, and 2 units respectively. Total invested: $500. Total units: 12.33. Average cost per unit: $40.55. But the simple average of prices across those weeks is $42. Your DCA average cost is 3.4% lower than the arithmetic average because you bought more units at the cheaper prices. This gap widens with greater volatility.

## Why the Effect Strengthens in Volatile Markets

Crypto's high volatility amplifies the DCA averaging benefit. In a market where prices swing 20% to 30% within a month, the difference between your dollar-weighted average and the simple price average can be significant. Traditional markets with 5% to 10% monthly swings produce a smaller DCA benefit. Crypto's volatility, often cited as a risk, actually enhances the core mechanism of dollar cost averaging.

## The Compounding Effect Over Multiple Cycles

A single month of DCA produces a modest averaging effect. A year of DCA across a market that has experienced both rallies and corrections produces a noticeably lower average cost than the midpoint of the year's price range. Four years of DCA spanning an entire Bitcoin cycle produces an average cost that sits well below the cycle's peak and typically generates strong returns by the next cycle's peak.
