## The Kelly Criterion: The Mathematical Optimal Position Size

The Kelly Criterion is the mathematically optimal fraction of capital to allocate to a bet with known win probability and payoff ratio. For bitunix tradfi trading: Kelly Fraction = (Win Probability x Payoff Ratio - Loss Probability) / Payoff Ratio.

Example: If your historical win rate on XAUUSDT macro trades is 55% and your average win-to-loss ratio is 2:1, the full Kelly position is: (0.55 x 2 - 0.45) / 2 = (1.10 - 0.45) / 2 = 0.325, or 32.5% of account capital.

However, full Kelly sizing is mathematically optimal only if your win probability and payoff ratio estimates are perfectly accurate. In practice, uncertainty around these estimates means using half-Kelly (16.25% in the example) or quarter-Kelly (8.1%) produces better long-term outcomes by reducing the risk of over-sizing when estimates are slightly wrong.
