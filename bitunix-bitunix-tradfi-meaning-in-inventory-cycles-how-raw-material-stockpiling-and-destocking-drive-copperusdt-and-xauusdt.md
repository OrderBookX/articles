## The Inventory Cycle Mechanics

Industrial commodity consumers (copper fabricators, steel producers, electronics manufacturers) maintain strategic inventories of raw materials that provide production security. These inventories are not constant: they expand during periods of supply uncertainty (fear of shortages drives stockpiling) and contract during periods of ample supply (confidence that materials will be available defers purchases to the spot market).

When multiple downstream consumers simultaneously stockpile a commodity, the sudden additional demand from inventory building creates a temporary demand spike that drives prices higher even without underlying end-use demand growth. When stockpiling ends and destocking begins (consumers drawing down inventory rather than making new purchases), the apparent demand disappears and prices can fall sharply even while actual end-use demand is stable.
