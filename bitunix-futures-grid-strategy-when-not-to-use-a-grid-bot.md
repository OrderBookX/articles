Futures Grid Strategy — When NOT to Use a Grid Bot

Knowing when to deploy a grid is important. Knowing when not to deploy one might be even more important. Grid bots aren't suited for every market condition, and forcing one into the wrong environment is a reliable way to lose money.


## When to Stay Out

Don't use a grid during strong directional trends. If BTC is rallying 15-20% in a week, a long grid barely trades because price keeps punching above the range. A short grid in this scenario accumulates massive unrealized losses with every grid level that fills. Neither direction produces good outcomes in a strong trend.


### Before Major Catalysts

## Before Major Catalysts

Don't use a grid right before major catalysts. Fed rate decisions, ETF approvals, protocol hard forks, major exchange listings — these events can break ranges violently and instantly. If you must run a grid through such events, widen your range significantly, lower your leverage, and tighten your stop-loss. But honestly, the smarter play is often to sit out entirely and redeploy after conditions stabilize.

## On Illiquid Pairs

Don't use a grid on illiquid pairs. If the order book is thin and the bid-ask spread is wide, your fills will be inconsistent. You'll experience slippage on almost every grid cycle, and the theoretical profits your grid should generate will evaporate in execution costs.

## Without Active Monitoring

Don't use a grid when you can't monitor it at all. Grids are automated, but they're not set-and-forget-forever. Market conditions change, ranges break, and funding rates shift. If you're going completely offline for weeks, either enable tight TP/SL or stop the bot.

Don't use a grid with money you can't afford to lose. This applies to all trading, but grid bots can create a false sense of security. The automated nature makes it feel safe. It's not. Leverage means your entire allocation can be lost.

It's also worth noting that grid trading conditions come and go. The market cycles between trending phases and ranging phases. During trending months, grid opportunities are fewer and riskier. During ranging months, they're abundant. Having the patience to sit out when conditions don't support grids and the readiness to deploy when they do is a meta-skill that determines long-term success with this strategy.
