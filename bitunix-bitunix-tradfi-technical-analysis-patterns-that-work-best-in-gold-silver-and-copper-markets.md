## Why Commodity Technical Analysis Differs From Crypto Technical Analysis

Commodity futures markets are contested by a different mix of participants than crypto markets. Physical producers (miners, refiners) systematically hedge at price levels above their production cost, creating predictable supply of short positions at specific price levels. Physical consumers hedge at levels below their replacement cost, creating predictable demand for long positions. These commercial hedger behaviors create price levels with genuine fundamental support and resistance that pure technical traders can observe in chart patterns.

Crypto markets lack this commercial hedger layer. Resistance and support in crypto are primarily psychological (round numbers, prior highs and lows) rather than fundamentally anchored. This means commodity technical patterns that reflect genuine physical market behavior are often more reliable than equivalent crypto patterns.

## RSI Divergence for Precious Metals Reversal Detection

Relative Strength Index divergence, where price makes a new high or new low but RSI does not confirm the new extreme, is a useful warning signal for potential reversals in XAUUSDT and XAGUSDT. When gold makes a new all-time high but the RSI reading is lower than it was at the prior high, the divergence suggests internal momentum is weakening despite new price highs.

Bullish RSI divergence (price makes new low but RSI higher than at prior low) often precedes gold and silver recoveries after extended selling. This pattern is most reliable when it occurs at technically significant support levels that coincide with fundamentally justified accumulation areas (for example, at XAUUSDT levels where central bank buying historically accelerates).
