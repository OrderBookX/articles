## The Risk Premium in Oil Futures

Oil producers who sell future production forward at current prices receive a price certainty benefit, which they pay for by accepting a slight discount to the expected future spot price. This discount compensates the speculator who takes the long side of the hedge with a positive expected return for bearing the price uncertainty risk.

When geopolitical risk is elevated (Hormuz tensions, OPEC compliance uncertainty, Russia-Ukraine conflict effects on energy), the risk premium embedded in oil futures increases. This elevated premium creates higher expected returns for long oil positions but also means more of the current price is "risk premium" rather than "fundamental value," which can reverse quickly if risks de-escalate.
