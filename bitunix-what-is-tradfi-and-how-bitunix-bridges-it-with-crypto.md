# What Is TradFi and How Bitunix Bridges It With Crypto

Traditional finance, commonly known as TradFi, refers to the conventional financial system that has governed global economies for centuries. It includes banks, stock exchanges, insurance companies, mutual funds, and government-backed monetary systems. Every time you open a savings account, apply for a mortgage, or invest in a stock index fund, you are participating in TradFi.

## How Traditional Finance Works

The foundation of TradFi is centralization. Central banks control monetary supply and set interest rates. Commercial banks act as intermediaries between depositors and borrowers. Regulatory bodies enforce compliance standards such as Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) protocols. This centralized structure provides stability, consumer protection, and institutional trust, but it also introduces limitations including restricted access hours, high transaction fees, and barriers that exclude billions of people worldwide from participating in formal financial services.
