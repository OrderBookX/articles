## The Consensus Expectation as the Embedded Price

When the US CPI report is scheduled, Bloomberg and Reuters publish a consensus forecast   the median estimate from economists surveyed about the expected print. This consensus is already embedded in XAUUSDT's current price through the forward rate markets and Fed expectations.

If the consensus expects CPI at 3.1% and XAUUSDT is currently at $2,050, the current price reflects the market's assessment of what a 3.1% CPI environment means for Fed policy and real rates. A 3.1% actual print creates no information surprise and typically creates minimal XAUUSDT move.

A 3.5% actual print (well above consensus) is new information: inflation is running hotter than expected, which may delay Fed rate cuts, which pushes real rates higher, which is bearish XAUUSDT. The size of XAUUSDT's reaction is proportional to the surprise magnitude   the deviation from consensus, not from the prior print or from any absolute level.

## The Surprise Index: A Systematic Measurement Tool

Citigroup's Economic Surprise Index (CESI)   published free on Bloomberg for subscribers and tracked by many free financial data aggregators   measures the aggregate of recent economic data surprises (actual minus consensus). A positive CESI means recent data has been consistently beating expectations. A negative CESI means data has been consistently disappointing.

For XAUUSDT: a falling CESI (data consistently disappointing relative to expectations) suggests that real growth is weaker than priced in, which may force the Fed toward easier policy, which reduces real rates   a secondary bullish XAUUSDT signal when combined with the primary TIPS yield direction.
