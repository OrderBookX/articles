## How Inflation Expectations Are Measured

The most accessible measure of market inflation expectations is the 10-year TIPS breakeven rate: the difference between the 10-year nominal Treasury yield and the 10-year TIPS yield. This breakeven rate represents the inflation rate at which a nominal Treasury bond and a TIPS bond provide the same return.

FRED series T10YIE (10-year TIPS Breakeven) is the primary monitor. When this breakeven is rising (markets expect higher future inflation), inflation expectations are increasing. When it is falling, deflation expectations or successful monetary tightening is reducing the inflation outlook.

## Why Expected Inflation Matters More Than CPI for XAUUSDT

A simple example clarifies why: CPI is 8% (very high actual inflation) but the Federal Reserve has credibly communicated a path to bring it to 2% through aggressive rate hikes. The nominal 10-year Treasury yield is 6% (reflecting the expected rate path). The 10-year TIPS breakeven is 2.5% (markets believe the Fed will succeed). The real rate = 6% - 2.5% = 3.5%.

At 3.5% real rates, gold should be performing poorly despite 8% actual inflation   because the expectation of future inflation is moderate (the market believes the Fed will win) and the real rate is positive.

This is exactly what happened in 2022: CPI reached 9.1% but gold fell 4% for the year. The market trusted that the Fed would bring inflation down (breakeven fell from 3.0% to 2.3%) while nominal rates were rising (the combination produced rising real rates that suppressed gold).
