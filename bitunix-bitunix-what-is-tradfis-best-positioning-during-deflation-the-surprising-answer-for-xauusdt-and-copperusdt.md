## What Is TradFi's Deflation Type Distinction?

What is TradFi's critical deflation type distinction? Two fundamentally different types of deflation exist with opposite gold market implications:

Type 1: Good deflation (productivity-driven). When technology or efficiency improvements reduce production costs, prices fall while real incomes and economic activity rise. This type of deflation does not typically trigger safe-haven gold demand because the economic environment is not distressed.

Type 2: Bad deflation (demand-collapse-driven). When credit contraction, balance sheet recession, or severe economic shock causes demand to collapse faster than supply, prices fall with rising unemployment and financial distress.
