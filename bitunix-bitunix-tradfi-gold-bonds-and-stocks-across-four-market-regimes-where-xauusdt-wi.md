## Regime 1: Inflationary Growth (Rising Inflation, Rising GDP)

This regime   common in early commodity supercycles and post-recession recoveries with fiscal stimulus   is actually one of gold's weakest environments. Rising real activity competes with gold's monetary appeal. Risk appetite is high, pushing investors toward equities and commodities with direct economic exposure. Real interest rates often rise as growth outpaces monetary accommodation.

Historical examples: 2003-2006 (strong global growth, early commodity bull), 2021 Q1-Q2 (post-vaccine reopening surge). Gold typically returns 0-5% annually in this regime while equities return 15-25%.

## Regime 2: Stagflation (Rising Inflation, Stagnant or Falling GDP)

This is gold's best regime   consistently and dramatically. When inflation erodes purchasing power while the economy is too weak for central banks to raise rates aggressively, real interest rates are most negative. Central bank credibility is under maximum pressure. The 1970s stagflation decade produced gold's most extraordinary long-run returns.

Historical examples: 1973-1979, 2007-2008 (early stage), 2021-2022 (inflation spike with growth concerns). Gold returns 15-30%+ annually in sustained stagflation regimes.
