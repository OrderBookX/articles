## The Convertible Note Structure

MicroStrategy has issued multiple tranches of convertible notes with varying maturities, conversion prices, and interest obligations. As of 2024, the total convertible note outstanding is approximately $3-4 billion (growing with new issuances). Key characteristics:

Conversion price: each note converts to MSTR equity at a specific price above the current stock price at issuance (typically 30-50% premium). If MSTRUSDT price rises above the conversion price, note holders convert to equity   diluting existing shareholders.

Maturity: notes mature at specific dates (typically 2026-2030 range). At maturity, if the stock price is below the conversion price, MicroStrategy must repay the principal in cash. If the Bitcoin holdings are worth less than the outstanding notes, MicroStrategy must either sell Bitcoin at a loss or issue new equity at potentially distressed prices.

Interest: most convertible notes pay low or zero coupon interest (the conversion option replaces the interest). This reduces near-term cash burden but creates a large balloon payment at maturity.
