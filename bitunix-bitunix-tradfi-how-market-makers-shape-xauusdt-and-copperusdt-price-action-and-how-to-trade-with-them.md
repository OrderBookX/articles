## How Market Maker Behavior Creates Predictable Price Patterns

Market makers systematically hedge their inventory. When they have accumulated too many long positions from customer buying, they need to sell to reduce inventory. This selling pressure creates specific price patterns around key technical levels where many customer orders concentrate.

Round numbers (XAUUSDT at $2,000, $2,100, $2,200) are prime examples: many retail stop-loss orders and take-profit orders cluster at round numbers because retail traders commonly use them as price targets. Market makers, knowing these clusters exist, may briefly push price through these levels to trigger the orders, collect the spread from executing them, and then let price return to its fundamental level.

This behavior creates the "stop hunt" and "fake breakout" patterns that confuse retail traders who have stop-losses at obvious round number levels.
