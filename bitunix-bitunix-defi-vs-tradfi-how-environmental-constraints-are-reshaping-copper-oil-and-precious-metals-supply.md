## Environmental Constraints on Copper Mining: The Supply Ceiling Thesis

Chilean copper mines, which represent approximately 28% of global production, face acute water scarcity in the Atacama Desert region. Copper mining is water-intensive: producing one tonne of copper requires 200-300 cubic meters of water. As Atacama aquifer levels decline and Chilean environmental regulations restrict extraction from overexploited water sources, major copper mines have been required to invest in seawater desalination plants.

Desalination adds approximately $200-400 per tonne to copper production costs, raising the industry's all-in sustaining cost.
