## The Gold vs Copper Pairs Trade: Economic Cycle Rotation

Gold and copper represent opposite economic regimes: gold appreciates in risk-off, slow-growth, high-inflation environments; copper appreciates in risk-on, high-growth, expansionary environments. The copper-to-gold ratio reflects which regime is currently dominant.

When the ratio is falling (copper underperforming gold), the market is pricing in economic deterioration or safe-haven demand. When the ratio is rising (copper outperforming gold), growth optimism is increasing. Extreme ratio readings historically precede reversions toward historical average.

Crude oil and copper are both economic cycle assets that appreciate during growth expansions. However, they have distinct supply drivers (OPEC for oil, mine production for copper) that can cause divergence during supply shock events. When oil is rallying on OPEC cuts while copper is flat on stable Chinese demand, the oil-to-copper ratio rises. When the ratio reaches extreme levels, the pairs reversal trade anticipates normalization.

## Risk Management for All Pairs Trades

The primary risk in commodity pairs trading is that the relationship breaks permanently due to structural change rather than reverting to historical average. Always use stop-losses based on the ratio reaching new historical extremes that suggest the relationship has fundamentally changed.
