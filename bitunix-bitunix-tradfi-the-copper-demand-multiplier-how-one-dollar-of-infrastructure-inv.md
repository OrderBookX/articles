## Copper Intensity by Investment Category

Power grid infrastructure: approximately 15-25 tonnes of copper per million USD of investment. The highest copper-intensity infrastructure category. High-voltage transmission lines, transformers, substations, and distribution networks are extremely copper-intensive per dollar of construction cost.

Residential construction: approximately 5-10 tonnes per million USD. Lower copper intensity than power infrastructure, but huge total volume creates substantial aggregate demand. Electrical wiring, plumbing (some markets), and HVAC systems drive copper use.

Road and highway construction: approximately 1-3 tonnes per million USD. Relatively low copper intensity   primarily concrete and steel. Limited copper use in lighting and signaling.

Rail (electric): approximately 8-15 tonnes per million USD. Electric railway traction systems, catenary wire, and station power systems are all copper-intensive.

Solar and wind installation: approximately 4-7 tonnes per million USD for solar (panels + grid connection), 3-6 tonnes per million USD for onshore wind.
