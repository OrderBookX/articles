## The Strategic Reserve Release Mechanism

When commodity prices rise rapidly and the Chinese government judges that inflation or industrial competitiveness is being threatened, the NDRB releases reserves from state stockpiles to increase domestic supply and reduce prices. These releases are typically announced with short notice and create immediate bearish supply surprises.

The most notable recent example: in 2021, China announced sales of copper, aluminum, and zinc from its state strategic reserves   the first state reserve release of industrial metals since 2010. The announcement created a 5-8% COPPERUSDT decline in the days following the announcement. The mechanism: markets had been assuming all Chinese copper demand was being absorbed by commercial buyers; the state reserve release revealed that some apparent demand had been state accumulation that was now being reversed.
