## The ETF Creation/Redemption Mechanism and Physical Gold Demand

When an authorized participant (typically a large investment bank) wants to create new GLD shares, it delivers physical gold to the ETF custodian (HSBC) in exchange for newly created GLD shares. The minimum creation unit is typically 100,000 shares. At approximately $18-20 per share, this requires delivering approximately 100 ounces of gold per 100 shares created (with minor rounding).

The key implication: large institutional inflows to GLD require physical gold purchases. When GLD holdings increase by 10 tonnes in a week (about 3.3 million ounces × spot price in the range of 10 tonnes worth), that physical gold had to be purchased from the market. This physical buying pressure appears in LBMA clearing volumes before it appears in GLD daily holdings data.
