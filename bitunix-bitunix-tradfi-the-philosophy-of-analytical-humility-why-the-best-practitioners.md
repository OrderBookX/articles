## The Calibrated Confidence Model

The best practitioners operate with "calibrated confidence"   confidence that is proportional to the actual quality of the evidence. When the evidence is strong (evidence count 7/8, 12-condition score 9/12, conviction 8/10), confidence is high and position size reflects it. When evidence is moderate (4-5/8, 6-8/12, conviction 6/10), confidence is moderate and position size is reduced proportionally. When evidence is weak, confidence is low and no position is taken.

Calibrated confidence means the practitioner's confidence level correlates with their actual hit rate across many trades. If someone is "always 80% confident" but their hit rate is 55%, their confidence is systematically miscalibrated. If someone's 80% confidence trades win 78% of the time, confidence is calibrated.
