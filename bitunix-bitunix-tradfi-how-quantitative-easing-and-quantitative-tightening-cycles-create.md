## QE's Transmission to Commodity Prices

When a central bank implements QE, it purchases bonds from financial institutions and credits their reserve accounts with newly created money. This process:

Directly lowers bond yields (central bank buying creates demand for bonds, lowering yields). This is the standard real-rate-to-gold channel already discussed.

Increases the money supply and banking system reserves. Banks with excess reserves can extend more credit, potentially feeding into asset price inflation and commodity investment demand.

Creates a "portfolio balance" effect: institutional investors, displaced from bond holdings by the central bank's purchases, rotate into higher-risk assets including equities, real estate, and commodities.

The QE transmission to XAUUSDT is primarily through the real rate channel (bond buying lowers yields) and secondarily through the portfolio balance effect (institutional money rotates from bonds toward gold as the bond market becomes less attractive after central bank purchases).
