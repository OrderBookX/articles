## The Central Bank Reserve Manager's Mandate

A central bank reserve manager's primary objective is not return maximization   it is reserve adequacy, currency stability, and geopolitical risk management. The reserve manager holds assets that can be used to defend the domestic currency, meet external debt obligations, and provide a credible financial backstop in crisis scenarios.

Gold scores uniquely well on the geopolitical risk management criterion because it is the only reserve asset with no counterparty risk from any specific country or institution. A central bank holding US Treasuries has counterparty risk to the United States and the US financial system. A central bank holding gold in its own vaults has no counterparty risk whatsoever.

## The Post-2022 Acceleration in Central Bank Buying

Before 2022, central bank gold purchases were already elevated above historical norms (100-300 tonnes per year in the 2000s had risen to 400-700 tonnes per year in 2018-2021). The 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine and subsequent Western freezing of Russia's $300+ billion in dollar reserves was the catalyst for a structural acceleration: central banks outside the G7 observed that dollar reserves could be frozen by geopolitical decision.

The lesson absorbed by central bank reserve managers globally: any country that might ever come into conflict with the United States or its allies faces the risk of having its dollar reserves frozen. Gold, held in domestic custody, cannot be frozen by external decision.
