## Regime Detection Indicators and Their Signals

Reliable regime detection uses a small set of leading indicators rather than a comprehensive macro model. The yield curve slope (2-year vs 10-year Treasury spread) provides the most reliable recession prediction signal, with inversions typically preceding recessions by 6-18 months. PMI direction (rising vs falling relative to 50) indicates whether growth is accelerating or decelerating. Core CPI trend (rising, stable, falling) indicates the inflation direction component of the regime.

Combining these three indicators creates a regime assessment that can be updated monthly when new data is released. When the yield curve is steepening, PMI is rising, and CPI is moderate, the goldilocks or boom regime is indicated, favoring bitunix copper futures and equity futures. When the yield curve is inverted, PMI is falling, and CPI remains elevated, the stagflation regime is signaled, favoring bitunix gold futures and bitunix silver futures.
