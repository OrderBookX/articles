## Trigger 1: Income Increase

A salary raise, bonus, or new income stream is the most straightforward trigger for a DCA increase. If your disposable income grows by $500 per month, allocating $50 to $100 of that increase to your auto invest plan is proportionally conservative. The key principle is that the increase should feel comfortable. If your new DCA amount ever creates financial stress, you have gone too far.

## Trigger 2: Demonstrated Discipline

After maintaining your plan for at least six months without changing it based on market movements, you have proven to yourself that you can handle the emotional challenge. This demonstrated discipline earns the right to increase your allocation because you know you will maintain it through difficult periods. Increasing before you have this proof is premature. The crypto market will test your resolve in ways you cannot fully anticipate from the outside. Living through a 30% drawdown while your plan keeps buying is the graduation exam that earns you the right to allocate more capital to the strategy. Pass the test first, then scale.

## Trigger 3: Reaching a Milestone

Some investors increase their DCA amount after accumulating a specific number of units or reaching a portfolio value milestone. For example, after accumulating 0.1 BTC through DCA, you might increase your weekly amount to accelerate toward 0.5 BTC. This milestone-based approach provides clear markers for progressive scaling.

## How Much to Increase

Increase in increments of 25% to 50% of your current amount. If you are investing $50 per week, increase to $65 or $75. If you are investing $200 per week, increase to $250 or $300. Gradual increases are psychologically easier to sustain than dramatic jumps because the financial impact adjusts incrementally.

## When Not to Increase

Never increase your DCA amount during a bull market euphoria when it feels like free money. Never increase when your emergency fund is depleted. Never increase because social media makes you feel like you are not investing enough. These triggers lead to overcommitment that collapses during the next downturn. Increase only from positions of financial and emotional stability. The best increases happen during boring, sideways markets when your decision is not influenced by recent price action in either direction. Calm decisions produce sustainable commitments.
