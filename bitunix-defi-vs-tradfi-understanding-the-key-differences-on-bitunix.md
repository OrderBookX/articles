## What Separates TradFi From DeFi

Traditional finance operates through centralized institutions. Banks hold your money. Brokers execute your trades. Regulatory agencies set the rules. Every transaction passes through an intermediary that verifies, processes, and records it. This centralized approach provides consumer protection, dispute resolution, and institutional accountability, but it also creates bottlenecks, limits access to business hours, and charges fees at every layer.

DeFi eliminates intermediaries by using smart contracts on blockchain networks. Users interact directly with protocols rather than institutions. Lending, borrowing, and trading happen peer-to-peer without requiring permission from any central authority. This model offers transparency, 24/7 access, and lower barriers to entry, but it introduces risks including smart contract vulnerabilities, lack of regulatory protection, and the full burden of security on individual users.
