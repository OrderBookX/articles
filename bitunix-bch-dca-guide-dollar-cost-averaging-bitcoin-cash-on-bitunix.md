## The Math Behind DCA

DCA works through a principle called unit cost averaging. When you invest a fixed dollar amount at regular intervals, you automatically purchase more BCH when prices are low and less when prices are high. Over time, your average cost per token tends to be lower than the simple average price during the accumulation period.

Consider a simplified example: investing 100 USDT weekly for four weeks at prices of 10, 8, 12, and 10 USDT per token. Lump sum at week one buys 10 tokens. DCA buys 10 + 12.5 + 8.33 + 10 = 40.83 tokens for the same 400 USDT total. Same investment, more tokens, lower average cost.
