## What Counts as a Tradeable Supply Shock?

Not every supply disruption is a trading opportunity. The events worth positioning around in bitunix commodity futures are those that affect significant percentages of global production and cannot be immediately offset by alternative sources. A single mine closure representing 0.1% of global copper production is not a supply shock. A hurricane shutting down 15% of US Gulf Coast oil production for two weeks is a genuine supply shock for bitunix oil futures. A nationwide strike at Chilean copper mines affecting 10% of global output is a genuine supply shock for COPPERUSDT.

The key criteria: affected supply percentage must be meaningful (typically more than 2-3% of global output), the duration must be uncertain (open-ended disruptions create more premium than fixed-duration closures), and alternative sources cannot immediately substitute.
