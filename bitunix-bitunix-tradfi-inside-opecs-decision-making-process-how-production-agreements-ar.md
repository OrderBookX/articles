## The Principal-Agent Problem Inside OPEC

Every OPEC member faces a fundamental tension: what is best for OPEC as a collective (restraint to support prices) versus what is best for each member individually (produce as much as possible at the high price that the collective restraint creates). This is the classic cartel stability problem   each individual member has an incentive to free-ride on the collective's restraint.

The countries with the lowest fiscal breakeven oil price (the oil price needed to balance their government budget)   typically Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Kuwait   can afford to maintain restraint even at lower prices because they have substantial fiscal reserves. Countries with high fiscal breakevens   typically Venezuela, Nigeria, and Iraq   face immediate fiscal pressure to produce at maximum capacity regardless of OPEC agreements.
