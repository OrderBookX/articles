## The Bretton Woods System (1944-1971): The Last Dollar-Gold Link

By the late 1960s, the US had created more dollar claims than its gold reserves could satisfy, creating the fundamental imbalance that Nixon closed in August 1971 by ending dollar-gold convertibility.

The consequence for gold: in the decade after Bretton Woods ended, gold rose from $35 to $850 per ounce, an approximately 24x appreciation as markets repriced gold from its artificial fixed rate to its free-market equilibrium.
