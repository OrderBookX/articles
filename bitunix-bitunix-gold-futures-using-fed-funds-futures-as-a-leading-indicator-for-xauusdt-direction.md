## What Are Fed Funds Futures and Where to Find the Data?

Fed funds futures are contracts traded on the CME that reflect market expectations for the Fed's target interest rate at specific future dates. The CME FedWatch Tool (available free at the CME Group website) synthesizes these futures prices into probability percentages for rate outcomes at upcoming FOMC meetings.

For example, if the FedWatch Tool shows an 80% probability of a rate cut at the next FOMC meeting, the market has already priced a significant portion of the cut into asset prices including gold. If the Fed then cuts as expected, the response is typically muted because it was already priced in. The surprise scenario where the Fed cuts more than expected, or does not cut when expected, produces the largest XAUUSDT moves.
