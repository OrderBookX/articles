**Student:** Okay. What's the pro-gold-standard argument?

**Mentor:** At its core: without a commodity anchor, fiat currency systems have a systematic bias toward monetary expansion that creates long-run inflation. The historical evidence supports this   every major fiat currency system has eventually experienced periods of significant inflation or hyperinflation. Gold standard advocates argue this is not coincidence but causality: the absence of external monetary discipline leads to politically motivated money printing.

**Student:** And the counter-argument?

**Mentor:** The gold standard prevented necessary monetary responses to economic crises. The 1929-1933 Great Depression was significantly worsened by the gold standard constraining the Federal Reserve's ability to provide emergency liquidity. Countries that abandoned the gold standard earlier (UK in 1931) recovered faster than those that maintained it longer (USA until 1933). The standard forced deflation onto economies at precisely the moments when expansion was needed.
