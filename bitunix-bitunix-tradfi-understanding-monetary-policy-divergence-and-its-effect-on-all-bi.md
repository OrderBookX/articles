## The Four Divergence Scenarios and Their Instrument Implications

Scenario 1   Fed hiking while others easing (US rates highest):
The dollar strengthens as capital flows to the highest-yielding currency. XAUUSDT faces dual headwinds (rising real rates, rising dollar). COPPERUSDT faces headwind from dollar strength but may find partial offset if the easing central banks (ECB, BOJ) are stimulating growth in their regions.

Scenario 2   Fed cutting while others holding (US rates declining relative to others):
The dollar weakens as the rate differential compresses. XAUUSDT benefits from both falling real rates and falling dollar simultaneously. COPPERUSDT benefits from dollar weakness making copper cheaper for international buyers. This scenario (Fed pivot while other central banks are slow to follow) is typically the most favorable environment for the entire commodity complex.

Scenario 3   Fed and ECB hiking simultaneously (global synchronized tightening):
Dollar direction is ambiguous (both safe-haven demand and cross-currency flows in play). Real rates are rising globally. XAUUSDT faces headwinds from rising real rates across all major economies simultaneously. COPPERUSDT faces headwinds from global demand concern. This is typically a "risk-off, avoid commodities" environment.
