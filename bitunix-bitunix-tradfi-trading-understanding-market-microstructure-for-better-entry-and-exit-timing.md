## Limit Orders Versus Market Orders

Using limit orders for entries reduces execution cost compared to market orders by ensuring you transact at a specific price rather than accepting whatever price the market offers at the moment of entry. The trade-off: limit orders may not fill if the market moves away from your entry price.

For trend-following bitunix tradfi trading entries, a strategy that limits entry to either specific support/resistance levels or to the first pullback after a breakout ensures natural limit order fill opportunities without chasing market price. This approach captures better average entry prices than market orders at breakout points.
