## Tariff Effects on COPPERUSDT: Direct and Indirect Channels

Direct channel: when the US imposes tariffs on Chinese manufactured goods, Chinese export manufacturers reduce production   directly reducing electricity consumption, machine operation, and eventually new factory investment. All three are copper-intensive. A 25% tariff on $500 billion of Chinese goods reduces Chinese export manufacturing activity by an estimated 2-4%, which reduces Chinese copper demand by approximately 500,000-1,000,000 tonnes annually (roughly 2-4% of global supply). This is a meaningful COPPERUSDT demand headwind.

Indirect channel: Chinese retaliatory tariffs on US agricultural exports reduce US farmer income, which reduces US rural economic activity including construction and machinery purchases. This is a smaller secondary copper demand reduction from the US side.

The counter-channel: supply chain diversification (building new factories in Vietnam, Mexico, India as alternatives to China) creates new infrastructure investment in third countries   which requires copper. This supply chain diversification copper demand typically emerges 2-4 years after the tariff is imposed, creating a delayed offsetting demand boost.
