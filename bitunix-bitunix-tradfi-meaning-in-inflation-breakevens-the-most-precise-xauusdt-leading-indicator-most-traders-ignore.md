## What Inflation Breakevens Measure and Where to Find Them

The 5-year and 10-year inflation breakeven rates are calculated as the difference between nominal Treasury yields and TIPS yields of the same maturity. A 5-year nominal yield of 4.2% minus a 5-year TIPS yield of 1.9% implies a 5-year breakeven inflation rate of 2.3% (the market expects 2.3% annual inflation over the next five years).

When breakevens rise (market expecting more inflation), real yields fall even if nominal yields are unchanged, which is bullish for XAUUSDT. When breakevens fall (market expecting less inflation), real yields rise even if nominal yields are unchanged, which is bearish for XAUUSDT.

The leading indicator property: breakevens respond faster to changes in market inflation expectations than the official TIPS yield adjusts because breakevens are forward-looking market forecasts while TIPS yields incorporate actual adjustment periods. A breakeven that rises sharply signals future TIPS yield compression before the TIPS market has fully absorbed the new inflation expectations.

## The 5-Year vs 10-Year Breakeven Signal

The 5-year breakeven typically moves before the 10-year breakeven because short-term inflation expectations are more sensitive to current economic data. When the 5-year breakeven rises above the 10-year breakeven (the breakeven curve inverts toward near-term inflation), the market is pricing in a near-term inflation surge with an expectation that it will moderate longer-term. This pattern historically precedes XAUUSDT appreciation because near-term inflation is the most immediate real rate pressure on gold.
