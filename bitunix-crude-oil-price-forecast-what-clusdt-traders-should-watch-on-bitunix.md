## Macro Factors Behind the Crude Oil Price Prediction

The WTI crude oil forecast is heavily influenced by US inventory data released weekly by the EIA. Draws in stockpiles tend to push prices higher, while unexpected builds create selling pressure. Meanwhile, the Brent crude oil forecast tracks global supply dynamics more closely, reflecting production changes from OPEC+ and emerging market demand growth.

Interest rate policy also matters. When central banks tighten monetary policy, the stronger dollar that often results puts downward pressure on crude oil prices. Conversely, rate cuts can weaken the dollar and support a bullish crude oil price forecast.
