## What Is TradFi's Thesis Construction Starting Point?

What is TradFi's first analytical question? It is not "will gold go up?" It is "what are gold's primary drivers right now and what direction are they pointing?" The professional analyst begins with the driver framework, not with an opinion.

The driver inventory for XAUUSDT: real interest rates (TIPS yields), US dollar direction (DXY), monetary policy trajectory (Fed funds rate path implied by futures), central bank demand (IMF COFER data, World Gold Council quarterly), speculative positioning (COT managed money), physical demand trend (GLD ETF flows, Indian import data), and supply outlook (mine production from major producers plus recycling).

The analyst assigns a directional signal (+1 bullish, 0 neutral, -1 bearish) to each driver based on current data. Sum the scores. A score of +5 or higher represents a high-conviction bull thesis.

## What Is TradFi's Thesis Stress-Test Process?

What is TradFi's most important analytical discipline? After building the initial thesis, the analyst actively constructs the bear case. The question is not "what could go right" but "what scenario would make this thesis completely wrong?"

For a XAUUSDT bull thesis, the stress test asks: what if inflation falls faster than expected (Fed cuts rates less than priced, real rates stay elevated)? What if China's economy recovers strongly (risk appetite rises, safe-haven demand falls)? What if the dollar strengthens for non-Fed-policy reasons? Each stress scenario is assigned a probability. If any single stress scenario with meaningful probability (above 20%) would cause losses that exceed the acceptable maximum drawdown, the position size must be reduced.
