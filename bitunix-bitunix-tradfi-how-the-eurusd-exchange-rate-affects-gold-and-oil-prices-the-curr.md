## EUR/USD and European Gold Demand

When EUR/USD falls (dollar strengthens, euro weakens), gold priced in euros rises even if the dollar price of gold is unchanged. A European investor who bought gold when EUR/USD was 1.15 and EUR/USD falls to 1.05 sees their gold position up approximately 9.5% in euro terms   a significant gain entirely from currency movement.

The demand effect of this: euro-denominated gold price appreciation stimulates profit-taking by European holders (selling gold when it has risen sharply in their home currency), which can create near-term XAUUSDT selling pressure. Conversely, when EUR/USD rises (euro strengthens), gold falls in euro terms even if the dollar price is unchanged   European investors see their gold positions declining and may buy more at the perceived "lower" euro price.
