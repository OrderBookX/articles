## What Fiscal Dominance Means Precisely

In a standard monetary policy framework, the central bank raises interest rates when inflation is above target, regardless of the impact on government borrowing costs. In a fiscal dominance scenario, the debt-to-GDP ratio is high enough that interest rate increases significantly increase debt service costs   potentially triggering a fiscal crisis that is worse than the inflation problem the rate hike was meant to address.

At US public debt of approximately $34 trillion (2024) at an average maturity of approximately 5-7 years, a 1% increase in average borrowing costs adds approximately $340 billion annually to debt service. When debt service is already consuming 15%+ of federal revenue, further rate increases become fiscally dangerous   creating implicit pressure on the central bank to tolerate higher inflation rather than raise rates aggressively.
