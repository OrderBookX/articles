## Why Solar Demand Matters More for Silver Now Than Ever

Silver is used as conductive paste in photovoltaic solar panels. Each solar panel uses a small but measurable amount of silver in its electrical contacts and bus bars. As global solar panel installations have grown from tens of gigawatts annually a decade ago to hundreds of gigawatts currently, the cumulative silver demand from this sector has grown to represent a significant and growing fraction of total annual silver consumption.

The Silver Institute estimates that photovoltaic applications now represent over 15% of total annual silver demand, up from under 5% a decade ago. At current solar installation growth trajectories, this percentage continues rising. This structural demand growth provides a floor under silver prices that did not exist in previous decades.
