## Financial Readiness

Confirm that you have an emergency fund covering at least three months of expenses before allocating any money to crypto DCA. Your auto invest amount should come from genuinely disposable income that you will not need for at least 12 months. If a 50% decline in your invested amount would cause financial stress, reduce the amount until it would not.

## Thesis Documentation

Write down in one or two sentences why you are investing in each asset in your plan. For Bitcoin, it might be long-term store of value driven by fixed supply and halving cycles. For Ethereum, it might be smart contract platform growth driving demand for the native token. If you cannot articulate a thesis, you should not include that asset. Keep this document accessible for quarterly reviews.

## Plan Parameters

Define your cycle amount, frequency, and asset allocation before touching the interface. Making these decisions inside the setup page leads to impulsive choices driven by whatever price shows on the screen at that moment. Decide offline, then execute the plan as decided.

## Exit Criteria

Before you start buying, define when you will stop buying or start selling. This might be a return target, a time horizon, a portfolio size, or a life event like buying a house. Without exit criteria, you will hold forever or sell randomly, both of which produce suboptimal outcomes compared to a planned exit.

## Review Schedule

Block one day per month in your calendar for plan review. This prevents both daily obsessive checking and months-long neglect. Set a reminder if necessary. Consistency in monitoring matches the consistency of the investment itself.

## Tax Preparation

Set up a spreadsheet or tracking tool to record every purchase from day one. Export transaction data monthly and store it securely. This takes five minutes per month and prevents hours of reconstruction during tax season.

## Emotional Preparation

Accept in advance that your portfolio will be underwater at some point. This is not a possibility. It is a certainty. The market will decline, your plan will keep buying, and your portfolio value will temporarily drop below your total invested amount. Accepting this before it happens reduces the emotional shock when it does.
