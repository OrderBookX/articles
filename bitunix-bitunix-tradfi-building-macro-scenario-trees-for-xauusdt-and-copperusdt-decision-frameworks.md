## The Structure of a Macro Scenario Tree

A scenario tree starts from the current macro state and branches into two to three possible paths for each key variable over the next six months. For XAUUSDT analysis, the two primary branching variables are: Federal Reserve rate path (cut vs hold vs hike) and US economic conditions (soft landing vs hard landing vs no landing/reacceleration). These two variables with three possible states each create nine possible scenario combinations.

Simplification for practical use: combine the most likely and adjacent scenarios into three broad paths:

Bull path for XAUUSDT (probability: assign based on current macro assessment): Fed cuts rates, economic conditions soft-land. Real rates fall. XAUUSDT target: +15-25%.

Base path (probability): Fed holds rates, economic conditions continue gradual moderation. Real rates flat to mildly positive. XAUUSDT target: -5% to +5%.

Bear path for XAUUSDT (probability): Economic reacceleration forces Fed to resume hiking. Real rates rise. XAUUSDT target: -10 to -20%.

The probabilities assigned to each path should sum to 100% and reflect your current macro assessment. If you assign 50% to bull, 35% to base, 15% to bear, the expected XAUUSDT return is: (50% x 20%) + (35% x 0%) + (15% x -15%) = 10% + 0% - 2.25% = 7.75% expected return.
