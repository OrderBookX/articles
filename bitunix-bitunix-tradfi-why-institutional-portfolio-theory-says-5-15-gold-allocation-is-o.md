## The Core Portfolio Theory Argument: Diversification Into Negative-Beta Assets

In the Capital Asset Pricing Model, any asset with negative beta relative to equities reduces portfolio risk while maintaining some expected return. Gold has historically exhibited near-zero to modestly negative equity correlation during crisis episodes   meaning it tends to rise or hold steady when equity markets fall sharply.

Adding an asset with near-zero or negative equity correlation to a portfolio that is otherwise equity-heavy reduces portfolio volatility without proportionally reducing expected returns. This is the mechanical justification for gold allocation: the risk reduction it provides exceeds the return reduction, improving the Sharpe ratio of the combined portfolio.
