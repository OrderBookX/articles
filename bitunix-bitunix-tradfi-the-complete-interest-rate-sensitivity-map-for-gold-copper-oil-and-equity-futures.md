## XAUUSDT: Maximum Rate Sensitivity

XAUUSDT is the most rate-sensitive instrument in the bitunix tradfi suite. Real rates (TIPS yields) have the strongest documented relationship: historically, a 100 basis point rise in 10-year TIPS yields has been associated with approximately 15-20% XAUUSDT decline, while a 100bp TIPS yield decline has been associated with 15-25% XAUUSDT appreciation.

Nominal rate sensitivity is lower than real rate sensitivity because what matters for gold is the real opportunity cost of holding it versus interest-bearing assets. When nominal rates rise but inflation rises faster (real rates fall), gold appreciates despite higher nominal rates.

## COPPERUSDT: Indirect Rate Sensitivity Through Growth

COPPERUSDT is less directly sensitive to interest rates than gold. The transmission mechanism is indirect: higher rates slow economic growth (reducing copper demand) and strengthen the dollar (making dollar-priced copper more expensive for non-dollar buyers). The net rate sensitivity for copper is negative but moderate: a 100bp Fed rate hike typically corresponds to 8-12% COPPERUSDT decline through the growth and dollar channels, not through the direct real rate mechanism.

When rates rise because the economy is overheating (positive growth rates), copper's demand benefit from strong growth partially offsets the rate headwind. When rates rise because inflation is high but growth is weak (stagflation), the headwinds from growth slowdown and dollar strength compound.

## TSLAUSDT: Highest Rate Sensitivity Among Equity Derivatives

Technology and growth equities like Tesla are particularly rate-sensitive because their value is concentrated in future cash flows discounted at the current rate. A 100bp rate increase raises the discount rate applied to future Tesla earnings and FSD option value, reducing the present value and therefore TSLAUSDT. Tesla's sensitivity to rate changes is among the highest in the S&P 500, making rate cycle awareness critical for TSLAUSDT positioning.
