## The Cascade: Three Banks in Five Days

SVB's failure was caused by a specific combination: it held a large portfolio of long-duration US Treasury bonds (purchased when rates were near zero) that had lost significant market value as rates rose in 2022-2023. When depositors (primarily tech sector companies) began withdrawing funds at an accelerating rate, SVB was forced to sell the bond portfolio at large losses, which triggered a bank run that accelerated the failure.

The failure was announced pre-market on Friday March 10. By Monday March 13, Signature Bank had been closed and First Republic Bank was in serious distress. The FDIC and Treasury Department announced a guarantee of all SVB deposits (including uninsured deposits above $250,000) on the same Sunday night to prevent a broader bank run.
