## What Makes a Strategy Truly Passive

A passive strategy is not about ignoring your portfolio forever. It means removing the need for daily decisions. You define your allocation, set your schedule, and let the system execute. You review performance periodically, perhaps monthly or quarterly, but you do not make reactive changes based on short-term price movements. The goal is accumulation over time, not speculation on direction.

## The Psychology Problem That Automation Solves

When markets rally, greed pushes them to buy more than planned at inflated prices. This emotional cycle produces worse returns than a simple mechanical schedule. ## Avoiding the Most Common Mistake

The biggest mistake in passive investing is making it active. If you adjust your plan every time the market moves 5%, you are not running a passive strategy anymore. You are day trading with extra steps. Write down why you started the plan. Tape it to your monitor if necessary. Only adjust when your fundamental thesis about an asset changes, not when price moves trigger an emotional response.
