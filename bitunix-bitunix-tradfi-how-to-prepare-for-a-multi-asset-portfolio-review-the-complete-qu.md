## The Two-Hour Quarterly Review Structure

Part 1 (30 minutes)   Performance Attribution:

For each position closed during the quarter: calculate P&L, maximum adverse excursion (how far against the position it moved before either reversing or stopping out), and maximum favorable excursion (how far in favor before the exit). Compare the actual P&L to the maximum favorable excursion   if the actual P&L is significantly below the MFE, the position management (exit timing) could be improved.

Calculate instrument-level Sharpe ratio for the quarter (if enough trades): P&L divided by standard deviation of individual trade outcomes.

Identify the top three best-performing and worst-performing decisions. What distinguished them?

Part 2 (20 minutes)   Analytical Accuracy Assessment:

For each directional thesis (bullish or bearish) expressed during the quarter: did the primary driver move as expected? If the primary driver moved correctly but the position lost money, execution or position sizing is the issue. If the primary driver did not move as expected, analytical forecasting is the issue.

Calculate "thesis accuracy rate": what percentage of your directional calls were correct? Below 40% suggests the primary driver framework needs refinement. Above 60% suggests analytical edge is present.
