## Target-Based Exits

The simplest exit strategy is setting a price or return target before you start. For example, you might decide to sell 25% of your Bitcoin position when your total return exceeds 100%, another 25% at 200%, and hold the remaining 50% indefinitely. This tiered approach captures profits at multiple levels without requiring you to predict the exact top.

## Time-Based Exits

Another approach is selling based on time rather than price. After completing a four-year DCA cycle that spans an entire Bitcoin halving period, you might sell a percentage of your position regardless of price. This removes the emotional difficulty of picking a price target and replaces it with a mechanical rule.

## Cycle-Based Exits

More advanced investors time their exits to Bitcoin's halving cycle. They reduce positions during the euphoric phase that typically occurs 12 to 18 months after each halving and resume aggressive DCA during the subsequent bear market. This requires more market awareness than pure mechanical DCA but can significantly enhance returns for investors who understand cycle dynamics.

## The Common Mistake

The most frequent exit mistake is having no plan at all. Without predetermined rules, you will either sell too early because a 50% gain feels like enough, or hold too long because a 200% gain might become 300%. Both outcomes are driven by emotion rather than strategy. Write your exit rules before you start your auto invest plan. Commit them to paper and follow them regardless of how the market makes you feel.

## Partial Exits Preserve Optionality

Never sell your entire position at once unless your thesis has completely changed. Partial exits capture profits while maintaining exposure to further upside. A common approach is selling no more than 20% to 30% of your position at any single exit trigger. This ensures you participate in continued growth while securing realized gains.

## Reinvestment After Exit

When you sell a portion of your DCA position, you have three options with the proceeds. Reinvest into a different asset through a new auto invest plan. Hold as stablecoins for future deployment. Or withdraw to fund real-world goals. Define this in advance as part of your exit plan.
