## Risk Management for the Silver-Gold Spread

The primary risk in the silver-gold spread trade is that the ratio continues to move to more extreme levels before reverting, creating interim losses in the spread position. When entering a silver-gold spread at a 95:1 ratio, the ratio could move to 110:1 before reverting, creating a significant interim loss before the ultimate mean reversion profit is realized. Managing this extension risk requires position sizing that accommodates a further 20-30% movement in the ratio without triggering margin-driven liquidation.

Historical data shows that the ratio has rarely sustained extreme readings above 100:1 for more than six months, providing a rough time horizon for position holding requirements at the most extreme entry levels.
