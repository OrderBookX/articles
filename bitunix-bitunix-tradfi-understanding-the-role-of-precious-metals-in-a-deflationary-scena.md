## Gold in Deflation: The Historical Evidence

The 1930s Great Depression   the most severe deflationary episode in modern history   produced counter-intuitive gold behavior: because the dollar was pegged to gold at a fixed price, gold's purchasing power rose with deflation (as general price levels fell, each ounce of gold bought more goods). But this was under the gold standard, which no longer applies.

In the modern fiat money system, the relevant question is: in a deflation scenario, what happens to real rates? If deflation is driven by demand collapse (recession), nominal rates are typically cut aggressively by central banks   approaching zero or turning negative. Inflation expectations fall, but nominal rates fall even further   keeping real rates at low or negative levels despite deflationary pressures.

The modern deflationary analog: Japan 1995-2015 saw sustained deflation combined with near-zero nominal rates. Real rates fluctuated between mildly positive and mildly negative. Gold in yen terms significantly appreciated during this period as Japanese investors sought non-yen assets.
