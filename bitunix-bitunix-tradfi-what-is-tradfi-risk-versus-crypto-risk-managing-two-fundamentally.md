## What Is TradFi Risk Characterized By?

TradFi commodity risk is characterized by fundamental supply-demand volatility: physical production disruptions, seasonal demand patterns, inventory cycle dynamics, and monetary policy effects on monetary metals. These risks are: (1) generally well-documented in free public data (EIA reports, LME inventory, COT reports), (2) gradual in their escalation (inventory trends build over weeks), and (3) predictable in their correlation structure (gold and copper have measurable historical correlations to real rates and PMI respectively).

The maximum typical drawdown for a well-managed single-commodity TradFi position over any 30-day period is 15-25% for gold, 20-35% for copper and oil. These are volatile but not uniquely extreme.
