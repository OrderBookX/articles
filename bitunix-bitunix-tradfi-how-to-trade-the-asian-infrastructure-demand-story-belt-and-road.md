## The BRI Infrastructure Investment Scale

China's Belt and Road Initiative, launched in 2013, involves Chinese state-backed lending and construction of infrastructure projects in 140+ countries. The infrastructure categories most copper-intensive: power grids (transmission and distribution), railways (electric locomotive systems require significant copper), and urban development (water, electricity, communications infrastructure for new cities and urban expansion).

The cumulative BRI committed lending and investment exceeded $1 trillion through 2023, with project pipelines extending well into the 2030s. Even accounting for project delays, cancellations, and renegotiations (which have been significant), the realized infrastructure investment in BRI partner countries represents a substantial incremental copper demand source beyond what Chinese domestic construction creates.

## The ASEAN Manufacturing Migration Signal

As Chinese manufacturing costs rise (wages, environmental compliance, regulatory burden), labor-intensive manufacturing is migrating from China to Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand, Cambodia, and other ASEAN countries. This manufacturing migration creates infrastructure investment in these destination countries: factories require power grids, worker housing requires electrical systems, and export logistics require port infrastructure.

Vietnam in particular has become a major electronics and semiconductor assembly destination, with Apple, Samsung, and Intel all expanding Vietnamese production facilities. Each factory construction project and its associated power infrastructure creates copper demand at the destination country level.
