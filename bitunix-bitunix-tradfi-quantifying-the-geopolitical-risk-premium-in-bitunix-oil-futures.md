## The Quantification Framework: Supply Disruption Probability × Disruption Magnitude

The correct analytical question for any geopolitical event affecting oil is not "is this bullish or bearish?" but "what is the probability-weighted supply disruption in barrels per day, and how does that compare to current global spare capacity?"

Example: Russia-Ukraine escalation risk. Russia exports approximately 5 million barrels per day (bpd) of oil. In a maximum disruption scenario, 100% of Russian exports are lost (5 million bpd). The probability of this maximum scenario might be 5-10%. The probability-weighted supply risk: 5,000,000 × 0.075 = 375,000 bpd.
