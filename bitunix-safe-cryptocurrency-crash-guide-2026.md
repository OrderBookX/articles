Cryptocurrency crashes are sudden, significant declines in the price of digital assets, often sparked by market downturns, regulatory changes, or technological failures. Several factors can lead to a *cryptocurrency crash*:

- **Bear Market Conditions:** Prolonged periods of falling prices and pessimistic sentiment.
- **Capitulation:** Investors selling off assets at a loss, often out of fear.
- **Regulatory Changes:** New laws or regulations that impact the cryptocurrency market.
- **Technological Failures:** Issues with blockchain technology or major exchange outages.
