## Copper in Electrolyzer Systems

Electrolyzers, which split water into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity, contain copper in their power electronics, transformers, rectifiers, and distribution infrastructure. A 100 MW green hydrogen facility requires approximately 100-200 tonnes of copper in its electrical components alone, before accounting for the renewable energy generation (solar or wind) and grid connection copper.

The EU's Green Deal targets 40 GW of electrolyzer capacity by 2030 and 500 GW by 2050. At 1.5 tonnes of copper per MW of electrolyzer capacity, these targets imply 60,000 tonnes and 750,000 tonnes of copper respectively, the latter representing approximately 3.5% of current annual global copper production.

The hydrogen distribution infrastructure, when built at scale, multiplies the copper demand from electrolyzer capacity alone.
