## How to Monitor Treasury Market Functioning

The ICE BofA MOVE Index (from Article 344) is the primary measure of bond market volatility. But below-the-surface Treasury market stress can be detected through more specific indicators:

Off-the-run to on-the-run spread: newly issued ("on-the-run") Treasury bonds trade at a small liquidity premium versus older bonds of the same maturity ("off-the-run"). In normal markets, this spread is 5-10 basis points. When the spread widens above 30 basis points, primary dealer balance sheet capacity is stressed   a systemic liquidity signal.

Treasury repo market rates: the Secured Overnight Financing Rate (SOFR, published daily at newyorkfed.org, free) measures the cost of overnight borrowing collateralized by Treasury securities. When SOFR spikes significantly above the Fed funds rate (above 25bp above the fed funds upper bound), repo market stress is present   Treasury collateral transformation is disrupting.

Treasury auction tail: when the US Treasury conducts weekly auctions, the "tail" measures how far above the secondary market yield the Treasury had to price the auction to attract sufficient demand. A 3+ basis point auction tail indicates weak demand for Treasury supply   a potential fiscal sustainability signal.
