## How YCC Works and Why It Is Explosive

Under YCC, the central bank buys unlimited quantities of government bonds to prevent yields from rising above the target. This unlimited buying expands the central bank's balance sheet without limit and forces the currency lower relative to peers whose central banks are not doing YCC. Japan's Bank of Japan maintained YCC for years with the BOJ's balance sheet expanding to over 130% of GDP.

The explosive dynamic: YCC suppresses bond yields artificially below market-clearing levels. When the central bank eventually abandons or widens the YCC band (which always happens eventually because the cost becomes unsustainable), pent-up yield adjustment occurs rapidly. Japanese 10-year yields that were capped at 0.25% jumped to 1.0% and higher within months of YCC policy changes, creating massive repricing across global fixed income markets.
