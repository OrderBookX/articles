Understanding Bitcoin as a High-Beta Asset
------------------------------------------

A high-beta asset is one that exhibits greater price volatility compared to the overall market. Bitcoin's beta coefficient, ranging from 2.5 to 3.0, indicates that it is significantly more responsive to market changes than traditional assets. This characteristic is particularly evident in its correlation with the *M2 money supply* and global liquidity conditions.

Bitcoin's price movements are highly sensitive to liquidity cycles, often acting as a **leveraged play on liquidity**. During periods of increased liquidity, such as the *Federal Reserve*'s rate-cutting cycles, Bitcoin tends to surge, as seen when its price skyrocketed from $40,000 to $126,000 between 2024 and 2025. Conversely, during liquidity tightening phases, Bitcoin is often the first asset to experience liquidation.

<figure class="inline-image">![Visual representation of Bitcoin's price movement during liquidity cycles, highlighting the surge from $40,000 to $126,000.](https://cirrus-media.bintangjtobing.workers.dev/inline-images/2025/12/27/b64c90d8-d0d6-4c80-b256-79cfb389d41b.jpg)</figure>### Correlation with M2 Money Supply

Bitcoin's correlation coefficient with the M2 money supply stands at 0.78, highlighting its strong relationship with liquidity. This means that as the global money supply increases, Bitcoin tends to rise in value, positioning it as a prime **risk-on asset** during expansive monetary policy periods.
