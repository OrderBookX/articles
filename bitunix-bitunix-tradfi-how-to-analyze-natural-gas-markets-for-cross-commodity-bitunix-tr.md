## The Natural Gas-Oil Price Relationship

Natural gas and crude oil share production infrastructure   many oil wells produce associated gas alongside crude. When oil production is high (OPEC+ compliance weak), associated gas production is also high   creating potential natural gas supply surplus that depresses natural gas prices and reduces the energy cost advantage that gas-intensive industries (aluminum, chemicals, fertilizer) enjoy.

Cheap natural gas is competitively advantageous for European industry (reducing energy costs for manufacturers)   which supports European industrial production and European copper and silver demand. Conversely, when European natural gas prices are high (as they were in 2021-2022 after Russian supply disruptions), European industrial production contracts, reducing copper demand from European manufacturers.

The monitoring signal: European TTF gas price (tradingeconomics.com, free, daily) as a proxy for European industrial energy cost. When TTF is above €50/MWh, European industrial margins are compressed and production is declining. When TTF falls below €30/MWh, European industrial competitiveness is restored.
