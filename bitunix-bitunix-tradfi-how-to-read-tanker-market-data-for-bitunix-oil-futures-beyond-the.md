## The Five Most Informative Tanker Routes for Oil Intelligence

TD3C (VLCC, Middle East Gulf to China): the most important single tanker route in global oil markets. Very Large Crude Carrier rates from Ras Tanura/Kharg Island to Ningbo/Qingdao measure Saudi and Iranian crude flow to China   the world's largest oil import market. When TD3C rates are rising, Chinese crude oil demand is increasing faster than Middle East supply can accommodate at current prices.

TD7 (Aframax, North Sea to Continent): reflects North Sea and Caspian crude flow to European refineries. When TD7 rises while WTI-Brent spread is widening, European refineries are paying up for North Sea crude   demand-driven tightness in European refinery crude markets.

TD25 (MR Tanker, US Gulf to Latin America): medium-range tanker rates for US Gulf Coast refined product exports to Latin America. Rising TD25 with widening crack spreads signals robust US product export demand   confirming strong US domestic refinery operations.

TD20 (VLCC, West Africa to China): West African crude flow to China. Complements TD3C   when both are rising simultaneously, China is pulling crude from both the Middle East and West Africa simultaneously. Broad-based Chinese crude demand surge signal.

TD9 (Aframax, Baltic to UK Continent): Baltic crude (Russian, Latvian, Lithuanian cargoes pre-2022, more complex post-sanctions) to northern European ports. Post-2022, this route reflects how European refineries are sourcing crude alternatives to Russian supply.
