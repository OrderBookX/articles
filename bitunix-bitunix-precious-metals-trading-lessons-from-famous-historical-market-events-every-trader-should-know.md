## Lesson 1: The Hunt Brothers Silver Squeeze (1979-1980)

Nelson Bunker Hunt and Herbert Hunt attempted to corner the silver market by accumulating physical silver and futures contracts until they controlled an estimated one-third of global silver supply. Silver rose from approximately $6 per ounce in 1979 to nearly $50 per ounce by January 1980, before rules changes by the COMEX exchange that restricted silver futures purchases caused the price to collapse back to $10 within two months.

The lesson for bitunix silver futures via XAGUSDT: extreme silver price spikes driven by physical market corners rather than fundamental demand eventually collapse when the corner is broken. When silver appreciates 5-10x within months, the probability of rule changes or forced selling by leveraged participants increases dramatically. Managing position size and taking profits during extreme upside is more important in silver than in gold due to the silver market's smaller size and historical susceptibility to extreme positioning events.

## Lesson 3: The 2011 Silver Peak

Silver reached $49.51 per ounce in April 2011, an all-time high that has not been revisited. The rally was driven by a combination of post-2008 monetary stimulus, physical silver investment demand, and speculative momentum. Five consecutive COMEX margin increases over 8 days in May 2011 forced leveraged longs to liquidate, sending silver from $49 to $32 in two weeks.

The lesson for XAGUSDT trading: in rapidly rising silver markets, exchange margin increases can suddenly and sharply amplify liquidation pressure. Monitoring COMEX margin levels during silver's rapid appreciation periods provides early warning of potential forced liquidation events that can reverse gains quickly.
