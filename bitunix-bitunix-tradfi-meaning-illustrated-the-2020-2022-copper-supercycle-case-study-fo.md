## The Setup: Three Simultaneous Catalysts

The 2020-2022 copper bull had three simultaneous catalysts that are rare to find aligned at the same time.

Catalyst one was the China stimulus response to COVID. In mid-2020, China launched its largest infrastructure program since the 2008-2009 response, directing hundreds of billions of dollars into power grids, rail networks, and housing. China's copper consumption, already 55% of the global total, accelerated sharply. Monthly copper imports into China hit records not seen in a decade.

Catalyst two was the green energy transition announcement wave. In late 2020, the European Union's Green Deal, the United States' Build Back Better framework, and China's dual carbon targets were all announced within months of each other. Each program explicitly required enormous quantities of copper for grid upgrades, EV manufacturing, and renewable energy infrastructure. Analysts revised copper demand forecasts upward by 20-40%.

Catalyst three was the supply pipeline vacuum. Years of underinvestment in new copper mine development (during the 2012-2020 low-price period) meant there was no significant new supply ready to respond to accelerating demand. LME inventory fell from 250,000 tonnes to below 50,000 tonnes   a level not seen since the 2011 copper peak.
