## How Silver Enters Solar Panels and How Much Is Used

Modern photovoltaic solar panels use silver paste as the electrical contact material on the front of solar cells. The silver paste forms the grid lines that collect and conduct electrical current from the cell. Current silver loading per gigawatt of solar capacity: approximately 0.5-0.8 tonnes of silver per GW (the range reflects variation by cell technology).

At 2024 global solar additions of approximately 500 GW: solar silver demand = 500 GW × 0.65 tonnes/GW average = approximately 325 tonnes. Total global silver mine supply is approximately 25,000-26,000 tonnes annually. Solar represents approximately 1.3% of mine supply from solar demand alone.
