## The PPI-to-CPI-to-XAUUSDT Chain

The transmission: when producers face rising input costs, they eventually pass those costs forward to consumers. The PPI for final demand (available on FRED as FRED series PPIFDG) measures prices producers charge for finished goods. When PPI for final demand rises sharply for 2-3 consecutive months, CPI will typically rise with a 2-4 month lag as those higher costs are passed through to retail prices.

The XAUUSDT implication: a sharply rising PPI for final demand, when combined with flat or lagging CPI, creates a predictable forward inflation signal. TIPS breakevens (which respond to expected CPI) will rise within 2-4 months of the PPI surge.
