## Sizing Hedges Appropriately

The fundamental error in portfolio hedging is over-hedging: paying too much insurance premium on a position that does not actually need full protection. Hedges should be sized to reduce but not eliminate exposure to the risk being hedged, and should be held only as long as the risk condition that triggered them remains present.
