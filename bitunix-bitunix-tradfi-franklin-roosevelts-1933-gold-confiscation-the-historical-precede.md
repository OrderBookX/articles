## The 1933 Context: Why Confiscation Was Deemed Necessary

The Gold Standard was creating a deflationary death spiral in 1933. Banks were failing across the United States. Depositors were converting paper dollars into gold, which they then hoarded or shipped abroad. The government's gold reserves were falling as the banking system collapsed. Roosevelt's executive order was designed to stop the gold drain and give the Federal Reserve the flexibility to expand the money supply.

The economic logic was coherent even if the confiscation was coercive: the gold standard was preventing the monetary stimulus needed to escape the Depression. Ending private gold ownership was the first step toward ending the gold standard constraint.
