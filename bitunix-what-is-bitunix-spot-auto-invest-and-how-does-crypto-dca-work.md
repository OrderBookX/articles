## How Crypto DCA Actually Works

Dollar-cost averaging means investing a fixed amount into an asset at regular intervals. Instead of allocating your full budget at one price point, you spread purchases across weeks or months. When prices drop, your fixed amount buys more units. When prices rise, it buys fewer. Over time, this smooths out your average entry price and reduces the impact of short-term volatility.

The concept is simple. The execution is where most people fail. They intend to buy regularly but skip buys during uncertainty or chase buys during hype. Automation solves this consistency problem by removing human hesitation from the process entirely.

## What DCA Does and Does Not Do

DCA reduces timing risk. It does not eliminate market risk. If the assets you are accumulating decline over a sustained period, your portfolio will still lose value. The advantage is that your average cost will be lower than if you had bought everything at the top. This is not a shortcut to guaranteed returns. It is a consistency tool that removes emotional decision-making from the buying process.
