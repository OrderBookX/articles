## The Physical Oil Trader's Information Hierarchy

First priority: real-time physical market data. The oil trader's terminal shows live tanker positions (via Kpler or Vortexa), current refinery utilization rates, spot physical differentials between grades, and real-time pipeline flows. This data is hours to days ahead of any official statistics.

Second priority: short-term supply-demand balance. The physical trader calculates a weekly balance: production volumes (from OPEC production estimates, EIA weekly production data, North Sea daily loading schedules) versus consumption (refinery intake, petrochemical demand, export flows). When the weekly balance shows supply growing faster than consumption, physical prices will weaken within days.

Third priority: inventory positioning relative to seasonal norms. Is Cushing storage above or below its 5-year seasonal average? Is floating storage (oil held in anchored tankers as de facto storage) rising or falling? These inventory signals tell the physical trader whether the current supply-demand balance is building or drawing from the inventory buffer.
