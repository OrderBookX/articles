## The Five Major Economy Real Rate Map

The real interest rate for each country can be approximated as: central bank policy rate minus 1-year inflation expectation (using market-derived breakevens where available, or recent CPI where not).

United States: Federal funds rate minus 10-year TIPS breakeven. Most directly tracked by FRED series DFII10.

Eurozone: ECB deposit rate minus 10-year Bund inflation-linked breakeven. Available through ECB Statistical Data Warehouse (free at sdw.ecb.europa.eu).

Japan: Bank of Japan policy rate (near zero) minus CPI (which has been rising toward 3% in 2023-2024). Bank of Japan's own negative real rate environment is highly supportive for Japanese domestic gold demand.

China: PBOC 1-year LPR (loan prime rate) minus CPI trend. China's real rates have been relatively positive, partially explaining why PBOC gold accumulation is a policy decision rather than just a negative real rate response.

India: RBI repo rate minus CPI. India's inflation has often exceeded the RBI repo rate, creating persistent negative real rates that sustain the structural consumer gold demand.
