## The Two-Step Position Sizing Method

Professional precious metals position sizing always starts with the stop-loss, not the desired position size. Here is the two-step method:

Step 1: Define the maximum acceptable dollar loss for this trade. Common practice is 1-2% of total account equity per trade. If your account is $10,000 USDT, your maximum acceptable loss is $100-$200 per trade.

Step 2: Work backward from the maximum acceptable loss and your stop-loss distance to determine the position size. If gold is at $2,000 and your stop-loss is at $1,960 (a 2% stop), and your maximum acceptable loss is $200, then your maximum position size is calculated as: $200 divided by ($2,000 x 2%) = $200 / $40 = 5 contracts (each tracking $1 of gold price movement). ## The Correlation Position Sizing Adjustment

If you hold multiple bitunix tradfi trading positions that are positively correlated, such as long XAUUSDT and long XAGUSDT simultaneously, the correlation means that both positions will lose money at the same time if the thesis is wrong. This requires treating the correlated positions as a single position for maximum aggregate risk calculation.

If your maximum per-trade risk is 2% of account, and you hold both XAUUSDT and XAGUSDT longs simultaneously, the combined position should represent no more than 2-3% of total account risk, not 4% (2% each separately).
