The financial world is increasingly defined by the relationship between two systems that were once considered opposites. TradFi and DeFi represent different philosophies about how financial services should be delivered, but the boundaries between them are becoming blurred as technology evolves and market demands shift.

## TradFi: The Established System

TradFi encompasses every financial service delivered through centralized institutions under regulatory oversight. When you deposit money in a bank, buy shares through a broker, or take out an insurance policy, you are using TradFi infrastructure. The system prioritizes stability, legal accountability, and consumer protection. Central banks act as lenders of last resort, deposit insurance programs protect savers, and regulatory agencies enforce rules that prevent market manipulation and fraud.

## DeFi: The Decentralized Alternative

DeFi removes intermediaries by automating financial functions through smart contracts on blockchain networks. Users can lend, borrow, trade, and earn yield without needing approval from any institution. The system operates 24/7, requires no minimum balances, and provides full transparency through on-chain transaction records. However, DeFi also places the entire burden of security on users, offers no recourse for errors or exploits, and remains largely unregulated.
