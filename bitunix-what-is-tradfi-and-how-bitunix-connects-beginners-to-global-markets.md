## TradFi Basics Everyone Should Know

Traditional finance operates through centralized institutions that act as intermediaries. When you deposit money in a bank, the bank holds it and lends a portion to others. When you buy stocks through a broker, the broker executes the trade on an exchange and holds the securities in your account. Regulators like the SEC, FCA, and local authorities set the rules that these institutions must follow. This centralized structure provides consumer protection and dispute resolution, but it also limits access to business hours, charges fees at multiple layers, and excludes billions of people who cannot meet minimum requirements.
