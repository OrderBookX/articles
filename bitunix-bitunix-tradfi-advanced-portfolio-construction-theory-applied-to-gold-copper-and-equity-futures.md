## Beyond Mean-Variance: The Limitations of Markowitz for Commodity Portfolios

Harry Markowitz's mean-variance optimization assumes that asset returns are normally distributed and that historical correlations are stable forward. Both assumptions fail in commodity futures markets: returns exhibit fat tails (extreme events happen more frequently than normal distributions predict), and correlations are regime-dependent (different in crisis periods than in normal periods).

## Tail Risk Management: Beyond Sharpe Ratio

Sharpe ratio measures return per unit of volatility but does not capture tail risk: the probability and magnitude of extreme negative outcomes. Maximum Drawdown, Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR), and downside deviation are tail risk measures that complement Sharpe ratio for complete portfolio assessment.

CVaR (also called Expected Shortfall) measures the average loss during the worst X% of outcomes. A portfolio with a Sharpe ratio of 1.2 but a CVaR that exceeds your total trading capital at the 1% confidence level has a risk profile that is unacceptable regardless of the Sharpe ratio.
