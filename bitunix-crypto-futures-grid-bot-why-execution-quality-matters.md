Crypto Futures Grid Bot — Why Execution Quality Matters

A crypto futures grid bot places far more trades than a typical manual strategy. Where a day trader might execute 5 to 10 trades per session, a grid bot with 30 levels can complete dozens of cycles per day during active oscillation. This trade frequency makes execution quality critically important.


## Three Components of Execution

Execution quality has three components: fill rate (do your orders get filled?), fill price (at the exact level you intended?), and fill speed (without delay?). In a deep, liquid market, all three are strong. In a thin market, orders sit unfilled, execute at worse prices, or experience meaningful slippage.


## How Slippage Compounds

Over a single trade, a 0.02% slippage seems negligible. Over 500 trades in a month, that's 10% of your theoretical profit lost to execution friction. For grid operators running multiple bots over extended periods, this compounds into a significant drag.

## Impact During Volatile Moves

Liquidity also affects how the bot handles rapid price movements. During sudden drops or spikes, a liquid order book absorbs large orders without causing exaggerated price impact. A shallow order book amplifies moves, potentially pushing price through multiple grid levels in a single candle and reducing the bot's ability to capture intermediate profits.
