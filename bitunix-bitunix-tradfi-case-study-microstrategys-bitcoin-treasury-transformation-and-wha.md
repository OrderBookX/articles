## The Decision: Why Michael Saylor Made the Bet

In August 2020, Michael Saylor held a corporate cash position of approximately $500 million earning near-zero yield in money market instruments. With the Federal Reserve committing to near-zero rates and unlimited quantitative easing, Saylor concluded that cash was "melting ice"   losing purchasing power faster than it was earning yield.

The calculation: if holding cash was guaranteed to lose real value at 10-15% per year (through a combination of yield below inflation plus monetary debasement), then any alternative store of value with positive expected return was preferable. Saylor selected Bitcoin as the alternative, deploying $250 million initially at an average price of approximately $11,000.
