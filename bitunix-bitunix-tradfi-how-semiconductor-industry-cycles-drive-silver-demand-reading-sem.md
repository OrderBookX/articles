## Silver's Role in Semiconductor Manufacturing

Silver's unique electrical conductivity makes it the preferred material for specific semiconductor applications: wire bonding (connecting semiconductor die to package leads in older packaging technologies), electrical contacts in MEMS devices, conductive adhesives and solder pastes for chip mounting, and packaging of power semiconductors.

Global semiconductor industry silver consumption: approximately 50-60 million troy ounces annually (roughly 12-15% of total industrial silver demand). This demand follows the semiconductor CapEx and production cycle closely.

## The SEMI Book-to-Bill Ratio as XAGUSDT Leading Indicator

SEMI (Semiconductor Equipment and Materials International) publishes the monthly North America semiconductor equipment book-to-bill ratio (free at semi.org): the ratio of new equipment orders (bookings) to equipment deliveries (billings). This ratio measures the capacity expansion momentum of semiconductor manufacturers.

Book-to-bill above 1.0: semiconductor manufacturers are ordering more equipment than is being delivered   expansion cycle. New fabs being built or expanded require silver-intensive installation and setup.

Book-to-bill above 1.10 for three consecutive months: strong semiconductor CapEx expansion. New manufacturing capacity coming online in 6-12 months will consume silver in setup and production ramp. Forward silver demand from semiconductor is rising.

Book-to-bill below 1.0: capacity expansion has stopped or reversed. Semiconductor silver demand from new fabs will plateau or decline in the coming months.
