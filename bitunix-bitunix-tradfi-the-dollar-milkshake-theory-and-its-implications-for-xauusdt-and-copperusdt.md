## The Core Thesis: Why the Dollar Gets Stronger in a Crisis

The milkshake analogy works like this: the world's central banks have all been printing their own currencies (adding liquidity to the global milkshake), but the US dollar straw is the longest and widest because global debt and global trade are predominantly denominated in dollars. When global financial stress hits and dollar-denominated debt holders need dollars to repay obligations, they sell everything else to buy dollars. This forced dollar buying creates the dollar spike that hurts commodities priced in dollars.
