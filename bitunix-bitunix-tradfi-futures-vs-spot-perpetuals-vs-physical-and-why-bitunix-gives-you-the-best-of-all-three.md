## Physical Gold: The Original TradFi Asset

Physical gold provides permanent, counterparty-free ownership of a real asset. You can hold it regardless of what happens to any financial institution, exchange, or government. During extreme systemic risk scenarios, physical gold is the ultimate store of value because it requires no trusted intermediary.

The practical limitations: physical gold requires secure storage (typically costing 0.1-0.5% annually), insurance, and a dealer relationship for buying and selling with significant bid-ask spreads. It cannot be leveraged. It cannot be shorted. Price appreciation on physical gold during bear markets is impossible to capture.

Best suited for: Long-term wealth preservation investors who prioritize counterparty-free ownership over capital efficiency and trading flexibility.

## COMEX Gold Futures: The Traditional TradFi Approach

COMEX gold futures on the CME are the benchmark for institutional gold price discovery. They provide leverage (typically 5-20:1 for retail), the ability to go short, and standard contract sizes. They require rolling every two to three months before contract expiration, which creates transaction costs and operational complexity.

The practical limitations: COMEX futures require a US futures brokerage account, are closed during certain hours, have minimum contract sizes of 100 troy ounces (approximately $200,000 nominal), and require careful management of contract expiry dates to avoid physical delivery.

Best suited for: Institutional traders and experienced retail traders with US brokerage accounts who want regulated exchange access.
