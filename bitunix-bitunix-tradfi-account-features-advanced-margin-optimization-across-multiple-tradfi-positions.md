## The Opportunity Cost of Idle Margin

Every dollar of USDT sitting unused in your bitunix tradfi trading account has an opportunity cost: it is earning zero nominal return while being available for trades that could be generating positive returns. Idle margin is not inherently bad (maintaining the 30% cash buffer is essential risk management), but idle margin above the buffer represents unexploited capital efficiency.

Identifying why margin is idle is the first step to optimization. Common causes: waiting for a perfect setup that never arrives (perfectionism), uncertainty about which instrument to add next (decision paralysis), or holding excess cash buffer beyond what the risk management framework requires (conservative overcorrection after a drawdown).

## Efficiency Technique 2: Using Unrealized Profits as Partial Margin

When existing bitunix tradfi trading positions have accumulated significant unrealized profits, these unrealized gains are available in cross margin mode as additional margin for new positions. Strategically using these unrealized gains as margin for highly correlated new positions (adding to an existing XAUUSDT long with a new XAGUSDT long when both are supported by the same macro trigger) deploys the floating margin efficiently.

Risk management caveat: unrealized profits are not realized until the position is closed. If the market reverses sharply before you realize the floating gain, both the original position and the position funded by floating margin may face simultaneous drawdowns. Limit this technique to positions where the macro thesis provides high confidence in the relationship between the two instruments.
