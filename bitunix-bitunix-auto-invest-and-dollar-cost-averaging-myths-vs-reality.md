## Myth: DCA Always Beats Lump-Sum Investing

Reality: In a market that trends upward over the long term, lump-sum investing at the beginning outperforms DCA on average because more capital is exposed to the uptrend for a longer period. DCA wins when the market is volatile or declining during the investment period. The reason DCA is still recommended for most investors is not because it maximizes returns mathematically. It is because it minimizes the behavioral mistakes that destroy returns in practice.

## Myth: DCA Eliminates Risk

Reality: DCA reduces timing risk by spreading purchases across many price points. It does not reduce market risk. If you DCA into an asset that declines permanently, you lose money with a lower average cost than a lump-sum buyer, but you still lose money. DCA is a risk management tool, not a risk elimination tool.

## Myth: You Should DCA Into Everything

Reality: DCA works best with high-conviction assets that have long-term viability. Not every cryptocurrency deserves recurring investment. Most altcoins will not survive a full market cycle. DCA into assets that are likely to appreciate over years, not into every token that captures attention on social media.

## Myth: Frequency Matters a Lot

Reality: The difference between daily, weekly, and monthly DCA over a multi-year period is typically within 2% to 5% of total return. The far more important factor is simply doing DCA at all rather than optimizing the frequency. An imperfect plan executed consistently beats a perfect plan debated indefinitely.

## Myth: DCA Is Set and Forget Forever

Reality: DCA requires periodic review. Not of price or short-term performance, but of the fundamental thesis behind each asset in your plan. Markets change. Projects fail. Regulations shift. A quarterly review of your plan's asset selections is not emotional interference. It is responsible management.
