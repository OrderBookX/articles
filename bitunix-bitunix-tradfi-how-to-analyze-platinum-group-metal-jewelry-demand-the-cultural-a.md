## China's Platinum Jewelry Market: The Critical Variable

China represents approximately 30-40% of global platinum jewelry demand   the single largest national jewelry market for platinum. Chinese platinum jewelry preferences have been historically driven by:

Cultural affinity for white metals: in China, platinum's white color is associated with purity and luxury. At lower relative price levels versus gold (when platinum is cheaper than gold, as has been the case since approximately 2015), platinum jewelry offers high-perceived-luxury at lower absolute price.

Price point sensitivity: Chinese platinum jewelry demand has shown high sensitivity to the platinum-gold price ratio. When platinum trades at less than 80% of the gold price, Chinese platinum jewelry demand has historically increased as consumers trade up from gold jewelry at similar price points. When platinum approaches gold parity or exceeds it, Chinese demand weakens as the price premium reduces the value proposition.
