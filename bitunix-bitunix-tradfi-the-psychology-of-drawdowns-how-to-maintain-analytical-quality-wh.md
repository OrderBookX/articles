## The Three Stages of Drawdown Psychology

Stage 1   Initial drawdown (position down 1-3%): minimal psychological impact for practitioners with properly sized positions. The designed stop-loss is still distant. Analytical quality is unaffected. The action: nothing. Continue the weekly monitoring review as planned.

Stage 2   Significant drawdown (position down 3-5%, approaching the stop-loss): the first significant psychological pressure emerges. The practitioner begins questioning the thesis. Three specific cognitive traps appear:

Anchoring to entry price: the irrational feeling that the position "must" return to entry price before the market can resume trending. This prevents rational assessment of the current evidence.

Thesis revision without new evidence: rationalizing continued holding by reinterpreting existing evidence more favorably. The data has not changed   only the desired conclusion has changed. This is the most dangerous cognitive error in drawdown management.

Phantom liquidity concern: the feeling that exiting now will "lock in" a loss that would otherwise recover. In reality, a loss is a loss whether realized or unrealized   and an unrealized loss in a position with invalidated thesis is a future realized loss.

Stage 3   Stop-loss triggered (position at or beyond defined stop level): the thesis invalidation condition has been met. The analytical decision is already made   this was defined at entry. The psychological challenge is executing the exit without adding "just a little more time to recover." No exceptions. Execute.
