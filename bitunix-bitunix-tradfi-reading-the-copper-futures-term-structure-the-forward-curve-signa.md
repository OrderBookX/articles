## How to Read the Three-Dimensional Copper Term Structure

The LME copper forward curve has three key points: the cash price (immediate delivery), the 3-month price (most liquid reference), and the 15-month or 27-month price (longer-term market consensus). The relationships between these three points encode specific information:

Cash-to-3-month spread (the spot-to-forward spread): This is the primary measure of physical market tightness. When cash is at a premium to 3-month (backwardation), physical buyers need copper now and are paying up. When 3-month is at a premium to cash (contango), the market is comfortable with current supply and expects future supply to remain adequate.

3-month-to-15-month spread (the medium-term curve): This spread reflects the market's consensus about whether current supply-demand conditions will persist, tighten, or ease over the next 12 months. When the 15-month trades at a significant discount to the 3-month in backwardation (steep backwardation curve), the market expects current physical tightness to persist. When the 15-month is near parity or at a premium to the 3-month (flat or contango forward section), the market expects supply to normalize.
