## Loss Aversion Bias

Humans feel the pain of losses roughly twice as intensely as the pleasure of equivalent gains. When your DCA portfolio drops 20%, the emotional impact is stronger than the satisfaction of a 20% gain. This asymmetry drives investors to stop their plans during drawdowns even though continued buying at lower prices is mathematically optimal. Recognizing this bias in yourself is the first step toward preventing it from controlling your investment decisions. You will feel the urge to stop. Name it as loss aversion when it happens.

## Recency Bias

Whatever happened last week feels like what will happen forever. A two-week rally makes you feel like the market will never drop again. A two-week crash makes you feel like the market will never recover. Recency bias distorts your risk assessment and tempts you to adjust a plan that was designed for multi-year execution based on multi-week emotions.

## Social Comparison

When someone on social media posts 50x returns from a meme coin trade, your steady 15% annual DCA return feels inadequate. This comparison ignores survivorship bias. For every person posting their win, hundreds quietly absorbed losses on the same trade. Your DCA plan will never produce viral screenshots. It will produce consistent, sustainable wealth accumulation that compounds quietly over years while most social media traders cycle between windfall gains and devastating losses.

## Commitment Devices

The best defense against psychological sabotage is making it difficult to interfere with your plan. Write a personal investment policy statement before starting. Define the conditions under which you will modify your plan, specifically excluding price movements. Share your commitment with a trusted friend who will hold you accountable. Check your portfolio no more than once per month. Every additional check creates an opportunity for emotional interference that your plan was designed to prevent. Distance from data creates space for discipline.

## The Paradox of Automation

The irony of auto invest is that the tool is perfectly disciplined while the human controlling it is not. The bot will never skip a purchase, never panic sell, never chase a pump. Your only job is to not interfere with the process that is already optimized for consistency.
