**Student:** I've been thinking about trading the relationship between XAUUSDT and XAGUSDT rather than just either metal individually. Is the gold-silver ratio a tradeable spread?

**Mentor:** Absolutely, and it's one of the most historically reliable mean-reversion trades in commodity markets. The ratio has a long-run tendency to return toward its historical mean (approximately 65-75:1 in the modern era) from both extremes. When the ratio reaches above 90:1 (silver extremely cheap relative to gold), the mean reversion trade   long silver, short gold in equal dollar terms   has historically produced positive returns over 6-12 month horizons.

**Student:** And the same logic applies at the other extreme?

**Mentor:** When the ratio falls below 40:1, gold is extremely cheap relative to silver. Historically, this has occurred during the blow-off phases of major silver bull markets (the Hunt Brothers 1980, the WallStreetBets 2021 silver squeeze). At those extremes, gold has tended to outperform silver as the ratio mean-reverts.
