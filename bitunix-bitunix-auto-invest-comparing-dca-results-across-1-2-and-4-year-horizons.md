## One Year Horizon

After one year of weekly DCA, you have 52 purchase points. Your results depend heavily on whether the year was bullish, bearish, or sideways. In a bull year, DCA produces positive returns but underperforms a lump-sum at the start. In a bear year, DCA reduces your losses compared to lump-sum but your portfolio is likely still negative. One year is too short to judge DCA effectiveness because the result is dominated by a single market condition.

## Two Year Horizon

Two years typically captures at least one significant trend change. If you started during a bull market, you experienced the subsequent correction. If you started during a bear market, you experienced some recovery. With 104 purchase points, your average cost is more meaningful and less dependent on any single entry. Most two-year DCA plans into Bitcoin have produced positive returns historically, though the magnitude varies significantly by starting point.

If your plan is three months old and showing a 15% loss, that data point tells you nothing about where you will be in three years. Short-term DCA results are noise. Long-term DCA results are signal. Making decisions based on noise produces worse outcomes than making no decisions at all. Commit to evaluating your plan only on timeframes that match its design intent.
