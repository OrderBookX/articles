What Are Perpetual Futures?
---------------------------

Perpetual futures are a type of futures contract that, unlike traditional futures, have no expiration date. This allows traders to hold positions indefinitely, making them a popular choice for those who wish to manage long-term market exposure. The absence of an expiration date is balanced by a *funding rate*, which helps to keep the perpetual futures prices aligned with the spot market.

---------------------------------

- **No Expiration Date:** Traders can hold positions indefinitely without the need to roll over contracts.
- **Funding Rates:** These periodic payments between long and short position holders ensure price alignment with the spot market.
- **Leverage:** Perpetual futures allow both long and short positions with leverage, amplifying potential gains and losses.
- **Liquidation:** Occurs when a trader's margin falls below the maintenance requirement, enforcing risk management.

Understanding the Funding Rate
------------------------------

The **funding rate** is a crucial mechanism in perpetual [futures trading](https://www.bitunix.com?vipCode=BITUNIXBONUS&utm_source=3rdparty&utm_medium=blog-article&utm_campaign=labsnews). It is a recurring payment exchanged between long and short traders, maintaining the perpetual contract's price in line with the *spot price*. When the funding rate is positive, long position holders pay shorts, and vice versa when it is negative. This system incentivizes traders to maintain market equilibrium.

Leverage and Risk Management
----------------------------

Leverage in **perpetual futures** trading allows traders to control larger positions with a smaller amount of capital, amplifying both potential gains and risks. Proper risk management is essential, and traders must be aware of the **liquidation** price and manage their **margin** accordingly to avoid forced position closures.
