## The TIC Data for US Treasury and Agency Flow Intelligence

The US Treasury International Capital (TIC) system (free at ticdata.treasury.gov, monthly with 6-week lag) tracks foreign purchases and sales of US Treasury securities, agency bonds, corporate bonds, and equities. The most relevant data for bitunix tradfi pairs:

Foreign official sector Treasury flows: when foreign central banks and governments are net sellers of US Treasuries, they are reducing their dollar reserve holdings   the dedollarization signal measured with actual transaction data rather than stated intentions. Consistent official sector Treasury selling over 3+ consecutive months is a verified structural dedollarization signal that directly supports the XAUUSDT structural thesis (if they are selling Treasuries, what are they buying instead? Foreign private sector Treasury flows: when private foreign investors are net buyers of Treasuries, global risk appetite is directed toward US fixed income   typically associated with dollar strength and gold headwinds. When private foreign investors are net sellers, capital is moving out of US assets   potentially toward real assets including gold.

## The BIS Locational Banking Statistics for Copper Demand Finance Intelligence

The BIS Locational Banking Statistics (free at bis.org/statistics, quarterly) track cross-border bank lending by currency and counterparty country. The data relevant for COPPERUSDT: dollar credit extended to Chinese non-bank entities. When dollar credit to Chinese borrowers is growing rapidly, Chinese companies are expanding their dollar-financed operations   potentially including commodity purchases and inventory financing.

When Chinese dollar credit is contracting (Chinese companies repaying dollar loans), the reverse occurs: Chinese commodity purchasing power is being reduced as dollar credit is unavailable. This is the same "dollar tightening = commodity demand headwind" mechanism that affected emerging markets in every dollar strengthening cycle.
