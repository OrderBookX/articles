## Limit Orders: The Primary Precision Tool

Limit orders execute only at a specified price or better, never worse. For XAUUSDT entries, a limit buy order placed at a specific support level fills only when price reaches or goes below that level, ensuring you enter at the planned price regardless of how quickly the market moves through the entry zone.

The limit order discipline: if the market moves through your limit price without filling (price gaps past the level), do not chase with a market order. If the analysis justified a limit entry at $2,100, the trade at $2,115 has a different risk-reward profile and may not meet the minimum criteria. Missing a limit entry is acceptable; chasing with a market order at a worse price undermines the risk management framework.

## Stop-Limit Orders: The Enhanced Stop

A stop-limit order triggers like a stop-loss (at a specified adverse price) but executes as a limit order (only at the stop price or better) rather than a market order. This prevents the "stop gap" problem where price gaps through a stop-loss level in a fast market, causing execution significantly worse than the planned stop price.

The trade-off: in extremely fast markets, stop-limit orders may not fill if price moves through the limit price range without finding a counterparty. For most normal market conditions, stop-limit orders provide better execution than market stop-losses.

## OCO Orders: The Complete Entry Management Tool

One-Cancels-the-Other (OCO) orders place a take-profit limit order and a stop-loss order simultaneously. When either order fills, the other is automatically cancelled. OCO orders automate the position management process: enter the trade, set the OCO, and the system handles both profit capture and loss prevention automatically without requiring manual monitoring.

OCO orders are the most practical implementation of disciplined profit-taking and stop-loss management because they eliminate the human decision of "when to exit" for both profitable and unprofitable scenarios.

## Iceberg Orders for Large Positions

Iceberg orders display only a portion of the total order size in the order book, preventing market impact from large positions. For institutional-sized bitunix tradfi trading positions, breaking a large order into multiple smaller visible portions reduces the signaling effect that causes other participants to trade against the large order.

## Time-in-Force Specifications

Good-Till-Cancelled (GTC): Order remains active until filled or manually cancelled. Use for limit entries at specific price levels that may take days or weeks to reach.

Immediate-or-Cancel (IOC): Order fills immediately at the best available price, any unfilled portion is cancelled. Use when you need to enter quickly without leaving a resting order in the book.
