# Futures Grid Strategy — The Weekend Trader's Playbook

Not everyone can monitor markets daily. If you have a full-time job and limited screen time, futures grid trading can work — but only with a specific approach built around your schedule constraints.

## The Saturday Setup Ritual

Spend 30 to 45 minutes every Saturday morning analyzing the week's price action. Which pairs consolidated? Where did support and resistance hold? What does the upcoming week look like in terms of macro events (Fed meetings, CPI data, protocol upgrades)?

### Identify Grid Candidates
Look for pairs that spent most of the week in a defined range with at least 3 touches of both support and resistance. These are your grid candidates. BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT are the safest defaults.

## Sunday Deployment

Configure and launch your grids on Sunday evening when you have uninterrupted time to set parameters carefully. Use conservative settings: 3x leverage, range based on the prior 2 weeks of price data, 20 to 30 grids, and always enable TP/SL. These safety settings are non-negotiable for a trader who can't check charts during work hours.

## Monday–Friday: The 5-Minute Check

Before work each morning, spend exactly 5 minutes checking your bot dashboard. Three numbers: grid profit, unrealized PnL, total PnL. If everything looks normal, close the app and go to work. If a bot is paused or total PnL is deeply negative, flag it for review after work.

## When to Intervene

Only stop a bot mid-week if market structure has clearly changed — a breakout with volume, a major unexpected event, or SL triggering. Don't tinker based on temporary moves. ## The Reserve Capital Rule

Keep 30% of your grid trading capital uninvested at all times. This reserve serves two purposes. First, it is available for new grid opportunities that appear mid-week — a pair that suddenly enters perfect consolidation. Second, it protects your portfolio if a running grid hits SL. Without reserves, every SL trigger forces you to reduce active capacity. With reserves, you can redeploy immediately without downsizing.
