## The Three Narrative Relationships to Fundamentals

Alignment (narrative reinforces fundamentals): a correctly bullish fundamental setup (TIPS yields declining, gold scorecard +6, LME copper inventory in Stage 3) is simultaneously supported by a market narrative ("global central banks are easing, copper demand from AI infrastructure is unstoppable"). When narrative and fundamentals align, price momentum typically extends further and faster than fundamentals alone would justify   because human investors are bidding prices based on both the analytical reality AND the compelling story.

Amplification risk: when narrative is strongest (the "copper is going to $20,000 per tonne" headlines), the fundamental setup may still be valid but the market is pricing in the narrative's most extreme version. This is typically coincident with crowded COT positioning (everyone has already acted on both the fundamental AND the narrative). Entry here is late in the cycle.

Divergence (narrative lags or conflicts with fundamentals): when the narrative is still bearish ("the Fed will never cut, gold is dead") while the fundamental driver has already turned bullish (TIPS yields declining for 6 consecutive weeks), the narrative divergence creates the highest-quality entry opportunity   the practitioner who acts on the fundamental signal before the narrative catches up benefits from the full narrative-to-fundamental convergence move.

Narrative-to-fundamental divergence (narrative bullish, fundamentals bearish): equally dangerous. When headlines scream "the copper supercycle is unstoppable" while LME inventory is entering Stage 1 building and PMI new orders are declining for three months, the narrative has diverged above the fundamentals.
