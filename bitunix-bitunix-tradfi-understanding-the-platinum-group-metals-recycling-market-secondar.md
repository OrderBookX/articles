## The Vehicle Fleet Age and the Recycling Pipeline

Catalytic converters contain PGMs that are recovered when vehicles reach end-of-life and are scrapped. The PGM loading in each converter depends on when the vehicle was manufactured   older vehicles had higher loadings (pre-thriftification technology) while newer vehicles have lower loadings (efficiency improvements reduced loadings over time).

The critical insight: the vehicles being scrapped today were manufactured 12-18 years ago (the average vehicle age at scrapping). In 2024, vehicles being scrapped were manufactured approximately 2006-2012. During that period, palladium loadings in gasoline vehicles were at their highest historical levels (before the most recent round of thriftification). This creates a "peak recovery cycle" where palladium recycling volumes are elevated due to the high-loading vehicles entering the scrap stream.

Approximately 2028-2033, the vehicles reaching end-of-life will be from the 2015-2020 vintage   when palladium loadings began declining significantly. XPDUSDT secondary supply will therefore decline from the recycling channel starting approximately 2028-2033 as the high-loading vehicles age out of the scrap stream.

## The Geographic Recycling Concentration

Approximately 65% of global automotive PGM recycling occurs in the United States and Europe, where mandatory end-of-life vehicle recycling infrastructure is well-developed. In emerging markets   where vehicle scrappage rates are lower and informal recycling is common   PGM recovery rates are substantially lower.
