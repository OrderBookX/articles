## Why Rare Earths Matter for Copper and Platinum Analysis

Electric vehicle motors require significant rare earth elements including neodymium and dysprosium for permanent magnets. These same EVs also require copper for wiring and potentially platinum for fuel cells in PHEV applications. When rare earth supply disruptions threaten EV production timelines, the downstream effects on copper and platinum demand projections are significant.

China controls approximately 60% of global rare earth production and over 85% of rare earth processing capacity. Chinese export restrictions on rare earths (implemented periodically as geopolitical tools) create immediate supply constraints for EV manufacturers globally.
