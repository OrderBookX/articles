The CFTC publishes two versions of the Commitment of Traders report: the Legacy report (three categories: commercial, non-commercial, small speculator) and the Disaggregated report (four categories: producer/merchant/processor/user, swap dealers, managed money, other reportables). For XAUUSDT practitioners who have mastered the Legacy report, the Disaggregated report provides additional precision that identifies whether the speculative positioning signal is being driven by hedge fund directional bets (managed money) or institutional swap dealers hedging structural exposures.

## The Four Disaggregated Categories and Their Signals

Producer/Merchant/Processor/User (PMPU): These are physical market participants   gold miners hedging future production, gold jewelers hedging input cost risk, gold refiners managing inventory. When PMPU is net long (unusual   they normally hedge by being short), it signals that physical market participants expect prices to rise and are buying rather than hedging. This is a rare and very high-conviction bullish XAUUSDT signal.

Swap Dealers: These are large financial institutions that take opposite positions to their clients' commodity derivative positions. Swap dealer positioning often reflects the aggregate of client demand for hedging exposure. When swap dealers are net long, their institutional clients are net short (hedging long physical exposures). When swap dealers are net short, clients are net long (buying gold exposure through swaps from institutions).

Managed Money: This is the primary speculative category   hedge funds and commodity trading advisors making directional bets. The managed money percentile rank (position relative to 5-year history) is the classic COT signal discussed in earlier articles.

Other Reportables: A catch-all category including non-traditional market participants. Less analytically informative than the other three.
