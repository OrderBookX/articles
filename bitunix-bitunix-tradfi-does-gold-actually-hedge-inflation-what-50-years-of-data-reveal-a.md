## The Statistical Evidence: Gold's Inflation Correlation Is Not What You Think

Over short periods (1-3 years), gold has poor correlation with CPI inflation. In 2021, when US CPI rose from 1.4% to 7%, gold rose only 0.2% for the year. In 2022, when CPI peaked at 9.1%, gold actually fell 4%. By the simple "inflation hedges means gold rises when inflation rises" interpretation, gold failed spectacularly in the most significant inflation spike in 40 years.

Academic research confirms this. A 2020 study in the Journal of International Money and Finance found that gold's correlation with CPI over 3-year horizons is approximately 0.15   statistically insignificant as a short-term inflation hedge.

## What Gold Actually Hedges: Expected Real Rates, Not CPI

The more precise statement is: gold hedges against scenarios where real interest rates are negative or becoming more negative. This is not the same as hedging inflation.

In 2021-2022, inflation was rising sharply but so were nominal interest rates (the Fed began hiking). Real rates (nominal rates minus inflation expectations) actually rose from -1.5% TIPS yields to near 0% by end of 2022   meaning bondholders were less disadvantaged relative to gold than the previous year. Gold fell because real rates were rising, not because inflation was rising.
