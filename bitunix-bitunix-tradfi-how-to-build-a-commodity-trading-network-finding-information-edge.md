## The Critical Distinction: Intelligence vs. Material Non-Public Information

Building community intelligence networks must remain within clear ethical and regulatory boundaries. Using publicly available community insights   research reports, public conference presentations, published analyst calls, and public commentary   is legitimate information gathering. Using information obtained from corporate insiders about unreleased financial results or operational data is material non-public information and is illegal to trade on in most jurisdictions.

The practical rule: if the information was publicly available (even if you found it in an obscure source), it is legitimate to trade on. If it came from someone with a fiduciary or employment relationship to the relevant company, verify that it is also publicly available before acting.
