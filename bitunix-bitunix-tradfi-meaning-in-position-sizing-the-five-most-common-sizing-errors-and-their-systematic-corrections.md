## Sizing Error 1: Using Round Numbers Instead of Volatility-Adjusted Sizes

## Sizing Error 2: Sizing Up After Winning Streaks

The error: after three consecutive winning trades, the trader increases position sizes, believing they are "hot" and their edge is temporarily elevated. Behavioral finance research consistently shows that winning streaks are poor predictors of future performance; they reflect both skill and randomness.

The correction: position size is determined by the analytical framework (setup quality, conviction level, regime alignment) and account equity, never by recent performance. A four-trade winning streak does not change the analytical quality of trade number five.

## Sizing Error 3: Ignoring Correlation in Portfolio Sizing

The error: sizing each position independently without considering that multiple simultaneous positions in correlated instruments create combined risk multiples of each individual position's risk. Long XAUUSDT and long XAGUSDT are not two independent 1% risk positions; they are one 2% risk position in the precious metals risk factor.

The correction: when adding a new position, calculate its correlation to existing positions. If correlation is above 0.6, treat the new position's risk as additive to the correlated existing position. Do not exceed 3-4% of account in any single correlated risk factor group.
