## How Gold Mining Stocks Provide Operational Leverage

A gold mining company that mines gold at an all-in sustaining cost of $1,200/oz and sells at $2,100/oz has a profit margin of $900/oz. When gold prices rise from $2,100 to $2,300 (+9.5%), the profit margin rises from $900 to $1,100   a 22% margin increase from a 9.5% gold price increase. This is the operational leverage: a 9.5% gold price move creates a 22% profit move, which typically translates into a significant stock price appreciation.

The leverage factor: (change in margin / change in gold price) = $200 / $200 = 1.0 for this example. But at lower gold prices near the cost of production, the operational leverage is much higher.
