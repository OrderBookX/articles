## Why Platinum Lost a Decade

The 2011-2021 platinum underperformance had three specific causes that fed on each other.

Cause one: the diesel engine scandal. Volkswagen's 2015 admission of emissions test cheating (the Dieselgate scandal) destroyed investor and consumer confidence in diesel vehicles across Europe. Since platinum's largest automotive application was diesel catalytic converters, the structural demand pillar that had supported platinum since the 1990s was suddenly under question. European diesel vehicle sales fell continuously from 2015 through 2021.

Cause two: the South African production ceiling. Despite constrained global supply (South Africa producing 70-75% of global platinum), the mining industry's high production costs, Eskom power disruptions, and labor disputes prevented the supply reduction that should have supported prices as demand weakened. South African platinum mining kept producing even at near-breakeven or loss-making prices because of their high fixed cost structures.

Cause three: investor disengagement. As platinum consistently underperformed gold from 2011 onward, investment demand (ETF holdings, futures positioning) fell steadily. By 2019, platinum ETF holdings were at multi-year lows. The lack of investment demand kept a ceiling on prices even as industrial fundamentals occasionally improved.
