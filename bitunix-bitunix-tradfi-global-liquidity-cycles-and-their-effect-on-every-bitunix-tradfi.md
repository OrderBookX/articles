## The Global M2 Liquidity Measure and Its Commodity Impact

Global M2 (the sum of major central banks' broad money supply measures) is the most widely used proxy for global liquidity. Practitioners typically track: US M2, Eurozone M2, China M2 (expressed in USD terms), and Japan M2 (in USD terms). The combined change in these four measures represents the direction of global liquidity.

Historical analysis: when global M2 growth is positive and accelerating, virtually all risk assets rise simultaneously   including gold, copper, oil, Bitcoin, and high-growth equities. When global M2 growth is negative or decelerating, risk assets fall together. The correlation of global M2 growth with cross-asset performance is higher than any single country's M2 measure, reflecting the interconnected nature of modern financial markets.
