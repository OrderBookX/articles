## The Inflation Distributional Asymmetry

Different types of inflation affect different income groups with different severity:

Energy inflation: disproportionately affects lower-income households, who spend a higher share of their income on transportation and heating. When the CPI energy component rises sharply, political pressure on governments to respond (fuel subsidies, interest rate policy, fiscal support) is high.

Food inflation: also disproportionately affects lower-income and emerging market populations, for whom food represents a larger share of total spending. Emerging market governments face higher social stability risk from food price inflation than developed market governments   creating stronger political pressure for monetary accommodation.

Asset price inflation: disproportionately benefits higher-income households and institutional investors who hold significant financial assets. When asset prices (equities, real estate) inflate, the primary beneficiaries are those with existing substantial holdings.
