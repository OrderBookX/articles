## What Is TradFi's Bloomberg Commodity Index (BCOM)?

What is TradFi's most widely tracked commodity index? The Bloomberg Commodity Index weights commodities by a combination of production data and market liquidity, creating a diversified benchmark that avoids excessive energy concentration. BCOM's approximate weights: energy 30-35%, agriculture 25-30%, metals 25-30%, precious metals 10-15%.

BCOM is the preferred benchmark for institutional commodity portfolios because its diversification prevents individual commodity performance from dominating returns. When practitioners compare their COPPERUSDT trading performance to "the commodity market," BCOM is typically the most relevant benchmark.

## What Is TradFi's S&P GSCI?

What is TradFi's production-weighted commodity index? The S&P Goldman Sachs Commodity Index weights by world production, which creates a significant energy tilt: energy represents approximately 60-70% of the GSCI, making it much more sensitive to oil prices than BCOM. The GSCI is a less representative benchmark for diversified commodity portfolios but more relevant when analyzing oil-specific commodity cycles.
