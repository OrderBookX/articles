## Setting Rebalancing Triggers: Calendar vs Threshold Approaches

Two approaches to rebalancing trigger:

Calendar rebalancing: Rebalance on a fixed schedule, typically monthly or quarterly, regardless of how much allocations have drifted. This approach is simpler to implement and reduces the number of transactions annually.

Threshold rebalancing: Rebalance when any allocation drifts more than a defined percentage from target (for example, when XAUUSDT grows to represent more than 30% of the portfolio versus a 20% target). This approach responds to market conditions more responsively but can trigger many transactions during volatile periods.

## Accounting for Funding Costs in Rebalancing Decisions

Perpetual contract positions incur ongoing funding rate costs that affect the economics of rebalancing. Before rebalancing a position that has appreciated and therefore has a large nominal size, calculate the funding rate cost of the current position size versus the rebalanced size. If funding rates are currently unfavorable for the appreciated position, rebalancing (reducing the position) saves on future funding costs in addition to locking in gains.

## Rebalancing Across Crypto and TradFi Simultaneously

For a combined DeFi crypto and TradFi portfolio, rebalancing should be considered holistically: when crypto appreciates and TradFi lags, rebalancing reduces crypto and increases TradFi. When TradFi appreciates and crypto lags, rebalancing reduces TradFi and increases crypto.

This holistic rebalancing ensures that no single category grows so dominant that portfolio performance becomes highly dependent on continued outperformance from one asset class.
