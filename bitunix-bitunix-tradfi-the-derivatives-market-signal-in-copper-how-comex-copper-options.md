## Put-Call Skew: The Market's Fear Asymmetry

Options market put-call skew measures whether the market is paying more for downside protection (puts) or upside exposure (calls) relative to the options pricing model's theoretical value. When copper puts are more expensive than equivalent calls (negative skew), the market is paying a premium for downside protection   implying more concern about downside risk than upside risk.

When copper calls are more expensive than puts (positive skew), the market is paying a premium for upside exposure   typically when large industrial consumers (miners, fabricators) are buying call options to hedge against input cost spikes.

The COPPERUSDT signal: when the copper options market shifts from negative to positive skew (calls becoming more expensive relative to puts), it signals a market-wide assessment shift from "I need to protect against falling prices" to "I need to protect against rising prices." This shift in hedging demand often precedes actual physical market tightening by 2-4 weeks.

## Options Open Interest Clustering

Large concentrations of open interest in copper options at specific strike prices create predictable price dynamics around expiration. When a large number of copper call options are clustered at a round-number strike (for example, $4.50/lb or $4.00/lb), the market makers who sold those calls must hedge by owning copper futures proportional to the options' delta. As the price approaches the strike, the delta increases   requiring more futures hedging and pulling the price toward the strike (the "gamma trap").

## The Implied Volatility Signal

When copper implied volatility (the options market's pricing of expected future price variance) rises sharply without a corresponding spot price move, the options market is anticipating increased volatility. High implied volatility is often associated with an upcoming scheduled event (FOMC meeting, China NPC session, major OPEC meeting) that the options market believes will create a large copper move.

When copper implied volatility is above its 3-month average and the options skew is shifting positive (calls expensive), the combined signal indicates: volatility expected to rise, market is positioned for upside.
