## NASA FIRMS: Oil Flaring as a Crude Oil Production Proxy

NASA's Fire Information for Resource Management System (FIRMS, firms.modaps.eosdis.nasa.gov) provides free access to satellite-detected fire and thermal anomaly data globally. In oil-producing regions, thermal anomalies from gas flaring at oil production facilities indicate whether oil production operations are active and at what scale.

When thermal anomaly counts in major oil-producing regions (Permian Basin, North Sea, Saudi Arabia, Russia's Siberian fields) are above their seasonal average, production is running at elevated levels. When thermal anomaly counts drop significantly below seasonal averages in a major producing region, it signals production curtailments that may precede official production statistics by 2-4 weeks.

This free NASA data provides crude oil production intelligence that institutional traders pay substantial sums for through private satellite monitoring services.

## USGS Landsat for Mining Activity Intelligence

The US Geological Survey's Landsat satellite imagery (available free through earthexplorer.usgs.gov) provides historical and recent-vintage imagery of major mining sites globally. While interpreting mining activity from satellite imagery requires some familiarity with mine pit morphology and waste dump development, basic observations are accessible to any practitioner:

Is the open pit actively expanding (visible fresh excavation scars)? Are haul road networks showing fresh tire tracks versus overgrown paths? Is the processing plant area showing active equipment and material movement?
