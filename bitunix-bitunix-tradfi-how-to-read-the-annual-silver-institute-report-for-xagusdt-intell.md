## The World Silver Survey's Most Valuable Sections

Section 1   Silver Supply:
Mine production by country: track year-over-year changes in Peru, Mexico, China, Chile, and Russia (the top five). When two or more of the top five simultaneously show production declines, the supply side is tightening structurally.

Recycling volume: total silver recovery from photographic, jewelry, electronic, and silverware scrap. Rising recycling at current prices signals that price levels are sufficient to attract secondary supply   a potential resistance signal.

Net government sales: whether sovereign inventory sales are adding to or subtracting from supply. Most government silver inventories have been depleted; any remaining sales are minor.

Section 2   Silver Industrial Demand:
Electrical and electronics subcategory: the largest industrial demand component. Track year-over-year growth rate and compare to global PMI trajectory. Is electrical demand growing faster or slower than global manufacturing activity implies?

Solar photovoltaic demand: now tracked separately by the Silver Institute. Compare actual solar demand to the theoretical calculation (total GW installed × loading per GW). Is actual solar demand meeting, exceeding, or falling short of expectations?

Section 3   Investment Demand:
Physical bar and coin demand: measures retail investment demand globally. When this figure is rising year-over-year despite stable or rising silver prices, retail investors are net accumulating.

ETF net investment: aggregate inflows or outflows from silver ETFs globally (iShares Silver Trust, Aberdeen Physical Silver, Invesco DB Silver). Net ETF outflows while physical bar and coin demand is rising suggests a retail-to-institutional demand shift that often precedes a silver price consolidation or correction.
