## The 2008-2011 Bull: The Legitimate Foundation

The gold bull market from the 2008 low of $700 to the 2011 high of $1,921 was built on legitimate fundamental drivers. The Federal Reserve's quantitative easing programs (QE1, QE2, and Operation Twist) were expanding the monetary base at unprecedented rates. Real interest rates were deeply negative. The European sovereign debt crisis (Greece, Ireland, Portugal) created ongoing safe-haven demand. Central banks that had been net sellers of gold for decades became net buyers.
