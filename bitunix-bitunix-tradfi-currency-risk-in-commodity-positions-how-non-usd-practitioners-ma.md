## The Dual Exposure Problem

An Indonesian practitioner who enters a XAUUSDT long position is simultaneously making two bets: (1) that gold prices in USD will rise, and (2) implicitly, that the USD will maintain or appreciate against IDR. If gold rises 5% in USD terms but the USD weakens 3% against IDR during the holding period, the real local-currency gain is approximately 2%   not 5%.

This dual exposure has historically been most significant for practitioners in currencies that are closely correlated with commodity prices themselves.
