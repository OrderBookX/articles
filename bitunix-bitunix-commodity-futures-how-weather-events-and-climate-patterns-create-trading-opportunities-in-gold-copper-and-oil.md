## Cold Snaps and Energy Demand in Oil Futures

## La Nina vs El Nino: The Multi-Year Weather Cycle for Commodities

La Nina (cooler Pacific Ocean) patterns tend to create drier conditions in South America (affecting copper and agricultural commodity production) while bringing more active Atlantic hurricane seasons (affecting US oil production). El Nino (warmer Pacific) creates the inverse.

Monitoring the NOAA ENSO (El Nino-Southern Oscillation) forecast, which projects conditions 6-12 months ahead, provides a multi-month advance warning of these weather-related commodity supply impacts.
