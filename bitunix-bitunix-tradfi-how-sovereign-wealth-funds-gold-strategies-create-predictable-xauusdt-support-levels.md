## Why Sovereign Wealth Funds Have Increased Gold Allocations

SWFs historically held minimal gold because their primary mandate was to generate investment returns from the national savings they managed, and gold generates no yield. But several developments changed this calculus: the 2022 freezing of Russian sovereign assets demonstrated that dollar and euro denominated holdings carry geopolitical seizure risk. Environmental mandates have pushed some SWFs away from fossil fuel investments toward real assets including gold. Low global yields in the 2010s reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold.

## Central Bank vs SWF Buying: The Distinction Matters

Central banks buy gold for reserve diversification and monetary sovereignty reasons; SWFs buy gold for investment return and portfolio diversification reasons. The distinction matters because SWF buying is more price-sensitive (they will reduce buying if prices rise too fast) while central bank buying is more strategic and less price-sensitive. Central bank support is therefore more reliable as a floor, while SWF buying is more conditional.
