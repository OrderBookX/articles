## Conservative Risk Profile

For investors who prioritize capital preservation with modest growth exposure, a conservative DCA plan focuses almost entirely on Bitcoin. An allocation of 90% BTC and 10% ETH captures broad crypto exposure while minimizing altcoin risk. The cycle amount should be small relative to total savings, perhaps 1% to 3% of monthly income. This profile accepts slower accumulation in exchange for lower volatility and higher confidence in asset survival across market cycles.

## Moderate Risk Profile

Moderate investors are comfortable with meaningful crypto exposure and can tolerate 40% to 50% drawdowns without financial distress. A typical moderate plan allocates 60% BTC, 25% ETH, and 15% to one or two carefully selected altcoins. The cycle amount might represent 5% to 8% of monthly income. This profile balances diversification benefits with the risk management advantage of concentrated core positions.

## Aggressive Risk Profile

Aggressive investors have long time horizons, high income relative to expenses, and emotional resilience to watch significant portfolio declines without panicking. An aggressive plan might allocate 40% BTC, 25% ETH, and 35% across multiple altcoins including Layer 1 alternatives and DeFi tokens. The cycle amount could reach 10% to 15% of monthly income. This profile maximizes potential upside but requires strong conviction and genuine ability to sustain the plan through severe drawdowns.

## Matching Profile to Reality

Most people overestimate their risk tolerance. They think they can handle a 50% drawdown until it actually happens. Before choosing your profile, honestly assess how you would react if your crypto portfolio lost half its value over three months. If the honest answer is that you would panic and sell, choose the conservative profile regardless of how aggressive you want to be. The best plan is the one you will actually follow, not the one that maximizes theoretical returns.

## Evolving Over Time

Risk tolerance is not static. As your income grows, your portfolio builds, and your understanding of crypto deepens, you may naturally shift from conservative to moderate. This evolution should happen gradually and based on demonstrated emotional resilience, not on bull market confidence. Adjust your auto invest allocations slowly and only after you have proven to yourself that you can maintain discipline through a significant downturn.
