## Cross Margin: How It Works

In cross margin mode, all open positions share the same margin pool. A profitable position on XAUUSDT contributes its unrealized profit to the margin available for a losing COPPERUSDT position, reducing the probability of liquidation on either individual position.

Cross margin advantage: Larger overall margin buffer means individual positions are less likely to be liquidated during temporary adverse moves. A $5,000 USDT account in cross margin mode with three positions has the full $5,000 plus unrealized profits from winning positions available to absorb adverse moves in any single position.

Cross margin risk: Maximum potential loss is larger. If multiple correlated positions move against you simultaneously, the cross margin pool depletes rapidly and all positions can be liquidated together. During the initial indiscriminate sell-off at the onset of financial crises, cross-margined TradFi portfolios can face simultaneous drawdowns across multiple instruments.

## Isolated Margin: How It Works

In isolated margin mode, each position has its own separate margin allocation. A losing COPPERUSDT position can only consume the specific margin allocated to it, not the margin from your profitable XAUUSDT position. Maximum loss on any single position is capped at the isolated margin allocated to it.

Isolated margin advantage: Maximum loss per position is defined and limited at entry. You cannot lose more than the isolated margin amount on any single bitunix tradfi trading position regardless of price movement against you.

Isolated margin risk: Positions liquidate more easily during temporary adverse moves because they do not benefit from the buffer of profitable position unrealized gains.

Use cross margin for core portfolio positions that you intend to hold for extended periods, where you want maximum survivability through temporary adverse moves. Use isolated margin for tactical event-driven positions where you want to limit the maximum possible impact on your overall account.
