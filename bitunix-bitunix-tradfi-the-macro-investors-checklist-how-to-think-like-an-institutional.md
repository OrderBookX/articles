## The Eight Elements of Institutional Process

Element 1   Thesis documentation before positions: every trade idea is written down before execution, including the primary driver, the expected move, the invalidation condition, and the holding period. No undocumented entries.

Element 2   Independent risk review: institutional funds have a separate risk management function that reviews every proposed position against the risk budget and concentration limits. As a solo practitioner, this means consciously wearing the "risk manager hat" before every entry   asking: does this position fit within the risk budget? Does it create hidden concentration risk?

Element 3   Pre-mortem analysis: before entering a position, explicitly consider the scenarios under which the position loses money. Not to talk yourself out of the trade, but to ensure the loss scenarios are known and sized for. "If this trade loses 5%, it is because [specific fundamental change] occurred   and I will exit at that point."

Element 4   Separation of alpha generation from risk management: institutional managers separate the portfolio manager (who generates the trade ideas and analytical framework) from the risk manager (who enforces position sizing and concentration limits). The conflict between the analytical excitement of a good idea and the discipline of risk management is resolved structurally by having two separate functions.
