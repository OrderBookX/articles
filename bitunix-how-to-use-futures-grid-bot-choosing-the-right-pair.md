# How to Use Futures Grid Bot — Choosing the Right Pair

Pair selection is one of the most overlooked steps in grid bot setup. Most beginners pick whatever coin they're excited about and wonder why the bot underperforms. The truth is that not every trading pair suits a grid strategy, and choosing well gives you an advantage before the bot even places its first order.

## Four Characteristics of an Ideal Grid Pair

The ideal pair for a futures grid bot has four characteristics: high liquidity, consistent 24-hour volume, moderate and regular volatility, and tight bid-ask spreads. Each of these directly affects grid performance.

High liquidity means your orders fill at the intended price without significant slippage. Grid bots place many orders across multiple levels — if each fill deviates even slightly from the target price, the cumulative impact over hundreds of trades erodes your theoretical profit margin.

### Consistent Volume

Consistent volume matters because grid bots operate around the clock. If a pair has strong volume during Asian hours but drops to almost nothing during European hours, your bot sits idle for significant portions of the day. BTC/USDT and ETH/USDT maintain volume across all time zones, making them reliable grid candidates.

### Moderate Volatility

Moderate volatility is the Goldilocks zone. Too little volatility means price barely moves, and the bot completes few cycles. Too much volatility means price exits your range quickly, triggering the pause mechanism. What you want is regular oscillation — consistent back-and-forth movement within a predictable band.

### Tight Spreads

Tight spreads keep your execution costs low. Wide spreads mean each grid entry starts at a slight disadvantage, requiring more price movement just to break even on that grid level. On major pairs, spreads are typically a fraction of a basis point. On lower-tier altcoins, spreads can be several basis points wide.
