## The Paper-to-Physical Ratio and Its Implications

At any given time, the total notional value of open gold futures contracts (COMEX) and OTC gold derivatives outstanding represents ownership claims to far more gold than physically exists in COMEX vaults. The coverage ratio (physical gold held in COMEX vaults divided by total deliverable gold claims) has historically ranged from approximately 1:20 to 1:100.

This ratio does not cause immediate problems because 99%+ of COMEX gold futures positions are cash-settled or rolled rather than delivered. But in extreme scenarios   when multiple large holders simultaneously demand physical delivery rather than cash settlement   the paper-to-physical mismatch creates delivery stress.

The March 2020 COVID period saw COMEX gold briefly at an extraordinary $50-70 premium to LBMA London spot prices (normally within $0.50). The cause: futures holders demanded physical delivery simultaneously, and the pipeline of physical gold from London to New York was disrupted by COVID logistics. The paper-to-physical system showed its structural limit.

COMEX gold vault holdings (total registered and eligible gold) are published daily on the CME Group website. The deliverable cover ratio can be calculated as: COMEX registered gold (deliverable) divided by total COMEX open interest in the nearby contract, converted to equivalent ounces. When this ratio falls below 5:1 (meaning each deliverable ounce is claimed by 5 or more futures positions), the delivery stress risk is elevated.
