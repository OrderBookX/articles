## The Four Early Warning Signals of Regime Shift

Signal 1   Cross-Asset Correlation Breakdown: When instruments that have been highly correlated for 6+ months begin diverging significantly for 3+ consecutive weeks, a regime shift may be underway. Example: gold and TIPS yields had 0.85 correlation for 6 months. Then gold stops responding to TIPS yield moves for three consecutive weeks. Something has changed   possibly a geopolitical safe-haven premium or dedollarization flow that is overriding the monetary driver.

Signal 2   Leading Economic Indicators Inflection: The Conference Board's US Leading Economic Index (LEI, free at conference-board.org, monthly) has historically turned 6-9 months before recessions and economic recoveries. When the LEI reverses direction for three consecutive months (falls for three months after a sustained rise, or rises for three months after a sustained fall), a macro regime transition is typically 6-9 months away.

Signal 3   Yield Curve Shape Change: The US Treasury yield curve's slope (10-year minus 2-year yield, FRED series T10Y2Y) transitions between normal upward slope (economic growth expected) and inverted (recession expected). When the yield curve transitions from deeply inverted to normalizing (the inversion is shrinking), economic conditions are shifting   typically preceding the shift from recession risk to recovery within 6-12 months.

Signal 4   Central Bank Policy Language Shift: When the Fed, ECB, and BOJ simultaneously shift policy language from one direction to another (from "further firming may be appropriate" to "prepared to adjust policy as appropriate" to "the risks are balanced"), the shift in language precedes the actual policy change by 6-12 months. Monitoring these language shifts through the FRED speech database and official policy statement archives detects the early stages of monetary regime transition.
