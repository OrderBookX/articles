## The Unallocated Gold System

Most institutional gold ownership is held as "unallocated" positions: the institution holds a claim on a specific quantity of gold from a bullion bank's general pool rather than owning specific identified bars in a specific vault. This system allows bullion banks to lend the same gold to multiple counterparties simultaneously, creating the gold market's version of fractional reserve banking.

When gold lease rates are very low or negative, bullion banks lend out gold cheaply to producers who sell it forward, to jewelers who need working capital, and to investment banks who use it to create gold-backed financial products. The resulting supply of lent gold exceeds the physical supply available from mines alone, artificially suppressing prices during these periods.

This lease rate spike signals gold market tightening that often precedes XAUUSDT appreciation as the borrowed-and-sold supply is gradually absorbed.
