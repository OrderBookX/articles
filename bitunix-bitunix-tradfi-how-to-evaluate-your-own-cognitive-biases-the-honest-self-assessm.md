## The Personal Bias Assessment Protocol

Step 1   Review the last 20 completed trades from the position log. For each trade, identify which of the five primary biases, if any, influenced the decision:

Loss aversion: did you hold this position past the written invalidation condition because you didn't want to realize the loss?
Overconfidence: did you enter this position at a size larger than your conviction score justified because you "felt" more confident than the evidence supported?
Anchoring: did you maintain a bullish thesis on this instrument because the entry price (or a recent high) anchored your expectation of where the price "should be"?
Disposition effect: did you exit a profitable position earlier than the thesis target because the gains felt fragile, while holding a losing position past the invalidation point?
Conviction drift: did you add to a position that was moving in your favor even though the analytical evidence count had not improved   justified by price momentum alone?

Step 2   Calculate the percentage of trades affected by each bias. If loss aversion appeared in 40% of trades, it is your dominant bias requiring the most structural attention. If overconfidence appeared in 15%, it is secondary.

Step 3   Design the specific structural defense for your dominant bias. If loss aversion is dominant: add a mandatory "24-hour rule"   any trade that reaches its invalidation condition must be reviewed and exited within 24 hours, with a written justification required for any delay beyond 24 hours.
