## The TC/RC Market Structure

Copper mines produce concentrate   a slurry containing approximately 25-30% copper mixed with sulfur, iron, and other minerals. Smelters process this concentrate into refined copper cathode through smelting and electrolytic refining. The smelter charges the mine a treatment charge (TC, measured in dollars per dry metric tonne of concentrate) and a refining charge (RC, measured in cents per pound of refined copper).

When TC/RCs are high (smelters charging miners more): smelting capacity exceeds mine supply. Smelters have pricing power because they have spare capacity competing for limited concentrate. High TC/RCs signal an abundance of smelting capacity relative to mine output   supply of refined copper is constrained by mine production, not by smelting bottlenecks. Historically bullish for COPPERUSDT as mine supply constraints create physical tightness.

When TC/RCs are low (smelters accepting less per tonne): mine supply is abundant relative to smelting capacity. Mines have pricing power because multiple smelters are competing for their concentrate. Low TC/RCs signal physical copper concentrate abundance   potentially more supply coming to market as smelters process the available concentrate at low margins.
